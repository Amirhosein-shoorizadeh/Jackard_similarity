Law on the Sixth Five-Year Plan for Economic, Social and Cultural Development of the Islamic Republic of Iran (1400-1396)
Article 1 - Definitions and abbreviations
Sixth Plan: Sixth Five-Year Plan for Economic, Social and Cultural Development of the Islamic Republic of Iran (1400-1396)
Executive agencies: Executive agencies subject to Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law, in compliance with Article (117) of the amended law approved in 2007 and Article (5) of the country's Public Accounts Law.
Veterans: Veterans are subject to the Comprehensive Law on Providing Services to Veterans
Foundation: Martyr and Veterans Affairs Foundation
Organization: National Planning and Budget Organization
Central Bank: Central Bank of the Islamic Republic of Iran
Operational unit: Those organizational units of executive agencies that are responsible for producing, supplying, and providing the main and final products and services of the executive agency.
Article 2- The following issues are the central issues of the program. The government is obliged to implement the plans (projects) related to them, as well as the approvals of the Resistance Economy Command Headquarters, in the annual budget only in the areas mentioned below.
A. Specific strategic issues on water and the environment
B - Specific location-based issues regarding the development of the Makran and Arvand coasts and the regeneration of inefficient urban textures (urban outskirts and worn-out textures), historical textures, and rural areas.
C- Specific issues of the economic development sector regarding mining and mineral industries, agriculture, tourism, transit and rail transport, new technology, development and application of science and technology, and energy.
D- Specific cross-sectoral macro-issues regarding improving the business environment, employment, cyberspace, efficiency in providing financial resources for the country's economy, a fair payment system and eliminating discrimination, empowering the deprived and poor (with priority given to women heads of households), social insurance and organizing and sustaining insurance and pension funds, preventing and reducing social harms, and implementing the document on the fundamental transformation of education, public culture, and the Iranian-Islamic lifestyle.
E- Developing defense and security readiness
Section 1 - Macroeconomics
Article 3- In order to achieve an average annual economic growth of eight percent (8%) and a Gini coefficient of (0.34) in the final year of the program, quantitative macro-economic targets and economic sectors are determined separately in Tables (1), (2) and (3):
Article 4- In order to ensure at least two and eight tenths (2.8) percentage points of the eight percent (8%) economic growth from improving the productivity of total factors of production and also investment growth at an average annual rate of twenty-one and fourteen percent (21.4%) during the years of implementation of the program, all executive agencies shall take the following measures in coordination with the government. The government shall be responsible for implementation:
A- Necessary orientations and policies to equip the financial resources required for investment, including securing foreign financial resources up to an annual average of thirty billion dollars from credit lines from foreign banks in the form of autonomous foreign financing with priority given to Islamic financing, fifteen billion (15,000,000,000) dollars in the form of foreign direct investment, and twenty billion (20,000,000,000) dollars in foreign partnership agreements.
Note - The use of foreign financial facilities by executive agencies with priority given to Islamic financing during the implementation of the Sixth Plan Law is permitted within the framework of annual budget laws.
B- Respecting and protecting property rights and wealth production, and a value-based view of work and wealth creation through legal and legitimate means, and non-interference in the decision-making of economic actors, and developing a new enterprise system in the public sector, along with a ban on new investments for the public sector in activities that the private sector can perform in healthy competition at lower cost and greater efficiency, so that at the end of the first year of the law's implementation, the government approves and announces a plan of measures necessary to implement this ruling.
C- Economic priority in the country's foreign policy with the aim of attracting knowledge and innovation from countries with technology and developing export markets (commodity and domestic) for Iranian technical and engineering services and goods, sending labor, attracting professors and specialists to train and transfer technology to Iranian forces, and trying to join the World Trade Organization to prevent unfair discrimination against Iranian exports while respecting the country's interests.
D- Increasing the skills and expertise of the workforce, especially graduates of high schools, art schools up to the bachelor's level of universities and higher education institutions, by providing skilled, specialized, technical and professional training using the capacities of serving under the flag and internships of students.
The government is required to submit a report on the implementation of this clause to the Planning and Budget, Accounting, and Education, Research, and Technology Commissions of the Islamic Consultative Assembly in the first quarter of each year.
E- Attracting reputable global and regional companies into the domestic production chain directly and with priority to partner with domestic investors and producers in order to increase capability, competitiveness, and export of manufactured goods and services by removing legal and legal obstacles based on dignity, wisdom, and expediency, and subject to the absence of Western culture influence.
C- Necessary legal, financial and institutional support for the development of knowledge and technological and innovative progress towards the commercialization of ideas and knowledge in the production of products and services with positive added value within the framework of paragraph (80) of the general policies of the Sixth Plan.
G- Developing a necessary and efficient mechanism to reduce the gap between the interest rates on bank facilities and deposits, with the aim of increasing the efficiency and competitiveness of the banking system in attracting and mobilizing resources and granting facilities in such a way that in each year of the program, the gap between the interest rates on bank facilities and deposits is reduced by at least ten percent (10%) compared to the previous year.
H- Development of the capital market with emphasis on the design and engineering of financial and commodity instruments, development of the energy market and supply of crude oil and petroleum products on the energy exchange, institutional reform of corporate governance for public companies and financial institutions.
X- Necessary orientation and policies to promote information transparency in the capital market and launch rating agencies, subject to paragraph (21) of Article (1) of the Securities Market Law approved on 1/9/2005, to attract foreign investment and secure international financing.
D- Allowing the presence and participation of foreign financial and credit institutions in Iran within the framework of the Law on the Implementation of General Policies, Article 44 (44) of the Constitution, approved on 25/03/2008, and the Law on the Encouragement and Support of Foreign Investment, approved on 4/03/2002.
Z - Reforming foreign exchange, trade and tariff policies by the end of the second year of implementation of the program law at the latest, with the approach of improving the quality and making domestically produced goods competitive for export.
R- The government is authorized to provide the necessary guarantees through the operating bank or development organizations for external financing of economic projects of the non-governmental sector whose technical and economic justification has been approved by the Economic Council.
G- In order to achieve economic growth and development based on justice, the government is required to implement policies for job creation, skill development, and promotion of professional knowledge, and to support small home-based and knowledge-based businesses based on the National Decent Work Document, which will be approved by the Cabinet by the end of the first year of implementation of the Program Law at the latest upon the proposal of the Ministry of Cooperatives, Labor and Social Welfare, the Chamber of Cooperatives, and the Organization. The said document must be based on reducing the unemployment rate by at least one-eightieth of a percent (0.8%) annually during the years of implementation of the Program Law.

A- In order to focus on productivity growth in the economy, the executive agencies and the armed forces are required to implement the productivity management cycle in their own system, and to prepare the necessary arrangements to operationalize this cycle in the units under their supervision in coordination with the National Productivity Organization of Iran, and to submit its annual report to the National Productivity Organization of Iran. The executive agencies are required to submit their operational plans for promoting productivity by facilitating and encouraging non-governmental activities in the relevant areas to the National Productivity Organization for approval within the first six months of the implementation of the program law, and this organization is also required to submit the aforementioned set of measures to the Council of Ministers for approval within a maximum of one year.

B- In order to improve the productivity index, the government is required to measure the efficiency and productivity of executive agencies and operational units in each year of the implementation of the law, and to submit an action plan and report to the Islamic Consultative Assembly.
Section 2 - Budget and Public Finance
Article 6 - In order to achieve savings in public spending, reform the government's revenue system, and also end the budget's dependence on oil until the end of the implementation of the Sixth Plan Law:
A- The establishment of any new tax reduction, preference or exemption is prohibited during the years of implementation of the program law.
B- The duties referred to in Article (38) of the Value Added Tax Law approved on 17/02/2008 and its clauses, as well as the pollution duties referred to in Note (1) of the aforementioned article, the value added taxes on natural gas, and the vehicle registration duties, shall be distributed by the Iranian Tax Affairs Organization as follows in the case of clauses (a), (b), and (c) of the aforementioned article:
1- The collection fees of paragraph (a) of Article (38) of the Value Added Tax Law, in compliance with the legal arrangements and after deducting the funds stipulated in the aforementioned law and depositing them into the provincial funds concentration account with the General Treasury of the country through a liaison account opened by the General Treasury upon the request of the National Tax Affairs Organization, shall be deposited into the accounts of the municipalities and rural districts in the proportion of seventy percent (70%) for cities and thirty percent (30%) for villages and nomadic areas, and based on the population index. The share of villages without rural districts and nomadic areas shall be deposited into the accounts of the relevant city governorates to be spent in the same villages and nomadic areas with the participation of the Islamic Revolution Housing Foundation.
Note - The collection fees subject to this section do not require allocation procedures and will be deposited into the accounts of municipalities and villages with one hundred percent (100%) allocation by the fifteenth of the following month at the latest.
2- The taxes subject to paragraphs (b), (c) and (d) of Article (38) of the Value Added Tax Law, as well as the natural gas value added taxes subject to the aforementioned law and the vehicle numbering taxes subject to paragraph (c) of Article (43) of the aforementioned law shall be deposited into the funds concentration account in the name of the National Tax Affairs Organization with the General Treasury of the country. The said funds shall be calculated and distributed among all municipalities, villages and nomadic areas by the fifteenth of the following month at the rate of twelve percent (12%) for metropolitan cities, fifty-three percent (53%) for other cities and thirty-five percent (35%) for villages and nomadic areas based on indicators that are calculated and distributed among all municipalities, villages and nomadic areas in accordance with the instructions issued by the Organization and the Ministry of Interior. The share of villages without villages and nomadic areas shall be deposited into the account of the relevant county governor to be spent on the development and development of those villages and nomadic areas in accordance with the regulations and approvals of the county planning committees. Also, thirty percent (30%) of the value added tax subject to this component received from production units located in towns and industrial areas of the counties to provide infrastructure and provide services in them and complete the unfinished plans (projects) of towns and industrial areas of the province, will be made available to the Provincial Industrial Towns Company. This share will be deducted proportionally from the amount related to the cities of the province.
Note - Five percent (5%) of the amount subject to the thirty percent (30%) share of the provincial towns shall be made available to service companies subject to the Law on the Procedure for Transfer, Ownership, and Administration of Industrial Towns, approved on 2/31/2008. Industrial town companies and non-governmental industrial zones are also subject to this ruling.
During the Sixth Schedule, Subsection (2) of Clause (b) of this Article shall prevail in the section on the inconsistency with Notes (1) and (2) of Article (39) of the Value Added Tax Law.
3- Pollution charges for production units subject to Note (1) of Article (38) of the Value Added Tax Law shall be distributed in each county in proportion to the population among municipalities, rural districts, and governorates (for villages without rural districts and nomadic areas) of the same county. If pollution from large production units (fifty people or more) spreads to more than one county in a province, pollution charges shall be distributed among the affected counties in proportion to their impact, based on the policies announced by the organization, in a committee composed of the head of the provincial organization and the governors of the relevant counties, the Director General of Environment, and the Director General of Tax Affairs of the province.
Note - If the cities affected by pollution are located in two or more provinces, the members of the pollution toll distribution committee of large units (fifty people or more) consisting of a representative of the organization, heads of the relevant provincial organizations, a representative of the Environmental Protection Organization, and a representative of the General Tax Affairs Departments of the relevant provinces will distribute pollution tolls based on the organization's announced policies.
Article 7 - In order to bring annual budgets into line with the Sixth Plan Law, financial discipline, reform the planning and budgeting process, and monitor government performance and expenditures:
A- Annual budget bills shall be prepared and submitted to the Islamic Consultative Assembly in compliance with this law, with adjustments made in accordance with economic, social, and cultural developments and indicators, and with compliance with the financial rules set forth in Table (4) of this law.
b)
1- The share of the National Development Fund from the resources obtained from the export of oil, gas condensates and net gas exports shall be determined at thirty percent (30%) in the first year of the implementation of the program law, and at least two percentage points shall be added to this share annually. The Central Bank shall be obliged to immediately deposit these funds and the share of fourteen and a half percent (14.5%) of the National Iranian Oil Company from the total export of oil and gas condensates, as well as the share of three percent (3%) of oil-bearing, gas-bearing and undeveloped areas, throughout the year and in proportion to the collection of resources, and from the remaining amount, the share of the general budget of the government from the resources subject to this part shall be determined within the ceiling of Table No. (4) of this law and deposited in the general revenue account with the Treasury of the whole country, and the rest shall be deposited in the foreign exchange reserve account.
2- The ceiling of the government's general budget spending credits in the years of implementation of the program law shall be determined according to the figures in Table No. (4) of this law. The transfer and increase of resources and expenditures in this table shall be permitted up to a maximum of fifteen percent (15%) in the form of annual budget bills.
C- The government is required to adjust the annual budgets of twenty percent (20%) of the executive agencies listed in the annual budget laws, starting from the first year of implementation of the program law, in the form of performance-based budgeting, so that in the final year of implementation of the program law, one hundred percent (100%) of the agencies have a performance-based budget. The provisions of this paragraph do not apply to public schools.
D- It is prohibited to create and impose any financial burden in excess of the figures stated in the annual budget laws by executive agencies, including government agencies, in various cases such as guaranteed purchases and costs associated with purchases, compensation for losses, price differences, market regulation, input subsidies and others, fulfillment of specific obligations, basic goods, export bonuses, and the like, where public funds are used. The responsibility for implementing this ruling lies with the highest official of the executive agency or authorized officials and relevant financial managers. Violation of this ruling is considered an obligation in excess of the funds, and the violator will be sentenced to the punishment referred to in Article (598) of Book Five of the Islamic Penal Code (Penalties and Deterrent Penalties) approved on 2/3/1375 with subsequent amendments and additions.
E- All approvals, circulars and instructions, changes in the organization, changes in coefficients, legal tables and job classifications and increasing the legal basis, granting permission for any type of recruitment and employment of personnel, as well as approvals of the boards of trustees that involve a financial burden, can be proposed, approved and implemented if the resulting financial burden has been previously calculated and provided for in the country's general budget law. Action by the executive branch contrary to this ruling is considered an excessive commitment to credit.
The government should plan in such a way that by the end of the last year of implementation of the program law, the budgets of social insurance organizations and funds will become independent and no credit will be paid to them from the general budget.
C- Regarding clauses (d) and (e) of this article, all executive agencies are required to submit their intended items to the organization in the form of annual budget proposals after the budget circular is announced, so that the required funds can be foreseen in the annual budget. In the event that the organization does not accept the proposal and does not foresee the funds, the actions of the authorities and executive agencies shall be deemed as an obligation in excess of the funds.
G- Expenses paid by natural or legal persons for the purpose of constructing, completing, and equipping spaces, venues, and developing public sports, or as assistance to the Ministry of Sports and Youth, are considered acceptable tax expenses upon approval by the said Ministry.
H- The government is required to allocate thirty percent (30%) of the country's capital asset acquisition credits to the Provincial Planning Council from the first year of implementation of the program law in order to operationalize the decentralization approach and the effectiveness of executive management in provincial centers and to prepare the groundwork, balance, and spatial planning.
Note - The distribution of funds subject to this article is in addition to the provinces' share of funds from national and provincial national plans.
The provisions contained in Article (7) of this law govern the provisions of Article (16) of the Law on Permanent Provisions of National Development Programs, especially Subsection (7), Clause (d) and Clause (h).
Article 8 - In order to achieve the objectives of paragraph (12) of the general policies of the resistance economy, which are based on fundamental changes in structures and reform and improvement of the government's financial and accounting management system, as well as debt management, the government is required to take the following measures in accordance with the relevant laws:
A- Organizing the treasury of the entire country through:
1- Reforming the financial and accounting system of the country's treasury.
2- Improving the oversight system by reforming the public sector financial and accounting system and public sector financial reporting.
B- Organizing the country's financial and accounting system through:
1- Providing a single and integrated accounting software with the ability to record financial events based on accrual accounting.
2- Preparing consolidated financial statements at the public sector level and providing the necessary software.
3- Developing a training package for the public sector accounting system for the entire country
C- Improving and adapting the financial monitoring system to the performance-based budgeting system through:
1- Designing a financial monitoring system appropriate to the country's conditions and financial monitoring of performance-based budgeting
2- Establish a redesigned financial oversight system based on performance-based budgeting.
D- Reorganizing government debts through:
1- Maintaining the ratio of government and state-owned companies' debt to gross domestic product (GDP) at a maximum level of forty percent (40%).
2- Organizing the principal of debts through extension and settlement of the relevant interest through inclusion in the general budget.
3- Gradual elimination of treasury bills and the use of debt securities with the approval of the Islamic Consultative Assembly.
4- Repaying government debt to private individuals and legal entities and cooperatives through short-term markets and paying government debt to the banking system and public and non-governmental institutions through medium-term and long-term debt instruments.
Note - Increasing the debt ratio index to forty-five percent (45%) is possible with the determination of the Minister of Economic Affairs and Finance and the approval of the Council of Ministers. In emergency situations, the government can, with the approval of the Islamic Consultative Assembly, increase the debt ratio index for a limited period of time to fifty percent (50%) and, following the resolution of the emergency conditions within a maximum period of three years through debt settlement, increase the debt ratio index to forty percent (40%).
Article 9: The government is required to complete the government’s electronic procurement system program by the end of the second year of implementation of the law to implement all stages of medium and large transactions of ministries and agencies subject to the Tendering Law approved on 11/3/2004 and other public sector financial and transaction laws, except for confidential transactions, in compliance with the Electronic Commerce Law approved on 10/17/2003 and the Tendering Law approved on 11/3/2004. All agencies subject to the Tendering Law and non-governmental public institutions are required to comply with the Tendering Law and conduct their transactions through the aforementioned system.
Section 3 - Monetary and banking system and financial resources provision
Article 10: In order to expand and deepen the comprehensive financial system and its instruments (money market, capital market, and insurance), the government is authorized to:
A- It shall settle the equivalent of its total debts to natural and legal persons that have been or will be finalized by the end of 1395, in the order of priorities specified by the organization’s proposal, by the end of the implementation of the program law through the issuance of securities with the consent of creditors. The aforementioned securities shall be issued in compliance with the Securities Law of the Islamic Republic of Iran up to the ceiling stipulated in the annual budget laws and protected from inflation, in accordance with a regulation that is approved by the Council of Ministers upon the proposal of the organization and the Ministry of Economic Affairs and Finance.
Note - From the beginning of the implementation of the program law, debts that have been created by the government and have passed the audit process in reputable authorities and have been finalized and have not received any increase for non-payment, will receive interest equivalent to the inflation rate up to the maximum deposit interest rate announced by the Central Bank until the debt is paid by the government. A clause should be added to the relevant contracts, whereby the government is obligated to pay the creditor the equivalent of the aforementioned rate if it fails to pay its debt on time.
B- In order to secure the necessary funds for payments related to the aforementioned securities (including interest and principal), each year, it shall foresee a specific line item in the annual budget bill and submit it to the Islamic Consultative Assembly for approval.
Note - The securities issued are registered and are protected from inflation, and transactions in these securities are only permitted in organized securities markets under the supervision of the Securities and Exchange Organization and in compliance with the regulations of these markets, and their transactions outside the aforementioned markets are null and void. The offering and transactions of these securities at a discount (less than the nominal value) and with a floating interest rate are permitted, and the guarantee of redemption before the maturity of the aforementioned securities by banks, financial and credit institutions and persons under their control is not permitted. Banks, executive agencies and state-owned companies for whose financing the securities are issued must publish the information required by investors in accordance with the instructions approved by the Stock Exchange Council.
C- The Ministry of Economic Affairs and Finance is authorized to use government assets to issue Islamic Sukuk as provided for in this law, organize and manage assets and properties owned by the government, and centralize authorities related to the transfer of movable and immovable properties and other government assets, while implementing the Comprehensive Information System for Immovable Properties of Executive Agencies (SADA), as appropriate, to take the necessary measures to manage and use the assets and properties of executive agencies subject to Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law approved on July 8, 2007, with the exception of Anfal, properties of agencies under the supervision of the Supreme Leader, and cases and instances stated in Article 83 of the Constitution in order to support the issuance of Islamic Sukuk, such as evaluation by official experts, identification, determination and change of the beneficiary, assignment and any transfer or sale of the aforementioned properties with the approval of the Council of Ministers. All executive agencies subject to Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law, in implementing Article (137) of the National Public Accounting Law and Note (10) of Article (69) of the Law on the Regulation of Part of Government Financial Regulations, are required to cooperate and register all immovable property, including lands, properties, buildings and office spaces in their possession or occupation, whether they have a title deed or do not have a title deed, leased or endowed, or real estate, in the system within a maximum period of three months from the date of notification of this law. Issuing any license to transfer the right of use, change the user, or sell government immovable property without registering information in the aforementioned system and obtaining a tracking ID (code) is prohibited.
Issuance of Islamic Sukuk for agencies affiliated with the judiciary, the legislature, and other non-executive government institutions, and the use of the assets and properties of these agencies, as the case may be, is subject to the approval of their highest authority.
D- The Ministry of Economic Affairs and Finance is permitted, with the approval of the Council of Ministers and in compliance with the Law on the Implementation of General Policies, Article 44 (44) of the Constitution, and within the framework of the executive regulations of this paragraph, to use government shares in companies as collateral for the issuance of Islamic securities (Sukuk).
E- The Ministry of Economic Affairs and Finance is authorized to issue Islamic treasury bonds in order to eliminate the imbalance of resources and expenditures of the public budget during a fiscal year. The organization is required to foresee and approve the repayment of the principal and interest of the said bonds in full in the annual budget bills, as announced by the Ministry of Economic Affairs and Finance. The credits related to the repayment of these bonds are considered one hundred percent (100%) allocated.
Article 11- The Central Insurance of Iran is obliged to:
A- In order to increase the role of the insurance industry in the financial system and create a safe environment in other economic areas, including the business and investment environment, through insurance companies, to promote the economic role of insurance in ensuring the welfare and security of the people and to increase the insurance penetration rate in the country to its global average during the years of the plan, the necessary measures will be taken to achieve the following goals:
1- The penetration rate of commercial insurance should reach seven percent (7%) during the implementation of the program.
2- The share of life insurance in the country's commercial insurance premiums should be increased by at least fifty percent (50%) compared to the base year of the implementation of the program law.
3- Insurance sectors should increase and expand in the production and commercial sectors of the national economy.
B- The regulations and bylaws for investing insurance reserves should be reviewed by the Supreme Insurance Council in order to appropriately utilize the aforementioned resources to provide sustainable financing for the national economy.

This clause does not include agencies under the supervision of the Supreme Leader, the legislative and judicial branches, and non-governmental public institutions.

C- The Steel Workers' Pension and Support Fund Institution, with all its duties, powers, obligations and assets - including movable and immovable property, shares, privileges, inventory, documents and papers - shall be transferred to the Ministry of Cooperatives, Labor and Social Welfare, while maintaining its independent identity.
Within a maximum period of three years, all current and future obligations of the Fund - to the extent determined by actuarial studies to determine the current value of future obligations, which are approved by the National Audit Office within a maximum period of three months - will be paid with resources obtained from the transfer of shares, company shares, company properties, concessions, mining exploitation rights and government financial rights, and other resources as appropriate, through the Privatization Organization and the National Treasury. The amount of transfers will be fixed each year in the annual budgets.
Article 13: The Ministry of Economic Affairs and Finance is responsible for establishing and concluding bilateral and multilateral agreements, as well as bilateral monetary agreements, with trading partner and target countries, especially regional countries. In implementing this article, it is essential to observe the laws and general policies of the system based on dignity, wisdom, and expediency.
Article 14 - To exercise complete and comprehensive supervision by the Central Bank over monetary, banking, and credit institutions and to organize unorganized monetary institutions and markets in order to promote transparency and soundness and reduce the ratio of non-current claims to facilities:
A- The Central Bank is authorized, in accordance with the relevant laws and within the framework of the resolutions approved by the Monetary and Credit Council, in addition to its legal powers stipulated in the country's Monetary and Banking Law approved on 18/4/1351, to apply one or more of the following supervisory and disciplinary measures against banks and credit institutions that violate the law:
1- Imposing restrictions, prohibiting the distribution of profits and reserves among effective shareholders, temporarily removing their voting rights, and removing the right of preemption from effective shareholders.
2- Temporary suspension of a partial activity license for a certain period of time or cancellation of the activity license.
3- Restricting or prohibiting the payment of bonuses and benefits to managers
4- Disqualification of managing directors and board members
Note 1: The authority to investigate regulatory and disciplinary violations covered by this clause will be the Banks' Regulatory Board.
Note 2: An effective shareholder is a shareholder that elects one or more members of the Board of Directors or, at the discretion of the Central Bank, plays a role in electing a member of the Board of Directors.
B- Any banking, leasing, and exchange operations by natural and legal persons, and participation of banks, credit institutions, and subordinate legal persons in which banks hold more than fifty percent (50%) of their shares or are influential in determining their board of directors, in establishing investment funds and capital supply companies without obtaining a license from the Central Bank is prohibited, and the perpetrator will be sentenced to one or more of the sixth-degree ta'zir punishments of Article (19) of the Islamic Penal Code approved on 1/2/2013, in addition to imprisonment and flogging.
Note 1- The responsibility for repaying all obligations and debts of the aforementioned institutions lies with the Board of Trustees, Board of Directors, Founding Board, and effective shareholder in proportion to the extent of the liability (in proportion to the extent and circumstances of the loss).
Note 2 - In addition to the cases that are exempted in the country's monetary and banking law, a single-branch Qarz al-Hasanah Fund throughout the country based on annual resource attraction of up to three billion (30,000,000,000) rials is also exempt from the provisions of this paragraph and will continue to operate based on its statutes and existing licenses.
Clause (b) of Article (14) during the implementation of the program, in the section on the discrepancy, shall govern Note (4) of Article (1) of the Unorganized Monetary Market Regulation Law, approved on 10/22/2004, and shall be considered a special provision and shall govern Article (20) of the Islamic Penal Code, approved on 02/01/2013.
C- The law enforcement force is required to take action to suspend or close down branches or institutions in cases where the Central Bank directly declares them unlicensed.
D- Any advertising for the provision of monetary and banking services will be based on a regulation that will be prepared by the Central Bank within four months after the entry into force of this law and approved by the Council of Ministers. In addition to the regulatory measures that will be applied to the monetary, banking and credit institution, media outlets that violate this provision will be subject to a cash fine of up to ten times the cost of the advertisement, which will be deposited into the national treasury account.
E- The Central Bank is required, in cooperation with the judiciary and other relevant agencies, to plan in such a way that, by completing and developing the national credit assessment database and other executive and supervisory measures, the ratio of non-performing facilities to total facilities (currency and rial) is reduced by one percentage point annually.
C- Any lawsuit that originates from the Central Bank's supervisory actions must be filed with the bank in question, and individuals involved in the supervision cannot be made a party to the lawsuit, except in cases where the subject of the lawsuit is the attribution of a crime.
Note - Supervisory measures are measures taken in order to supervise banks, non-bank credit institutions, credit cooperatives, and Qard-ul-Hasanah funds, except for Qard-ul-Hasanah funds under the supervision of the Islamic Economic Organization and subject to Note (2) of Clause (b) of this Article, exchange offices, and leasing companies under the jurisdiction of the Central Bank of the Islamic Republic of Iran, and include measures taken at all stages of establishment, granting of licenses, supervision of activities, registration changes, reconstruction, merger, dissolution, and liquidation.

Section 5 - Regional Balance, Rural Development, and Empowerment of Vulnerable Groups
Article 26 - In order to make inter-regional and territorial justice competitive and strengthen self-reliance, increase the incentive to collect provincial revenue:
A- The government is obliged to:
1- During the first year of implementation of the law, prepare a national and provincial land planning document and, after approval by the Supreme Council for Land Planning, implement the plan from the second year of implementation of the law.
2- Determine the revenues and expenses that are of a provincial nature and apply them in the annual budget during the first two years of implementation of the program law within the framework of the provincial revenue-expenditure system as the sum of revenues, expenditure credits, and acquisition of capital assets of each province (based on funds deposited to the national treasury). The surplus of collected revenues over the approved ceiling of each province shall be distributed among the relevant provinces in quarterly intervals based on the instructions issued by the organization for securing expenditure credits and acquisition of capital assets approved in the annual budget law.
3- Three percent (3%) of the income from the export of crude oil and net natural gas condensates shall be allocated, one-third to oil- and gas-producing provinces and two-thirds to less developed regions and cities, respectively, for the implementation of development programs in the form of annual budgets approved by the Planning and Development Council of these provinces. The provision of sub-paragraph (3) of paragraph (a) of Article (26) of this law shall prevail over the provision of sub-paragraph (1) of paragraph (b) of Article (7). 5- Prepare rural master plans and determine the boundaries of villages throughout the country with the suggestions of technical experts, under the supervision of the Housing Foundation and the approval of the district administration of each district, and with the knowledge of the village heads and heads of the Islamic Councils of the villages, and their approval by a committee consisting of the head of the Islamic Revolution Housing Foundation of the province as the head, the governor of the county, the district governor, a representative of the provincial Housing and Urban Development Organization, the head of the Islamic Revolution Housing Foundation of the county, a representative of the provincial Agricultural Jihad Organization, a representative of the deputy governor for development affairs, and the head of the Islamic Council of the village as the supervisor.

A- Economic and sectoral measures:
1- In line with regional planning and strengthening the rural economy and developing an export-oriented economy during the implementation of the Program Law, the government is required to prepare an economic development and employment generation plan for five thousand villages in each year, taking into account the local and environmental talents and capacities and the local-economic capabilities of that region, with the participation of local forces and by utilizing banking facilities, government support and private sector investment, through the provincial organization, and submit it to the approval of the relevant provincial Development and Planning Council. The organization is required to announce the number of provincial quota villages by the first three months of the first year of the implementation of the Program Law.
2- Targeted and transparent arrangement and presentation of funds related to rural and nomadic development and construction in the annual budget, under an appendix entitled "Rural Development and Construction", and allocation of the development budget related to this chapter should be carried out.
3- Reducing the time it takes to respond to inquiries and issue construction permits for rural and nomadic economic enterprises from each of the relevant agencies to a maximum of fifteen days.
4- Developing at least fifty-four rural business clusters, operating and building ninety-eight rural industrial zones, and creating one million nine hundred and fourteen thousand job opportunities in villages and nomadic areas through the establishment and development of competitive and export-oriented economic enterprises through the private and cooperative sectors.
5- Providing the resources needed for an annual growth of at least five percent (5%) of the welfare indicators determined in the organization of the tribes.
6- Preparing master plans and development plans and their revision for all villages and settlements with more than twenty households and nomadic areas during the implementation of the program law, with priority given to less advantaged areas.
7- Training 100,000 villagers and nomads as leading and facilitating elements in the field of local planning, developing economic activities and cultural programs, improving service delivery, attracting public participation, and monitoring the effectiveness of executive agency plans.
8- Identifying villages at risk of natural disasters in order to implement plans to make these settlements safe, in cooperation with the responsible agencies and with the participation of the people and local institutions, so that at least thirty percent (30%) of the villages at risk are made safe by the end of the implementation of the law.
9- Determining indicators of worn-out and disorganized rural textures and preparing and implementing plans to improve worn-out textures in priority villages.
10- Waiver of interest and penalties on facilities paid to individuals covered by the Imam Khomeini Relief Committee (RA) and the Welfare Organization for the reconstruction of residential areas in rural areas damaged by natural and unexpected disasters who are unable to repay their installments, until the end of the validity of the Fifth National Development Plan Law, by paying the equivalent to the operating banks and deferring it for three years from the payroll, subject to Article (10) of the Law on the Regulation of Part of the Government Financial Regulations, approved on 11/27/1380, and subsequent amendments and additions, until the end of the implementation of the Sixth Plan Law.
B- Infrastructure development measures:
1- Feasibility study, design and establishment of sanitary disposal systems for rural waste within the boundaries of villages and their collection and disposal outside the boundaries of villages and cities, and determination of the responsibilities of the relevant agencies. The executive regulations of this part shall be approved by the Council of Ministers upon the proposal of the Ministry of Interior.
2- Designing and implementing sanitary wastewater disposal plans (projects) in villages, with priority given to villages located near rivers, wetlands, dams, and villages that have problems with wastewater disposal due to low permeability of the land, through the private sector.
3- Maximizing the integration of the country's rural, nomadic, and agricultural development and development activities and programs and managing them with a jihadi and supervised approach by the end of the first year of implementation of the Sixth Plan Law and creating the integration of planning and providing rural and nomadic services and projects in the form of comprehensive programs, based on the needs and real participation of the people and human, natural, economic, and social conditions.

A- The Ports and Maritime Organization and the Iranian Airports and Air Navigation Company are permitted to form partnerships with reputable international companies (domestic and foreign) to invest in and operate major ports with international and airport functions, with the exception of navigation assistance and aircraft landing and take-off services, in compliance with the general policies of Article 44 (44) of the Constitution and the Law on the Implementation of General Policies of Article 44 (44) of the Constitution approved in 1387 and its subsequent amendments, to form companies for the purpose of investing in and operating major ports with international and airport functions, with the exception of aircraft landing and take-off services. The share of the Iranian Airports and Air Navigation Company, the Ports and Maritime Organization and the domestic private sector will be at least fifty-one percent (51%).
B- The Ports and Maritime Organization is allowed to transfer the right to operate and manage small and local ports, and grant permits to build new small ports to professional and reputable non-governmental legal entities, while maintaining its sovereign duties by providing the necessary support and incentives, in compliance with domestic and international laws and regulations and competitive principles.
Article 52 - In order to strengthen the rail transport economy and encourage investment by the non-governmental sector in this field, accelerate and facilitate the implementation of plans (projects), and increase public satisfaction and acceptance of the services provided in this type of transport:
A- Investment by the non-governmental sector in the construction and operation of urban and intercity rail transportation will be considered as investment in less developed areas and will be subject to all laws and regulations related to investment in less developed areas.
B- Value-added tax on rail transportation services will be calculated at a zero rate, in addition to the exemptions mentioned in paragraph (12) of Article (12) of the Value-Added Tax Law.
C- The Ministry of Roads and Urban Development is obliged to establish a suburban passenger rail transport company with the aim of organizing the outskirts of cities and developing suburban rail transport affairs and building independent suburban lines under the auspices of the Islamic Republic of Iran Railway Company, in accordance with the general policies of Article 44 (44) of the Constitution and the Law on the Implementation of General Policies of Article 44 (44) of the Constitution approved in 1387 and its subsequent amendments, and the Law on the Support of Urban and Suburban Rail Transport Systems approved in 1382/5/52. This company is in charge of all suburban passenger rail transport affairs in the country in cooperation with the relevant municipalities. The activities of urban train companies independently in the development of suburban rail transport affairs are unimpeded. The statutes of the suburban passenger rail transport company shall be approved by the Council of Ministers within six months after the entry into force of this law, upon the joint proposal of the organization, the Ministry of Roads and Urban Development, and the Ministry of Interior.

A- The specialized parent company of Iran Airports and Air Navigation and its affiliated airport companies and the Islamic Republic of Iran Railways Company are subject to the benefits of the Law on the Development of the Iranian Tourism and World Tourism Industry, approved on 7/7/1370.
B- The government is obliged to review, through legal measures, and cancel mandatory discounts and exemptions in the field of air navigation services, airports, landing and takeoff, flight services, assignment of premises, and other related matters, and to make their rates realistic from the time this law comes into effect.
C- The Civil Aviation Organization is required to prepare a written program to achieve the following goals, in order to strengthen the aviation economy, encourage investment in the civil aviation sector, utilize capacities and capital, and improve the scientific and technical level of the country's aviation industry, while observing the principle of supporting domestic knowledge-based industries and transferring advanced technology to the country, as well as creating employment for educated personnel, and implement it after approval by the Supreme Council of Civil Aviation:
1- Anticipating and managing the country's air fleet development needs in accordance with policies based on sustainable development.
2- Policy-making and taking necessary measures to ensure the supply of all types of aircraft and helicopters required by the country's air transport fleet through joint domestic and international cooperation, with priority given to supporting domestic production.
3- Policy-making and taking necessary measures to involve the domestic aviation industry, with priority given to the private sector, in the construction and production of various types of aircraft and helicopters that are supplied and purchased from abroad.
D- In order to support the knowledge-based economy and strengthen and develop the aviation industry, all companies active in this industry in the fields of design, manufacturing, assembly, and repair and maintenance of all types of aircraft and their accessories will benefit from the protective laws and policies foreseen and approved for knowledge-based companies during the implementation of this law.
Note - The Vice President for Science and Technology will be responsible for the eligibility and enjoyment of these supports.

E- Orders for the construction of airplanes and helicopters to domestic manufacturers will be exempt from paying order registration fees.
Article 54 - The Ministry of Industry, Mines and Trade, in cooperation with the Vice President for Science and Technology, the Ministries of Interior and Roads and Urban Development, while supporting domestic manufacturers of equipment required for the urban and intercity rail transportation industry, is required to organize the foreign purchase of the required equipment in such a way that, while observing the law of maximum use of production and service capacity in meeting the country's needs and strengthening them in the matter of exports and amending Article (104) of the Direct Taxes Law approved on 1/5/2012 with subsequent amendments and additions, technology transfer to the country is carried out in exchange for foreign purchases, in such a way that by the end of the implementation of the program law, at least eighty-five percent (85%) of the knowledge of designing and manufacturing the equipment required for the urban and intercity rail transportation industry is carried out with the help and use of national scientific and technological institutions such as the Academic Jihad, universities and research centers, and domestic knowledge-based companies.
Article 55 - The cost of management services for capital asset acquisition plans of the National Transportation Infrastructure Construction and Development Company, the Islamic Revolution Housing Foundation, the Government and Public Buildings and Facilities Implementation Company, the Iran Water and Power Resources Development Joint Stock Company, and the Sports Venues Development and Maintenance Company shall be determined at up to two and a half percent (2.5%) of the relevant credit allocation performance, including the total amount received from the government's general budget, including cash, treasury bills, partnership bonds, and other securities, and shall be made available to the aforementioned foundation and companies by the organization.
The organization is obligated to allocate unavoidable funds to the aforementioned foundation and companies within the framework of their budgets, up to two and a half percent (2.5%) of the above funds.
Article 56 - The Ministry of Industry, Mines and Trade and the National Standards Organization are required to supervise the design and manufacture of domestic vehicles in such a way that the safety of the said vehicles is improved in proportion to the maximum speed so that casualties in road accidents are reduced to the minimum global average.

Municipalities are also obliged to develop urban trains from their own resources.

The facilities and credits required for the government share are approved annually by the National Headquarters for Sustainable Urban Regeneration upon the proposal of the Ministry of Roads and Urban Development and in coordination with the municipalities, and are forecasted in the form of annual budgets and made available to the relevant executive bodies and municipalities. The operational plan in the two sections of housing renovation and provision of services and infrastructure will be prepared in the first quarter of the implementation of the program law by the Ministries of Roads and Urban Development and the Ministry of the Interior, the Organization, the Supreme Council of Provinces, and all member agencies of the National Headquarters for Sustainable Urban Regeneration at the national, provincial, and urban management levels, and will have a commitment and obligation to implement urban regeneration programs.
Note - In preparing the operational plan for the historical areas of cities, the Cultural Heritage, Handicrafts and Tourism Organization is added to the aforementioned agencies.
B- In order to reduce the vulnerability of rural settlements (with the aim of improving and renovating rural housing) and also to provide housing for low-income urban groups (in small cities), the government is obliged to provide the necessary financial resources, low-cost facilities, and land in the form of an annual budget during the implementation of the program law and to implement the following measures through the Islamic Revolution Housing Foundation:
1- Annual improvement and renovation of at least two hundred thousand rural housing units by paying low-cost facilities with a five percent (5%) fee and providing the difference in the facility's profit for the period of civil participation and installment sales.
This ruling governs Article (6) of the Statute of the Islamic Revolution Housing Foundation, approved on 17/9/1366, during the implementation of the Program Law.
2- Providing financial resources, low-cost facilities, and the land needed to help build or purchase at least one hundred and fifty thousand housing units for low-income groups in cities annually (with priority given to cities with populations under one hundred thousand).
Note - Identification and prioritization of target groups and introduction of eligible individuals will be carried out by the Housing Foundation in cooperation with the Imam Khomeini Relief Committee, the Welfare Organization, and housing donors.
C- Bank Maskan is obligated to fulfill the remaining obligations of its obligations and to provide the resources needed by Maskan Mehr without the obligation to build new units, as determined by the Ministry of Roads and Urban Development.
D- The Ministry of Roads and Urban Development, while respecting the duties and powers of other agencies, without creating new organizations and using the facilities of other agencies, is obliged to take action to establish an integrated spatial information system, provide its required infrastructure, and create an appropriate structure for sharing spatial data by all relevant agencies in order to achieve the realization of urban and rural development and development plans, planning and control of violations, urban discipline, and protect the rights of citizens. This ruling does not violate or abrogate any of the laws.
Note - In Article (18) of the Law on Organizing and Supporting the Production and Supply of Housing, approved on 1387/2/25, with subsequent amendments and additions, the phrase "Fifth Plan" is changed to "Sixth Plan." The resources required for the clauses of this article can be obtained from this location.
Article 60 - In order to make buildings more resilient and improve consumption patterns, especially energy consumption, in the construction and housing sectors, the following measures should be taken:
A- Municipalities are required to include the requirement to comply with national building regulations in building permits. The issuance of completion permits for units constructed on the basis of these permits is subject to full compliance with these regulations.
B- All government implementers are required to insure their technical (professional) liability and, if necessary, the liability of other factors related to the design, calculation, and supervision of buildings under their coverage, whether urban or rural, with one of the qualified companies.
C- In order to conduct the necessary studies to reduce earthquake risk:
1- The network of accelerometer and seismograph stations and earthquake precursors should be developed.
2- During the implementation of the law, the standardization program for building reinforcement materials and methods should be fully implemented and the manufacturers and providers of the aforementioned materials and methods should be supported.

Section 12 - General Education, Higher Education, and Science and Technology
ماده۶۳ ـ
A- The government is obliged to take the following measures to bring about change in the education system:
1- Implementing the Fundamental Transformation of Education Document in such a way that all actions of the Ministry of Education are carried out based on this document and no action is taken that is contrary to the law and the document.
2- Preparing a teacher ranking system and establishing a payment system based on expertise, competencies, and competitive performance based on the teacher ranking system and human resource engineering based on the country's comprehensive scientific development document and map, approved by the Islamic Consultative Assembly in the form of an annual budget.
3- Promoting the status of education as the most important institution for training human resources and generating social and human capital, responsible for implementing approved policies and guiding and supervising them as a matter of governance, by developing cooperation between executive agencies.
4- Developing and facilitating student cultural trips, especially pilgrimage camps and Rahian Noor.
B- The country's administrative and employment organization and the organization are required to provide the Ministry of Education with the employment ranks and funding required for education and training, in compliance with the relevant laws and regulations and the student recruitment capacity of the aforementioned universities, in order to improve the quality of the education system by recruiting teachers through Farhangian and Shahid Rajaee Universities, during the implementation of the program law.
C- The Ministry of Education is required to allocate the necessary human resources, equipment, and facilities to the Union of Islamic Students Associations, the Student Mobilization Organization, and the Student Organization based on regulations that are proposed by the Ministry and approved by the Council of Ministers.
D- The government is obligated to formulate and implement policies to support school-building donors and must include the equivalent of their realized donations in the annual budget to complete the donors' unfinished projects.
Note - The executive regulations of this clause shall be prepared by the Ministry of Education and Training and approved by the Council of Ministers within a maximum of two months after this law comes into force.
E- In order to increase the safety and resilience of schools and educational and sports spaces, the government is required to allocate the equivalent of three billion (3,000,000,000) rials of dollars during the implementation of the program law from the foreign exchange reserve account after deducting the share of the National Development Fund, with priority given to incomplete educational, educational and physical education projects of the Ministry of Education.
C- The cost of all non-governmental natural and legal entities in the construction, development, completion and equipping of educational and training spaces, boarding houses, libraries, sports halls affiliated with the Ministry of Education, which is carried out within the framework approved by the Provincial Planning and Development Council, is considered an acceptable tax expense with the approval of the National School Development, Renovation and Equipping Organization.
Note - The executive regulations of this paragraph shall be prepared by the organization within three months after the entry into force of this law, in cooperation with the Ministries of Economic Affairs and Finance and Education, and shall be approved by the Council of Ministers.
G- All income, donations and public cash gifts to the student organization, after being deposited into the treasury, are considered as the exclusive income of the Ministry of Education. The Ministry of Education is obliged to spend the deposited amounts in the form of annual budget only for cultural and educational activities by the student organization in schools.
H- The government is obligated to provide the necessary credit and resources to the Center for the Intellectual Development of Children and Adolescents during the years of the program to promote Iranian-Islamic values and develop a culture of study and research in deprived rural areas and nomadic and border areas.
Article 64 - In order to realize a knowledge-based economy, increase productivity, regulate the mutual relationship between education and employment, expand active international cooperation and interactions, and increase the role of the people in the scientific and technological management of the country:
A- The government is authorized to establish higher education units and branches in partnership with reputable international universities, universities, higher education, research, technical and vocational institutions, and a comprehensive scientific-applied university within the country within the framework of the policies of the Supreme Council of the Cultural Revolution, in order to promote scientific advancement and competition among the country's universities and international interactions during the implementation of the program law. The manner of joint investment, facilitation of foreign exchange transactions, and movement of faculty members and students will be specified in the executive regulations that will be approved by the Council of Ministers upon the proposal of the organization, the country's administrative and employment organization, and the relevant executive agencies.
B- All executive agencies subject to Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law and agencies subject to Article (50) of the Law on the Addition of Articles to the Law on the Regulation of Part of the Government Financial Regulations (1) approved on 15/8/2005 are required to spend one percent (1%) of the allocated budget credits, excluding Chapters (1) and (6), and in the case of state-owned companies, non-operational expenses, on research and technology development.
Note 1- The aforementioned institutions, while observing the framework of the country's comprehensive scientific map and the research priorities of the relevant institution, which are approved by the Supreme Council for Science, Research and Technology, are required to report the spending of this item to the Supreme Council for Science, Research and Technology and the Statistical Center of Iran every six months. The Supreme Council for Science, Research and Technology is required to submit the performance report of this item to the Islamic Consultative Assembly annually by the end of August at the latest. The Statistical Center of Iran is also required to publish information related to research and development spending annually.
Note 2 - The government should prioritize the development and promotion of humanities in universities in terms of Islamization, localization, efficiency, and updating by making changes or reforms in matters related to syllabi and textbooks, teacher training, and student selection.
C- All government companies, non-governmental public institutions, and affiliated and subsidiary companies, with the exception of insurance and pension funds, are required to allocate at least three percent (3%) of their distributable profits from the previous year for use in research and technology development in the annual budget, under the supervision of the Supreme Council for Science, Research and Technology, in order to support problem-based research and commercialization of research and innovation, in implementing the general policies of the Sixth Plan.
Note - The executive regulations of this paragraph shall be prepared upon the proposal of the organization and in cooperation with the relevant executive agencies and shall be approved by the Council of Ministers.
D- In order to increase the productivity of the national innovation system, avoid conducting duplicate research and disseminating information, and create transparency in conducting research projects, and with the aim of identifying, applying, and commercializing the achievements of research and development, the executive agencies are required to register their list of research and technology projects, theses, and dissertations in the "SAMAT" system. The organization and the Ministry of Science, Research, and Technology are required to prepare the required executive mechanism within one year from the date of entry into force of this law and submit it to the Council of Ministers for approval.
Note - The procedure for dealing with information and data related to security, defense, and classified matters, the subject of the Penal Code for Publishing and Disclosing Confidential and State Secret Documents - approved on November 29, 1974 - and its executive regulations - approved in 1975 - are determined in regulations prepared by the Organization and Ministries of Defense and Support of the Armed Forces, Information, and Science, Research, and Technology after approval by the General Staff of the Armed Forces, and approved by the Council of Ministers.
E- All executive agencies subject to this law are required to prepare their operational plans within one year in accordance with the country's strategic document on elite affairs, in coordination with the National Foundation for Elites, in order to recognize and honor the honorable and famous people of Iran, support the country's scientific, cultural and artistic elites, honor the pioneers of the aforementioned fields, and utilize their power and capacity for the development of the country. They are required to implement the plan from the second year of the law's implementation. The National Foundation for Elites is required to submit an annual report on the performance of this clause to the Education, Research and Technology Commission of the Islamic Consultative Assembly.
C- In order to lead the knowledge-based economy and increase the production and export of knowledge-based products and services, the general policies of the resistance economy, to develop and disseminate technology, and to support knowledge-based companies, the government is authorized to take action, in accordance with the annual budget law, to provide financial support for joint demand-driven research with universities and higher education, research and technology institutions, and seminaries in cases related to solving the country's problems, provided that at least fifty-one percent (51%) of its costs are provided and committed by the employer or beneficiary.
G- The executive bodies subject to this law shall provide the necessary measures to facilitate the participation of knowledge-based and technological companies and economic activists of the country in the international production chain in order to expand knowledge-based productivity and improve the level of technology in Iranian companies. The organization is required to formulate the plan for “Promoting the Participation of Economic Activists in the International Production Chain” in accordance with the annual budget law and the applicable laws by the end of the first year of the implementation of the plan law and implement it after approval by the Council of Ministers.
H- The organization is required to carry out the necessary planning in accordance with the annual budget law in line with the mission of the Academic Jihad in the country's comprehensive scientific map document and to utilize the capabilities of the aforementioned institution in order to achieve cultural, scientific, technological and educational development and facilitate the employment of university graduates, in coordination with other relevant authorities.
X- Executive agencies are authorized to transfer intellectual property, technical knowledge, and equipment created and obtained within the framework of contracts with universities and government research and technology institutions to the aforementioned universities and institutions in order to develop, disseminate, and apply technology.
D - Payam Noor University is allowed to establish an international hub to provide virtual (electronic), semi-face-to-face, open and distance education in cooperation with reputable international and domestic universities.
Z- The Ministry of Science, Research and Technology is required to organize the tuition fees of the technical and vocational schools under the Ministry of Education that have been transferred to the aforementioned ministry by the second year of implementation of the program law.
R- Tuition fees for students within the country covered by the Imam Khomeini Relief Committee (RA) and the Welfare Organization are funded by the organization from the funds of the independent program.

Section 13 - Communications and Information Technology
ماده۶۷ ـ
A- The Ministry of Communications and Information Technology is authorized to increase the capacity of the country's broadband transmission (transit) to 30 terabits per second, and to provide remote sensing satellite services and develop information and communication technology services and applications through affiliated agencies, to participate and invest with the private sector and foreign companies in fiber optic projects and infrastructure projects for communications and information technology, measurement and telecommunications satellites, and the development of space science and technology infrastructure, with the exception of telecommunications mother networks, frequency assignment affairs and main networks for analysis and exchanges, and the management of basic postal services, and in compliance with the general policies of Article 44 (44) of the Constitution and the general policies of the Sixth Development Plan, especially security issues in the information and communication technology sector, and the policies and approvals of the Supreme Council for Cyberspace, to take the following measures to meet the needs of the country:
1- The Infrastructure Communications Company is authorized to establish an International Communications Transit Company in order to effectively participate in regional and international markets for the export of broadband technical and engineering services through partnership and joint investment with private and public non-governmental sectors and foreign companies, while respecting national interests. The articles of association of the said company shall be approved by the Council of Ministers.
2- Invest in the development of electronic service infrastructure in deprived and rural areas so that it becomes possible to provide at least four main electronic government services (health, education, agriculture, and banking) in eighty percent (80%) of villages with more than twenty households in the country.
B- In order to develop e-government, maintain the integrity of the national information network, and increase the efficiency of the country's communication infrastructure, executive agencies and civilian institutions that have an independent communication network are authorized for exclusive use within the scope of the relevant agency's internal activities, and are required to obtain a license from the Ministry of Communications and Information Technology and in accordance with the approvals of the Supreme Council for Cyberspace to provide communication and telecommunication infrastructure services on the national information network to other natural and legal persons in accordance with the relevant laws and regulations.
C- Executive agencies are required to take steps to electronicize all processes and services with electronic capabilities and complete the relevant databases by the end of the third year of the implementation of the program law. Executive agencies can use private sector participation to electronicize processes and services. Executive agencies are required to assign all services that can be provided outside their administrative environment and that can be assigned or outsourced to post offices and government service counters and rural ICT offices, as appropriate. The tariff for providing e-government service counters must be approved by the Regulatory Commission. The Ministry of Communications and Information Technology is required to submit a report on the performance of this clause to the Industries and Mines Commission of the Islamic Consultative Assembly every six months.
D- Executive agencies, units under the supervision of the Supreme Leader, whether military or civilian, if he agrees, Islamic city and village councils, and private professional institutions charged with a public mission are required to create in their electronic systems the information items and registered statistics subject to the Iranian Statistics Center Law required to establish a comprehensive system of registered statistics and the Iranian National Statistics Network, and to provide the Iranian Statistics Center with the possibility of its electronic and online exploitation on the national information network platform by the end of the second year of the implementation of the program law at the latest. The Iranian Statistics Center is required to prepare and notify each of the agencies of the registered statistics items and the method of their production within a maximum period of nine months.
E - All executive agencies of the country are required to provide, by the end of the second year of implementation of the Program Law, the possibility of electronic exchange of information and electronic response to inquiries required by other executive agencies, in accordance with their job descriptions within the framework of specific and subject-matter laws, free of charge.
Note 1: The executive regulations for the census of all inquiries and the establishment of a standardization system and inter-agency information exchange in the first six months of the implementation of the Program Law shall be compiled by the National Cyberspace Center in cooperation with the Ministry of Communications and Information Technology and other relevant executive agencies and shall be approved by the Supreme Cyberspace Council.
Note 2 - The Ministry of Communications and Information Technology is required to provide all the necessary infrastructure for information interaction between executive agencies on the basis of the National Information Network, solely through the National Information Exchange Center (NIX) and with technical standards approved by the Ministry of Communications and Information Technology, by the second year of implementation of the Program Law.

During the implementation of the program, the provision of Subsection (1) of Clause (c) of Article (113) shall prevail over Article (2) of the Law on the Transfer of Existing Prisons and Security and Educational Measures Centers Outside the City, approved on 2009/09/7, in the section of inconsistency.
2- In the event of an understanding between the Prisons and Security and Educational Measures Organization of the country and the buyer or buyers, and after signing an official contract, the prison building inside the city will be leased to the Prisons and Security and Educational Measures Organization of the country until the construction and operation of the new prison is completed, and its rental price will be deducted from the agreed upon price of the prison.
3- The Ministry of Agricultural Jihad (the National Forests, Rangelands and Watershed Management Organization) and the Land Affairs Organization are required to transfer free land to the Prisons Organization for the construction of the aforementioned prisons in compliance with the relevant laws and regulations. Also, all executive agencies are required to provide the necessary cooperation, as appropriate, in issuing legal permits and establishing and providing the necessary infrastructure facilities (including water, electricity, gas, telephone, optical cable and access routes) in these projects.
4- The National Real Estate and Documents Registration Organization is required to transfer all the documents and records of the aforementioned prison to the buyer or buyers after the prisoners are settled in the new prison outside the city, in cooperation with the Ministry of Economic Affairs and Finance, and after an official announcement by the Ministry of Justice.
5- The Prisons and Security and Educational Measures Organization of the country is authorized to attract and develop the participation and investment of the non-governmental and public sectors in the field of exploitation or development of the necessary infrastructure in the fields of vocational training, employment during imprisonment and after release, education, health and treatment, support plans for the families of prisoners, and assistance to the self-sufficiency of prisons in the form of various types of official contracts.
Note - The executive regulations of this paragraph shall be prepared by the National Prisons, Security and Educational Measures Organization, in cooperation with the Ministry of Justice, and shall be approved by the Council of Ministers within six months after the entry into force of this law.
D- In order to prevent crime with the aim of reducing the number of criminals by ten percent (10%) annually:
1- The Judiciary is required to prepare and formulate a comprehensive program to prevent crime and promote social health, in compliance with relevant laws, by the end of the first year of implementation of the program law, using the capacities of all executive agencies and taking advantage of the social participation of the people, non-governmental organizations, and scientific and research centers of the country.
2- The Judiciary is required to identify the factors affecting the occurrence of lawsuits and crimes separately in the jurisdictions of each province, and after a comprehensive research study, take the necessary measures, prepare the required bills, and submit them to the Islamic Consultative Assembly, in compliance with Article Seventy-Four (74) of the Constitution, by the end of the second year of the implementation of the Program Law for the Prevention and Reduction of Crime, Lawsuits, and Disputes.
3- The National Prisons and Security and Educational Measures Organization is obliged to inform the judicial authorities about the status of the prisoner population and the open space of the prison, and to provide the competent judicial authorities with the capacity to accept prisoners online and in compliance with security aspects. Judges, taking into account the declared capacity and the proportionality of the security order, shall refrain from issuing security orders leading to arrest or imprisonment sentences except in cases of necessity pursuant to relevant laws, and shall use new criminal provisions such as suspension of prosecution or punishment, postponement of the issuance of the sentence, conditional releases, and alternative punishments to imprisonment, in compliance with relevant laws. The Judicial Disciplinary Prosecution Office shall supervise the proper implementation of this paragraph.
Note - The executive instructions for this paragraph shall be prepared by the National Prisons, Security and Educational Measures Organization in cooperation with the Ministry of Justice and shall be approved by the Chief Justice.
E- The Judiciary (Judiciary of the Islamic Republic of Iran) can recruit a maximum of eight hundred new judges annually to fill its approved non-permanent judicial positions and a maximum of three thousand two hundred administrative and support personnel to fill its approved non-permanent administrative and support positions within the approved appropriations through a recruitment exam and in a competitive environment from among the top talents and academic and seminary elites.
C- The National Forensic Medicine Organization is authorized to delegate the forensic medicine services required by governmental and non-governmental agencies, public institutions, diagnostic and treatment centers, insurance companies, the public, and some of the duties mentioned in Article (1) of the Law on the Establishment of the National Forensic Medicine Organization, approved on 1372/4/27, with the exception of the cases subject to paragraph (1) of Article (1) of the aforementioned law, to a trusted medical group that will be appointed for this purpose, with full supervision.
Note - The executive regulations of this paragraph shall be prepared by the Forensic Medicine Organization in cooperation with the Ministry of Justice and shall be approved by the Chief of the Judiciary.
G- In implementing the policy of reviewing criminal laws and with the aim of reducing criminal titles, the judiciary is obliged to review the examples of life-threatening punishment titles in light of changing circumstances, reduce criminal titles, use security, disciplinary, civil, administrative, and restorative enforcement guarantees, and make punishments proportionate to crimes. It is also required to take action to refine, amend, and eliminate gaps in criminal laws in compliance with the Law on the Compilation and Revision of Laws and Regulations of the Country, approved on 11/3/2010, and its subsequent amendments, and to implement the required legal action plan by the end of the second year of the law's implementation at the latest.
H- In order to use the services of experts and consultants in executive agencies, especially in the fields of budgeting, planning, legal, administrative and employment, the tariff for the aforementioned services for executive agencies that are authorized to use them in accordance with the applicable laws shall be prepared by the organization and in cooperation with the Legal Affairs Office of the President and approved by the Council of Ministers.
X- In order to optimally protect the treasury and the rights and property of the government, a guideline containing various mechanisms for encouraging and retaining employees in specialized legal positions in executive agencies, including in the case of extraordinary judicial protection, shall be jointly proposed by the Vice President for Legal Affairs, the Minister of Justice, the Head of the State Administrative and Employment Organization, and the Head of the Organization, and shall be notified within the framework of the law after approval by the Council of Ministers.

Article 114- The National Real Estate Registration Organization is required to launch and organize the National Real Estate Registry electronically by the end of the second year of implementation of the Program Law and to register all transactions regarding real estate and land in it instantly and electronically in such a way that it is possible to register transactions instantly and online in the Real Estate Registry and also to respond instantly and online to inquiries at the same time. The aforementioned registry, after completion, will replace the existing Real Estate Registry and act as its successor.
Article 115: All officials subject to Article 142 of the Constitution are required to declare their assets and property to the Head of the Judiciary in accordance with the aforementioned Article. Failure to declare assets or concealing them or reporting them incompletely is a crime and is subject to a sixth-degree Ta’zir punishment (deprivation of social rights).

Article 117- In order to guarantee property rights and enforce contracts, reduce delays in proceedings and reduce delays in the execution of court rulings and the provisions of official documents in force:
A- The Judiciary is required to establish an electronic system by the end of the second year of the implementation of the law, which will enable the Judiciary to respond immediately and online to inquiries required by the competent judicial authorities regarding the property of convicted persons in a centralized manner for the Judiciary and under the direct supervision of the Head of the Judiciary through online access to all databases of the property of convicted natural and legal persons, so that the seizure of the said property can be carried out quickly and easily. All authorities that have information about the property of individuals in any way, such as the National Land and Property Registration Organization, municipalities, the Tax Affairs Organization, the Police Force, the Securities and Exchange Organization, the Central Bank, and all banks and financial and credit institutions, and the Real Estate Consultants Union Tracking ID (Code) System, are required to provide the Judiciary with all their information about the property belonging to the said persons, all changes regarding them, and any subsequent transfers thereof, in a way that this information is accessible online and instantly through the said system. The information accessible through this system is classified as confidential and is made available only with the approval of judges appointed by the Chief of the Judiciary upon request of competent judicial authorities or authorities executing judgments or authorities executing the contents of official documents. The manner of implementation of this clause is in accordance with a regulation that will be prepared by the Judiciary within six months after the entry into force of this law, in cooperation with the Ministries of Justice, Communications and Information Technology, and Economic Affairs and Finance, and approved by the Council of Ministers.
