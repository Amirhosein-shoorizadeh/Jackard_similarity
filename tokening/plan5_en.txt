Law on the Fifth Five-Year Development Plan of the Islamic Republic of Iran (1390-1394)
Chapter One - Islamic-Iranian Culture
Article 1- The government is obliged, in cooperation with other powers, to formulate an “Islamic-Iranian development model” that requires the growth and development of humans based on justice and truth, and the achievement of a society based on Islamic and revolutionary values, and the realization of social and economic justice indicators, by the end of the third year of the program, and submit it to the Islamic Consultative Assembly for approval. After approval by the Islamic Consultative Assembly, this model will serve as the basis for preparing the sixth and subsequent programs.
Note - The secretariat for preparing this model will be located in the executive branch, and the heads of the three branches of government are required to assist in the process of preparing it by adopting the necessary mechanisms in each branch.
Article 2 - The government is obliged to prepare cultural annexes for important and new projects based on the country's cultural engineering plan and the cultural annex regulations, which will be approved by the Supreme Council of the Cultural Revolution by the end of the first year of the program.
Note - The Presidential Vice President for Strategic Planning and Supervision, which is referred to as the "Vice President" in this law, prepares the titles of important projects subject to this article at the beginning of each year and notifies the relevant agencies for implementation.
Article 3: In order to deepen Islamic values, religious beliefs, and enhance religious knowledge, strengthen cultural and social norms, the spirit of collective work, innovation, promote the culture of resistance and sacrifice, propagate Islamic values ​​and the Islamic Revolution, and develop the Persian script and language, the government is obligated to provide the necessary support to the non-governmental sector, both real and legal, in the following cases:
A- Religious, religious, artistic, cultural, educational and scientific executive programs
B- Design, production, distribution, publication and export of cultural, artistic, media, handicraft and cultural heritage services and products.
C- Developing digital media, cultural and artistic productions and activities, and multimedia software, as well as an active and influential presence in cyberspace.
D- Developing and launching cultural, artistic, media, religious and Quranic institutions, committees and organizations.
E - Action to remove Latin names from the entrances of public places, companies, and the packaging of non-export goods.
Note 1: The government is required to formulate all necessary standards for Persian script and language and the Hijri calendar by the end of the first year at the latest.
Note 2: The design, production, distribution, and export of cultural, artistic, cinematic, media, and handicraft services and products that promote the culture of nudity, ill-dress, lack of modesty, and vulgarity are prohibited.
Article 4- In implementing the Charter for the Development of Quranic Culture approved by the Supreme Council of the Cultural Revolution, the Deputy is required to prepare strategic documents for the development of the fields of general education, propaganda and promotion, research and higher Quranic education in the country, based on the proposal of the Council for the Development of Quranic Culture, and submit them to the Council of Ministers for approval, no later than the end of the second year of the program.
Article 5 - In order to explain the foundations of pure Islam of Muhammad (PBUH) and the rich culture of the Holy Quran, the Islamic Revolution, and the religious and political thoughts of Imam Khomeini (RA) and the Supreme Leader, as well as to introduce and introduce Islamic-Iranian culture and civilization, and to develop Persian script, language, and literature, especially for Iranians abroad, with an emphasis on the rapprochement of Islamic religions, strengthening dialogue and convergence between followers of religions and scientific and intellectual elites of the world, and developing communication and cooperation with centers of Iranology, Islamology, and preserving spiritual, cultural, and scientific honors, the "National Document for the Development of Cultural Relations of the Islamic Republic of Iran at the International Level" will be prepared by the end of the first year of the program by the Ministry of Culture and Islamic Guidance (Islamic Culture and Communications Organization) in cooperation with the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and other relevant agencies, the Supreme Council of the Qom Seminary, and the Mustafa Al-Alamiyah Society, and will be approved by the Council of Ministers.
Article 6 - In order to develop religious and cultural spaces and optimally utilize the holy sites, the martyrs' garden, and religious places, and to establish the mosque's position as the main worship, educational, political, social, and cultural base, the following measures will be taken:
A- The Ministry of Housing and Urban Development, the Islamic Revolution Housing Foundation, and municipalities are required to provide suitable lands for the construction of mosques and houses of knowledge, after preparation, free of charge and while maintaining public and state ownership, in the design and implementation of comprehensive and detailed urban and rural master plans and newly established towns and cities.
B- Owners of newly constructed commercial, administrative and service premises are required to construct a suitable prayer room in the aforementioned premises. Approval of the plan of the aforementioned complexes is subject to the prediction of a suitable location for the prayer room based on the instructions issued by the Supreme Council of Architecture and Urban Planning.
C- The National Forests, Rangelands and Watershed Management Organization and municipalities are required to construct or allocate sufficient and suitable space for a mosque or prayer room in national parks and urban gardens.
D- All executive agencies, educational centers, hospitals and medical centers, sports complexes, welfare and recreational complexes, and commercial complexes, whether governmental or non-governmental, are required to construct or allocate and maintain sufficient and suitable space for a mosque or prayer room.
E- The Ministries of Roads, Transport and Oil are required to construct mosques and prayer rooms at passenger terminals and intercity fuel supply stations, as well as maintain and manage the aforementioned mosques and prayer rooms through the non-governmental sector.
F- In order to enhance the cultural and artistic function of mosques, establish cultural justice, promote Islamic culture, and attract young people and adolescents to mosques, the Ministry of Culture and Islamic Guidance is required to adopt an arrangement in accordance with Islamic standards so that by the end of the program, at least one-quarter of urban mosques and villages with a population of over a thousand will have a cultural and artistic center.
Article 7: In order to keep alive and visible the religious and political thought and practical conduct of Imam Khomeini (RA) and the Supreme Leader, and to highlight its role as a fundamental criterion in formulating the Islamic-Iranian development model, policies and programs, and its extension to all the pillars of the system, the government provides financial support in the annual budget laws for conducting fundamental and applied research and studies in the field of culture-building, education, and establishing the necessary specialized fields, relying on science, technology, and making appropriate use of the capacity of universities and research institutions, and theses of postgraduate courses on topics related to Imam Khomeini (RA), the Supreme Leader, the Islamic Revolution, sacred defense, and sacrifice and martyrdom inside and outside the country, and equipping the country's public libraries and reputable foreign libraries with works published in this field. Also, to encourage the creators of superior scientific, cultural, artistic and literary works, as well as revolutionary and justice-seeking figures and personalities worldwide, especially those who take effective measures to promote the thoughts of Imam Khomeini (RA) and the Supreme Leader, in accordance with Article 129 of the Constitution, the National Emblem of the Imam (RA) and the Leader should be awarded.
Article 8 - In order to develop seminary and university scientific associations and centers, theory-building, criticism, and debate centers, the government shall provide the necessary financial and legal support for the development of basic and applied education and research in the fields of religion and the dissemination of Islamic culture and knowledge, and related specialized trends.
Article 9: The government is authorized to take the following measures in order to increase the per capita benefit of different segments of the population from cultural, artistic, and sports spaces and to create the necessary infrastructure for the development of cultural and artistic activities:
A- Provide a maximum of fifty percent (50%) of the cost of completing cultural, artistic, religious and Quranic centers with physical development of at least fifty percent (50%) of the non-governmental sector, as well as the construction of adjacent sports facilities belonging to mosques and Basij youth cultural and sports centers, the completion and exploitation of which is necessary for public use, in the form of a grant, based on the regulations approved by the Council of Ministers.
Note - In the event of a change in the use of such centers or a change in use from public to private, the equivalent of the value of government aid at current prices will be taken from the recipients of this aid.
B- Provide financial and legal support for the establishment of cinema, television and media towns and their management.
Article 10: The government is obligated to take legal measures in the following cases in order to organize the country's media space, confront the invasion of foreign culture and media crimes and irregularities, within the framework of the policy and regulations approved by the Supreme Council of the Cultural Revolution, and provide the basis for maximum utilization of media capacity:
A- Preparation and compilation of a "comprehensive media system" by the end of the first year of the program with the approach of facilitating the activities of the non-governmental sector.
B- Promoting public awareness, knowledge, and skills, strengthening aligned satellite and internet media, and confronting foreign opposition media.
C- Propose, establish and implement the required policies and regulations and create and strengthen the necessary mechanisms to organize, monitor and filter visual, audio and written content in the country's media space, including written, visual, audio, digital, virtual media and non-verbal communication networks, data and satellite networks and telecommunication systems to the Supreme Council of the Cultural Revolution.
Article 11- The Cultural Heritage, Tourism and Handicrafts Organization is authorized to:
A- Legal measures should be taken to enable the establishment of cultural and tourism centers and to establish specialized non-governmental centers in the field of cultural heritage, such as museums, restoration of cultural and historical monuments, expertise of historical and cultural properties, as well as centers to supervise accommodation centers, catering, travel and tourism service offices with the necessary standards and easy conditions.
B- Provide financial support for the establishment of specialized museums, especially Sacred Defense and Martyrs museums, by public, non-governmental, cooperative, and private sector institutions or organizations.
C- Provide financial support for the establishment of centers for the preservation of traditional nomadic and rural artefacts and culture by the private sector and public non-governmental and cooperative institutions, in compliance with Islamic standards, in order to develop tourism in those areas.
D- Provide financial and moral support for the ownership and legal rights of the owners of buildings, monuments and movable historical objects in order to preserve, protect and use them appropriately, and to take the necessary measures to insure cultural, artistic and historical works.
E- Identify and support the cultural, historical and spiritual heritage of Iran's cultural sphere, existing in neighboring countries, the region and other countries as cultural heritage.
Article 12 - In order to deepen the values, beliefs, and culture based on Islamic identity, promote the character and tradition of the Ahl al-Bayt (peace be upon them), and optimally utilize the spiritual capacity of pilgrimage sites, especially in the holy cities of Mashhad, Qom, and Shiraz, the government will take steps to carry out the following activities until the end of the program:
A- Accurately identifying the needs and problems of pilgrims, planning and developing the necessary mechanisms to organize pilgrims' affairs, and providing the necessary infrastructure through support from municipalities and non-governmental sectors.
B- Developing facilities, cultural activities and pilgrimage services in pilgrimage and religious tourism hubs, providing favorable conditions for pilgrimage, and implementing the required infrastructure projects within the framework of annual budgets.
Sports
Article 13 - The government is required to take the following measures in order to develop public sports, championships, and develop sports infrastructure:
A- Include appropriations for the acquisition of capital assets and required expenses in independent lines in the annual budgets.
B- The credit required for the payment of facilities in the form of managed funds, interest and fee subsidies, or grants to private and cooperative sector applicants for the construction, development, and completion of sports units should be foreseen in an independent line of the annual budgets.
Note - It is prohibited to pay any funds from the national budget in any form to professional sports.
Article 14- The Ministry of Agricultural Jihad is obliged to provide and transfer to the Physical Education Organization free of charge non-agricultural national lands, with the exception of lands located in the four environmental zones and outside the boundaries of cities and villages, for the purpose of constructing and developing sports facilities and spaces. The aforementioned Ministry is also obliged, with the approval of the Physical Education Organization, to transfer lands with the above conditions, which are required by the non-governmental and cooperative sectors for constructing sports facilities, at the transaction value announced by the Ministry of Economic Affairs and Finance, with priority to veterans' cooperatives. Changing the use of such lands is prohibited.
Chapter Two - Science and Technology
Article 15 - In order to fundamentally transform higher education, especially in the humanities, realize the software movement, and deepen the foundations of belief, Islamic values, and professional ethics, and with the aim of improving the quality of Islamic knowledge and education, the Ministries of Science, Research, and Technology, and Health, Treatment, and Medical Education are required to take the following measures:
A- Reviewing texts, content, and educational and academic curricula based on religious teachings and values ​​and Islamic-Iranian and revolutionary identity, and strengthening postgraduate courses by utilizing the latest achievements of human knowledge, with priority given to the needs of the labor market.
B- Developing and promoting quality indicators, especially in the humanities, especially in the fields of Quranic and lineage sciences and interdisciplinary studies, by utilizing the facilities and capabilities of the seminaries and providing the required training appropriate to the roles of girls and boys.
C- Institutionalizing the scientific and practical experiences of the Islamic Revolution and the Sacred Defense by carrying out educational, research, and theory-building activities in related fields.
D- Cooperating with seminaries and benefiting from the seminary's capacities in various fields.
E- Expanding the chairs of theory, criticism, and freethinking, conducting interdisciplinary studies, developing scientific poles, and producing indigenous knowledge with an emphasis on the humanities in cooperation with the Supreme Council of the Seminary and the Islamic Propaganda Office of the Qom Seminary.
F- Establishing a comprehensive system of monitoring, evaluating, and ranking universities and higher education and research institutions based on indicators approved by the aforementioned ministries, subject to non-contradiction with the approvals of the Supreme Council of the Cultural Revolution, with the aim of improving the quality of education and research.
Note - After the establishment of a comprehensive system of monitoring, evaluation and quality assurance, any expansion and development of fields, groups and academic levels is subject to compliance with the indicators announced by the relevant ministries by universities and higher education and research institutions. The Ministries of Science, Research and Technology and Health, Treatment and Medical Education are authorized, as the case may be, to delegate the implementation of quality assessment and ranking operations based on this system to approved institutions in the non-governmental sector.
Article 16 - The government is authorized to take the following measures in order to achieve the second scientific and technological position in the region and consolidate it by the end of the Fifth Plan:
A- Revising the regulations for promoting faculty members in such a way that up to fifty percent (50%) of the research credits of faculty members are focused on solving the country's problems.
To achieve this, necessary measures will be taken to promote the faculty, including developing and strengthening postgraduate courses, increasing study opportunities for faculty members inside and outside the country, establishing research centers and advanced science and technology in the country, and facilitating communication between universities and executive agencies, including industry.
B- Creating the necessary capacity to increase the percentage of those accepted into postgraduate higher education courses, by improving quality so that the rate of increase in the entry of undergraduate graduates into postgraduate courses reaches twenty percent (20%).
C- Planning and necessary support to provide the required faculty for universities and higher education and research institutions in order to improve the ratio of full-time faculty members to students in all universities, both government and non-government, so that by the end of the program, the ratio of total students to full-time faculty in universities and non-government higher education institutions reaches a maximum of forty and in Payam Noor University reaches a maximum of two hundred and fifty.
D- To establish, launch and equip applied laboratories in universities and educational institutions, academic, scientific and research towns, technology towns, science and technology parks and growth centers through executive agencies and their subsidiaries and affiliates. Part of the research force of these laboratories can be provided by researchers of the executive agency or company, faculty members and postgraduate students of the university. Executive agencies and companies can spend part of their research credits through these laboratories.
E- In order to increase the share of research in the gross domestic product, plan in such a way that the share of research in the gross domestic product increases annually by half a percent (0.5%) and reaches three percent (3%) by the end of the program. In this regard, the research resources subject to this paragraph should be specified in the annual budget in the form of specific programs each year and at the end of the year, the country's research performance report subject to this paragraph should be submitted to the Education and Research Commission of the Islamic Consultative Assembly.
F- The Ministries of Science, Research and Technology and Health, Treatment and Medical Education are required to monitor its indicators and submit an annual performance report to the Education and Research Commission of the Islamic Consultative Assembly, in cooperation with other relevant agencies, while implementing the following measures:
1- Improving the quantity and quality of universities and higher education institutions based on educational justice and the priorities of the vision document, while observing the other provisions of this article.
2- Reforming the pyramid of full-time faculty in universities and higher education institutions, both governmental and non-governmental, by providing the necessary platforms and emphasizing meritocracy.
3- Expanding scientific relations with reputable international educational and research centers and institutions through the establishment of joint universities, holding joint training courses, joint implementation of research projects, and professor and student exchanges with other countries, with an emphasis on countries in the region and the Islamic world, especially in the fields of humanities, religious education, and advanced and prioritized sciences of the Islamic Republic of Iran, based on the country's comprehensive scientific map, with the aim of developing the country's science and empowering faculty members.
4- Establishing coordination between the country's research institutions and organizations for policy-making, planning, and macro-supervision of the Ministry of Science, Research, and Technology and the Supreme Council for Science, Research, and Technology.
5- Developing and implementing a higher education and research needs assessment plan in the first year of program implementation in order to achieve balanced development of government and non-government higher education and research institutions, considering needs and facilities.
6- Establishing an integrated system for monitoring and evaluating the country's science and technology under the supervision of the Supreme Council for Science, Research and Technology in coordination with the Statistical Center of Iran to monitor the country's scientific status on a national, regional and international scale and determine the level of achievement of the goals of upstream documents based on a comprehensive and dynamic system of registered statistics and submitting an annual report to the Education and Research Commission of the Islamic Consultative Assembly.
7- Taking the necessary legal measures to complete and implement the country's comprehensive scientific map in line with paragraph (11) of the general policies announced in the Fifth Plan.
8- Planning for the realization of the main science and technology indicators, including the share of income from exports of products and services based on advanced and intermediate technologies, per capita GDP resulting from science and technology, the number of patent certificates, the number of international scientific products, the ratio of foreign investment in science and technology activities to the country's research expenditures, and the number of knowledge-based companies.
The government is obligated to plan in such a way that by the end of the program, it will reach at least the second rank in the region in this field.
Article 17- The government is authorized to take the following measures in order to develop and disseminate technology and support knowledge-based companies:
A- Financial support for demand-driven joint research with universities, higher education, research and technology institutions, and seminaries in cases related to solving existing problems in the country, provided that at least fifty percent (50%) of its costs are provided and committed by a non-governmental employer.
B- Financial support and facilitation of the formation and development of small and medium-sized private and cooperative companies that operate in the field of commercialization of knowledge and technology, especially the production of products based on advanced technologies and the export of technical and engineering services, as well as support for the establishment of growth centers and science and technology parks through the non-governmental sector.
C- Necessary legal support to encourage foreign parties to international contracts and foreign investment to transfer technical knowledge and some of the related research and development activities within the country and to carry them out with the participation of domestic companies.
D- Financial support for the creation and development of an idea exchange and technology market in order to use scientific capacities to meet the needs of the industrial, agricultural and service sectors.
E - Financial support for student theses and dissertations in order to promote productivity and solve the country's problems.
F- Providing and paying part of the cost of registering patents, producing technical knowledge, and providing financial support to manufacturers to purchase technical knowledge and patent rights.
Note 1: Executive agencies are required to provide research facilities and equipment, laboratories and workshops at preferential rates to knowledge-based institutions and companies approved by the Supreme Council for Science, Research and Technology within the framework of the Cabinet's approval.
Note 2: In order to develop and disseminate technology, executive agencies are permitted to transfer intellectual property, technical knowledge, and equipment created and obtained within the framework of contracts with universities and government research and technology institutions to the aforementioned universities and institutions.
Note 3: The National Real Estate and Documents Registration Organization is required to register patents only after submitting a scientific audit certificate of the inventions by the competent authorities.
Note 4- Faculty members may, with the approval of the Board of Trustees of the same university, establish one hundred percent (100%) private knowledge-based institutions and companies or participate in these institutions and companies. These institutions and companies are not subject to the Law on the Prohibition of Employee Intervention in Government Transactions and its subsequent amendments for the purpose of concluding direct or indirect research contracts with executive agencies.
Article 18: The government shall take the following measures to expand targeted material and moral support for scientific and technological elites and innovators:
A- Improving social status through cultural development
B- Facilitating academic advancement and entry into preferred academic fields before and after entering university.
C- Providing financial support and scholarships through the Ministries of Science, Research and Technology, Health, Treatment and Medical Education, and the National Foundation for Elites.
D- Educational guidance in priority fields and disciplines needed by the country.
E- Establishing social security insurance and basic health insurance for the individual and his family.
F- Eliminating concerns about financial risk in carrying out research and innovative activities.
G- Supporting the commercialization of their achievements
H - Strengthening spiritual dimensions, increasing insight, self-confidence, and national belonging
I - Establishing and strengthening internationally capable scientific, research, and technological centers with special facilities in various branches of fundamental and strategic sciences and technologies by employing elites and scientists to create knowledge, new ideas, and transform science into wealth throughout the program.
Y- Creating suitable job opportunities for elites and top talents in line with their expertise and capabilities and the country's priorities by supporting risky investments to transform technical knowledge into a product that can be offered to the labor market.
K- Establishing appropriate mechanisms to increase the participation of scientific associations, elites, and top talents in decision-making and management of the country.
L- Creating the necessary mechanism for the participation of scientific associations, elites and scientists of the country in international and world-class scientific and research conferences, meetings and forums, and utilizing the capabilities of Iranian scientists and elites in the world, and providing appropriate study opportunities inside and outside the country.
M- Providing and paying part of the costs of registering scientific patents at the national and international levels and creating the necessary arrangements and facilities for publishing their useful scientific works.
Article 19
A- In line with the implementation of paragraph (8) of the announced general policies and with the aim of qualitatively improving the three areas of knowledge, skills and Islamic education, the government is authorized to formulate a program for fundamental transformation in the country's education system within the framework of established laws and in compliance with the following priorities, and to implement it after approval by the Council of Ministers:
1- Transformation in educational and training programs and courses to acquire the competencies needed by Iranian individuals by graduates of general education courses based on the philosophy of Islamic education and needs assessment and the use of technology.
2- Educational guidance based on students' interests and characteristics, professional development, and the human capital needed by the country.
3- Reforming the academic progress evaluation system with an emphasis on the evaluation approach to realize the teaching-learning process in all academic courses and the complete realization of this process by the end of the program.
4- Creating the necessary conditions and motivation for talented students to enter the humanities fields.
5- Planning to establish a balance between the number of students in different academic fields in accordance with the needs of the community gradually and throughout the program, and to establish a balance by the end of the program.
6- Creating a suitable platform for acquiring at least one competency and skill according to the needs of the labor market by the end of the program for all theoretical secondary school students.
7- Establishing a quality assurance system to monitor, evaluate, improve, and increase productivity.
8- Assessing and improving the professional qualifications of existing and required human resources in education and training based on educational and training indicators.
9- Providing a comprehensive Quran education program, including fluent reading and understanding of the Quran, and a combined program of the Quran, Islamic and Arabic education with Quranic goals.
10- Planning to develop educational programs to promote physical and mental health based on religious and Quranic teachings.
11- Strengthening and expanding the student and family counseling system to achieve students' mental health.
12- Utilizing the Physical Education Organization's sports facilities during unused hours to promote students' physical health.
13- Increasing the number of sports spaces per capita for students
14- Applying information and communication technology in all processes to achieve educational justice, facilitate existing processes, and provide educational programs and courses electronically.
15- Establishing effective interaction and communication with other educational systems in the country, including higher education as a subsystem of a single system.
16- Establishing constructive educational interaction with other countries and international centers such as UNESCO, ISESCO, and UNIC.
B- The government is required to take action to increase productivity through optimization of human and financial resources in the Ministry of Education in such a way that the share of non-personnel funds to personnel funds increases during the program.
C- Education is permitted to promote local dialects and native literature in schools within the limits set forth in Article 15 (15) of the Constitution.
D- The Ministry of Education is required to take action to ensure access to fair educational opportunities in proportion to gender and the needs of the regions, especially in less developed regions, and to eliminate educational deprivation. It is required to take action regarding distance and media education and to cover the costs of nutrition, transportation, health, and other matters related to boarding schools.
E- The Ministry of Education is required to take the following measures to meet the special needs and rehabilitation of various educational groups:
1- Making schools more flexible and expanding the implementation of integrated and comprehensive programs
2- Developing comprehensive information programs to inform and educate parents of children with special needs.
3- Strengthening the child assessment plan and increasing counseling in these schools.
4- Developing exceptional schools and children with special needs and providing the necessary special conditions and facilities to empower and increase the skills needed by such learners.
F- The Iranian Broadcasting Organization is obligated to prioritize the promotion of Islamic-Iranian values, the promotion of public culture, and the fight against cultural and social anomalies by strengthening and expanding educational, scientific, and training programs in relevant networks, especially for students, in order to support the transformation of the education system and strengthen the national identity of youth.
Article 20
A - In order to lay the groundwork for training specialized and committed, knowledge-based, creative, and entrepreneurial human resources, in line with the needs of the software movement, with the aim of quantitative and qualitative development, the government is authorized to:
1- Calculate and annually provide the per capita cost of training the specialized human resources demanded by the country based on educational costs and the costs of supported research, investigation, and technology services within the framework of operational budgeting for each university and government educational, research, and technology institute.
2- Financial support and facilitation of conditions for increasing the number of research and extension journals in the country in various scientific fields while maintaining quality in a way that by the end of the program, the number of journals and articles published in domestic scientific journals will increase by at least two times compared to the final year of the fourth program. Also, provide the necessary arrangements for access to reputable scientific databases.
3- Provide financial and legal support and special assistance to the top ten universities affiliated with the Ministry of Science, Research and Technology and the top five universities affiliated with the Ministry of Health and Medical Education that have outstanding performance in expanding the frontiers of knowledge and technology.
4- Provide financial support to scientific associations.
B- Universities, higher education and research centers and institutions, and academies that are licensed by the Council for the Development of Higher Education of the Ministries of Science, Research and Technology, Health, Treatment and Medical Education, and other relevant legal authorities, shall operate without being required to comply with the general laws and regulations governing government agencies, especially the Public Accounts Law, the Civil Service Management Law, the Law on Holding Tenders and their subsequent amendments and additions, and shall operate only within the framework of the financial, transactional, administrative, employment and organizational approvals and regulations approved by the Board of Trustees, which shall be approved by the Ministers of Science, Research and Technology, Health, Treatment and Medical Education, as the case may be, and by the President in the case of academies. Members of the academic staff of the Ministries of Science, Research and Technology, Health, Treatment and Medical Education are also subject to the provisions of this paragraph. The provisions of this paragraph shall also include previous approvals, decisions and regulations, and the aforementioned approvals shall remain in force as long as they are not amended.
Note 1: Any reform of the financial, administrative, transactional, employment, and organizational structure and regulations of universities and state higher education and research institutions, as well as specialized academies, is subject only to the provisions of this paragraph.
Note 2: The funds allocated from the government's public resources to these centers and institutions are considered aid and, after being paid to these centers, can be spent at the designated cost and based on the detailed budget approved by the Board of Trustees and under their responsibility.
C- The equivalent of the income that higher education, research and technology institutions and centers and academies attract each year from donations, gifts and endowment proceeds is also provided and calculated from general income as credits for the acquisition of capital assets.
D- In order to increase the efficiency of the universities' main missions in education, research, and technology, at least twenty percent (20%) of student welfare services should be structurally separated from the university body and transferred to the non-governmental sector each year.
E- In order to reduce entrepreneurship, prevent parallel affairs, strengthen the governing role of the Ministry of Science, Research and Technology, ensure uniformity in policy-making and planning, and improve the quantity and quality of student services, the government is required to establish a single management unit to organize related affairs, utilize the full range of legal, personnel, and credit facilities and capabilities of the Student Welfare Fund, the activities of student sports affairs, the Physical Education Department, and the Student Affairs Office of the Ministry of Science, Research and Technology, during the first year of the program.
F- Expenditure credits and acquisition of capital assets are allocated from the government's general budget to universities, higher education and research institutions, and academies based on an independent line in their annual budget in the form of national credits.
G- The government is allowed to provide credit facilities in the form of long-term loans (Qarz-ul-Hasana) to the Student Welfare Fund or other relevant institutions in order to support students of state, Islamic Azad, Scientific-Applied, and Payam-e-Noor universities, and technical and vocational schools affiliated with the Ministry of Education, non-state higher education institutions that are licensed by one of the Ministries of Science, Research, and Technology or Health, Treatment, and Medical Education.
H- Universities and higher education and research institutions are permitted to accept students from the capacity in excess of their free education quota or from new capacities they create, based on the cost price or an agreement with the non-governmental sector and with the approval of the Board of Trustees at different levels, and to include the financial resources received, as appropriate, after depositing them into the general treasury as specific income.
I- Universities are allowed to supplement part of their educational capacity by admitting foreign students with tuition fees. In special cases, in order to promote Islamic and revolutionary values, it is permitted to admit foreign students without tuition fees or at a discount, with the approval of the Board of Trustees.
Y- The country's universities, as the case may be, at the discretion of the Ministries of Science, Research and Technology and Health, Treatment and Medical Education, with the approval of the Development Council of the relevant ministries, may establish branches in their city of establishment or other cities and free zones within the country and also abroad, on an autonomous basis and by charging tuition fees from volunteers.
K- The tuition fees for students who are veterans (25%) and above, their children, children of witnesses, freedmen and their children, and students covered by the Imam Khomeini Relief Committee (RA) and the Welfare Organization shall be covered from the funds of the independent row of the Deputy.
Note 1- Student admission to the branches of the mentioned universities in the country will be done without a national exam. The criteria for student admission for the undergraduate course will be determined by the working group on Article (4) of the Law on Student Admission to Universities approved on 16/8/2007, and for postgraduate courses, upon the proposal of the universities and, as the case may be, with the approval of one of the Ministries of Science, Research and Technology or Health, Treatment and Medical Education.
Note 2: The amount of tuition received from students is determined and announced by the University Board of Trustees, based on the type of field and the level of educational services, educational assistance, and welfare provided.
It is not prohibited to conduct joint courses with reputable foreign universities in accordance with the regulations of the Ministries of Science, Research and Technology and Health, Treatment and Medical Education in such branches.
Note 3: The government is required to issue visas and residence permits to faculty members and foreign students of these university branches and the Al-Mustafa Al-Alamiyah Society within a maximum of three months from the date of the candidate's request.
Note 4: Iranian students at these university branches will be eligible for academic exemptions, just like other students.
Note 5 - The method of admitting students subject to clauses (h) and (y) of this article at different levels will be based on the proposal of the Board of Trustees of the aforementioned universities and institutions and, as the case may be, with the approval of the relevant ministries and in compliance with the Law on Admission of Students to Universities approved on 16/8/2007. At master's and higher levels, it will be through the entrance exams of the aforementioned institutions, taking into account the academic ability of the candidates, in compliance with the criteria of the National Education Assessment Organization.
Note 6 - The academic certificate of students subject to clauses (h) and (y) of this article shall be issued with the type of admission and place of study.
L- Seminaries enjoy the facilities, benefits, and amenities that are or will be designated for educational and research centers.
Article 21 - In order to expand professional competence through increasing knowledge and skills with an eye towards performing real work in the environment, reform the educational pyramid of the workforce, and promote and empower human capital, reduce the gap between the competency level of the country's workforce and the global standard level, create new job and professional opportunities for young people, and promote the status of technical and vocational education for the country's technical, vocational, and scientific-applied education system, including formal, informal, and unorganized, the government is required to prepare the necessary mechanisms in the following areas within one year from the date of ratification of this law and implement them by anticipating appropriate requirements:
A - Continuation of the apprenticeship and traineeship system in formal secondary and higher education, informal technical and vocational, and scientific-applied education.
B- Providing skills development in the country through granting financial facilities at preferential rates and providing physical and physical spaces with easy conditions, and paving the way for the active and effective presence of non-governmental organizations in the development of formal and informal skills and scientific-applied education in the country.
C- Increasing and facilitating the participation of beneficiaries of technical and vocational training in the government and non-government sectors.
D- Coordination in policy-making and management in planning the country's technical and vocational education as a coherent and dynamic system tailored to the country's needs.
E- Applying the National Vocational Qualifications Framework in a coherent manner to relate qualifications, certificates and diplomas at different levels and types in the field of professions and occupations in order to recognize lifelong learning and determine the competencies of different skill levels.
F- Needs assessment and estimation of the required associate human resources and issuance of necessary licenses and support for the establishment and development of associate training centers in the private and cooperative sectors and creation of the required capacities by the fourth year of the program.
G- Ranking of formal and informal technical and vocational training centers based on executive management indicators, teaching-learning process, human resources, research and development resources, and learner support indicators.
Article 22 - The government is required to provide the difference between the approved and allocated funds, taking into account the adjustment of the years of implementation of the plan subject to the Law amending Article (1) of the Fourth Economic, Social and Cultural Development Plan Law, approved on 1/30/2006, for the reconstruction and reinforcement of schools without reinforcement from public resources or the foreign exchange reserve account in the form of an annual budget, within the first three years of the plan at the latest.
Note - The government is also required to regulate and implement policies to support school-building donors and must include the equivalent of their donations in the annual budget to complete unfinished projects.
The executive regulations of this article shall be prepared by the Ministry of Education and Training within a maximum of two months from the approval of the program and shall be approved by the Council of Ministers.
Article 23: From the beginning of the program, the faculties and vocational technical schools of the Ministry of Education, with all facilities, movable and immovable property, human resources, assets, obligations and responsibilities, will be withdrawn from the aforementioned ministry and will be merged with the Ministry of Science, Research and Technology.
The Ministry of Science, Research and Technology is required to accept students at these faculties and educational institutions for full-time associate's and part-time bachelor's degrees.
Note - The basis for abstraction and transfer is colleges and vocational technical schools that were active or licensed in the 1388-1389 academic year.
Chapter Three - Social

Social insurance
Article 26: The government is authorized to take legal action to reform the structure of pension funds based on the following principles in order to create stability, sustainability, and balance between the resources and expenditures of these funds.
A.
1- Reducing the dependence of these funds on assistance from the government's general budget, with the exception of the government's legal share in insurance premiums during the program, through various means, including regulating factors affecting the funds' resources and expenditures in accordance with insurance calculations, reducing the funds' non-insurance support and transferring it to support institutions, strengthening mechanisms for timely collection of insurance premiums, and strengthening economic activities and profitable investment with priority given to investment in the money and capital markets, so that the desired investment profit is not less than the profit on bank partnership bonds.
2- Any new entrepreneurial activity is prohibited for government funds, and previous and existing cases, as well as management shares that are transferred to the funds in exchange for claims, must be transferred to the private and cooperative sectors through a scheduled program approved by the Council of Ministers until the end of the program's implementation, in compliance with regulations.
3- It is also prohibited to create any insurance obligations and financial burdens beyond the figures stipulated in the tables of the annual budget laws for the funds. The obligations imposed can only be implemented within the limits of the aforementioned figures.
B- Using new methods in the administration and provision of services to these funds in various ways, including revising the structure of social insurance, the Social Security Organization, and affiliated funds, especially the National Pension Fund, and reforming the structure and equipping of other funds affiliated with executive agencies based on the government statutes in a way that emphasizes reducing the costs of manpower from insurance resources and reducing tenure.
Note - The government is authorized to take the necessary legal action regarding the eligibility conditions, the time of benefit, the retirement points system (non-specialized jobs), the determination of difficult and harmful jobs, the amount and composition of insurance premium rates, annual increases, and how to reform the structure of the funds.
Article 27: The government is authorized to establish and deploy a comprehensive multi-layered social security system, with at least three layers:
- Social assistance, including support and empowerment services
- Basic social insurance, including basic pensions and basic health insurance
- Supplementary retirement and medical insurance
Act in accordance with integrity, structural coherence, alignment, and coordination between these layers in the country.
The executive regulations of this article shall be approved by the Council of Ministers.
Article 28
A- In order to provide competitive conditions and increase the efficiency of social insurance and prevent the creation of any monopoly or special privileges for pension funds, whether private, public, cooperative or government, private pension funds are permitted to be established for at least ten years, based on a regulation approved by the Council of Ministers, while ensuring the rights of the insured and pensioners of the relevant fund.
B- All insured persons, with the exception of the personnel of the Armed Forces and the Ministry of Intelligence, may, by observing the principles and requirements of insurance calculations, change their insurance fund to other funds, including the funds subject to paragraph (a) of this article. The criteria for changing funds and transferring insurance premiums and insurance records between the funds subject to this paragraph shall be determined in the relevant regulations.
C- Owners of free professions and businesses are subject to social security laws and regulations.
D- All foreign nationals residing in the country are required to have an insurance policy to cover possible accidents and illnesses during their stay in Iran. The Central Insurance of Iran is responsible for determining the tariff rate in accordance with the regulations, which is subject to approval by the Deputy.
E - In order to establish supplementary pension insurance, social insurance funds are authorized to open private individual accounts for insured persons with the participation of the insured person.
F- If the veteran's income is included in the Social Security Fund and another pension fund, retirement in each fund is carried out independently, and upon fulfilling the retirement conditions of the veteran in each fund, he/she will benefit from the retirement pension of that fund.
Article 29: All resolutions, circulars, instructions, as well as decisions and approvals of the boards of trustees and executive authorities and authorities of the executive branch, with the exception of court rulings that involve a financial burden for pension funds or executive agencies and the government, are enforceable if the resulting financial burden has been previously calculated and financed in the laws of the national budget or the annual budget of the relevant agency or fund. Otherwise, the action of the aforementioned authorities is considered an over-credited commitment and will not be subject to payment by the relevant agency or funds. The relevant agencies and funds are not authorized to enforce the rulings of the executive authorities and authorities of the executive branch whose financial burden has not been financed. The enforcement of the aforementioned rulings is possible only within the limits of the aforementioned resources; in any case, imposing a budget deficit on the government and executive agencies and funds is unacceptable. The responsibility for implementing this clause lies with the heads of the agencies and funds, managers, and relevant officials.
Article 30- Employees covered by pension funds are allowed to continue to be covered by their pension funds in the event of transfer to other institutions or retrenchment, dismissal, resignation, and use of unpaid leave without time limit. In this case, the insurance premium is the insured's share and the employer's share, with the exception of transferred individuals.
Article 31: If the declared salary and wage growth rate of the insured in the last two years of their service exceeds the natural salary and wage growth rate of the insured and is not consistent with previous years, provided that this salary increase is not due to a job promotion, the insurance fund is obliged to calculate and pay the insured's pension based on the average salary and wage of the last five years of service.

A- The activities of the Supreme Council for Health and Food Security, which was formed in accordance with Article (84) (a) of the Fourth Development Plan Law by merging the Food and Nutrition Council and the Supreme Council for Health, will continue during the implementation of the Fifth Plan. Note 1: All health and medical service providers in the country, both governmental and non-governmental, are required to comply with the policy of the Ministry of Health, Treatment and Medical Education. Non-governmental health and medical service providers who are unwilling to cooperate with the comprehensive and universal health system are not contracting parties to the basic and supplementary insurance system and do not benefit from the country's subsidies and public resources related to health matters.
Note 2- Physicians who are in contractual or official employment with government and non-government educational and medical centers are not allowed to practice medicine in diagnostic, educational, and medical centers and private and charitable hospitals. Other health professionals will be subject to the provisions of this note upon the proposal of the Ministry of Health, Medical Treatment and Education and the approval of the Cabinet. In order to compensate the services of this category of physicians, the government will determine the tariff for medical services in such units and centers in proportion to the actual price.
Physicians assigned to the non-governmental sector are exempt from this provision, provided that they receive salaries and benefits from only one location at the government sector level and without any other income.
Academic staff of the Ministries of Health, Medical Treatment and Education, Welfare and Social Security and affiliated organizations, the Board of Directors of the Universities of Medical Sciences and Health Services of the country, heads of hospitals and health care networks are not allowed to work in the non-governmental treatment, diagnostic and educational sector, and any payment to them in this regard is prohibited. The responsibility for implementing this clause lies with the ministers of the aforementioned ministries and their respective deputies and the financial officers of the aforementioned agencies.
Note 3: The creation, development, and modification of health-care units, the provision, allocation, and distribution of medical, dental, and laboratory equipment and supplies are carried out based on the country's needs within the framework of the leveling of health services.
Article 33: In the event of disability or death due to service or non-service, active members of the armed forces and active Basij members are covered by the Armed Forces Social Security Organization for disability, life insurance, and accidents.
Article 34- In order to create and maintain integrity in the provision, development, and equitable allocation of public health resources:
A- The government shall support the private and cooperative sectors in establishing health knowledge towns. The implementing regulations for this clause shall be prepared during the first year of the program and approved by the Council of Ministers.
B- In order to achieve the equity index in health and reduce the share of direct public costs to a maximum of thirty percent (30%) of health costs, create equitable public access to health services, help cover unbearable treatment costs, cover medication, treat special and difficult-to-treat patients, reduce the dependence of healthcare units' operations on dedicated revenue, and help train, secure, and sustain the required specialist human resources, ten percent (10%) of the net total funds from the implementation of the law on targeting subsidies will be added in addition to the health sector credits.

E- The Ministry of Health, Treatment and Medical Education is responsible for providing the necessary conditions for the development of traditional medicine and the use of natural and herbal medicines and products.
F- The Ministry of Health, Treatment and Medical Education is required, in cooperation with the Ministry of Agricultural Jihad, to determine the permissible amount of pesticides and chemical fertilizers for the production of horticultural and agricultural products and to prohibit the supply of products that have used pesticides and chemical fertilizers illegally.
The Ministry of Agricultural Jihad is responsible for providing information and education on reducing the use of chemical pesticides and fertilizers, while also providing people with access to products that are safe to eat.

A- The Ministry of Health, Treatment and Medical Education, with the aim of providing electronic health services, should establish an electronic health record system for Iranians and information systems for health centers in coordination with the National Database of the Statistical Center of Iran and the Civil Registration Organization, while preserving the privacy and confidentiality of data, and with priority starting with the family doctor program and referral system.
All health centers, both governmental and non-governmental, are required to cooperate in this regard.
B- The Ministry of Welfare and Social Security, in cooperation with medical and insurance service organizations and centers, will organize the health insurance service program in an integrated manner and based on information technology in interaction with the "Iranian Electronic Health Record" system within the first two years at the most.
All relevant units, both governmental and non-governmental, are required to cooperate in this regard.
Article 36

A- The list of measures and goods harmful to health and drugs with potential for abuse is determined and announced by the Ministry of Health, Treatment and Medical Education and the percentage of duties for these goods at the beginning of each year by a working group under the responsibility of the Ministry of Health, Treatment and Medical Education and with the membership of the Ministries of Economic Affairs and Finance, Commerce, Welfare and Social Security, Industries and Mines, and the Deputy Minister.
B- The Ministry of Health, Treatment and Medical Education is obliged to take the necessary measures for the immediate and unconditional treatment of traffic accident victims in all government and non-government health and medical units, as well as during their dispatch to specialized centers and subsequent necessary referrals. In order to provide part of the necessary resources to provide diagnostic and medical services to the above-mentioned victims, ten percent (10%) of the third-party, passenger and excess insurance premiums shall be collected from commercial insurance companies and directly deposited in a separate invoice into the special income account with the National Treasury in the name of the Ministry of Health, Treatment and Medical Education. The distribution of these resources by the aforementioned ministry, with the approval of the deputy, shall be made based on the performance of the aforementioned units based on the approved tariffs once every three months. C- Advertising of services and goods that threaten health, examples of which are determined and announced annually by the working group subject to paragraph (a) of this article, is prohibited by all media.
Note - Failure to comply with the provisions of paragraph (c) of this article shall be subject to a fine of ten million (10,000,000) rials to one billion (1,000,000,000) rials by order of the competent judicial authorities. Health insurance
Article 38- In order to develop the quantity and quality of health insurance, achieve comprehensive and equitable coverage of health services, and reduce the people's share of health costs to thirty percent (30%) through various means such as reforming the structure of funds, managing resources, adjusting tariffs, using the funds' internal resources, and, if necessary, through government assistance in the form of annual budgets and throughout the program, the following measures shall be taken:
A- The government is required to determine and announce the necessary mechanisms for universal and compulsory basic health insurance by the end of the first year of the program and provide the necessary grounds in the form of annual budgets to cover all members of society.
B- The government is authorized to merge the health insurance departments of all funds subject to Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law and Article (5) of the National Public Accounts Law into the Health Insurance Organization. The new organization will be called the “Iran Health Insurance Organization.” All matters related to health insurance will be centralized in this organization.
The statute of the Iranian Health Insurance Organization is approved by the Council of Ministers upon the proposal of the Deputy Minister.
Note 1: The provisions of this paragraph shall apply to the medical services funds of the Armed Forces and the Ministry of Intelligence with the permission of the Supreme Leader.
Note 2: Hospitals and private centers of the Social Security Fund that are responsible for direct treatment will remain under the ownership of the aforementioned fund and will sell services to the Iranian Health Insurance Organization in accordance with the regulations.
Note 3: By changing the name of the Supreme Council of Health Insurance to the Supreme Council of National Health Insurance, the composition of the members, duties, powers and responsibilities of this council and its secretariat shall be approved by the Islamic Consultative Assembly upon the proposal of the Deputy with the approval of the Council of Ministers.
Note 4- Assets, liabilities, movable and immovable property, human, financial and credit resources, facilities, buildings and equipment related to the health insurance sector, with the exception of the Social Security Fund, shall be transferred to the Iranian Health Insurance Organization upon the determination of the Deputy. Credits related to the health sector shall also be projected based on performance in providing services and calculated as part of the entitlement tariff in the annual budget.
Note 5 - The conclusion of contracts and any payment in excess of the established tariff for those diagnostic, health and treatment services that will be announced in the basic health insurance package by commercial insurance companies and the Iranian Health Insurance Organization with natural and legal persons under any title is prohibited.
The insured person is responsible for paying the premium for supplementary insurance. Supplementary insurance refers to a list of services that are not covered by basic health insurance.
C- The government is allowed to define basic health insurance services for all people in the country in a uniform manner, in accordance with the establishment of a referral system and family doctor, and gradually implement them until the end of the program.
D- The basic family health insurance premium will be a share of the household head's income as follows:
1- Rural and tribal households and needy groups are covered by support institutions and charitable institutions equivalent to five percent (5%) of the minimum salary and wages of those subject to the labor law in the first, second and third years of the program and six percent (6%) of the minimum salary and wages of those subject to the labor law in the fourth and fifth years of the program. One hundred percent (100%) of the insurance premiums for needy groups are financed from the general government budget.
2- Households of employed and retired civil and military employees equivalent to five percent (5%) of their continuous salaries and benefits in the first, second and third years of the program and six percent (6%) of their continuous salaries and benefits in the fourth and fifth years of the program, provided that the maximum does not exceed twice the insurance premiums of those covered by subsection (1) of this clause. A portion of the insurance premiums of those covered by this subsection will be funded from the general government budget.
3- Those eligible for social security according to the Social Security Law
4- The share of households from other groups in proportion to income groups, as proposed by the Supreme Health Insurance Council and approved by the Cabinet.
Note - Executive agencies are required to deduct the basic health insurance premium of employees, retirees, and officials from their monthly salaries and deposit it into the account of the Iranian Health Insurance Organization within a maximum period of one month.
E - The Supreme Health Insurance Council is required to review the relative value and determine the health service tariff for all providers of health, treatment, and diagnostic services in the country, including government, non-government, and private, each year before the start of the new year, in accordance with the principle of balance between resources and costs and real prices, in order to strengthen appropriate health and treatment behaviors and uniform and uniform calculation principles under competitive conditions, and based on paragraph (8) of Article (1) and Articles (8) and (9) of the Universal Health Insurance Law, and submit the matter to the Council of Ministers for approval after approval by the Deputy.
F- The Ministry of Health, Treatment and Medical Education is authorized to delegate the supervision of health service providers to institutions in the non-governmental sector, based on approved accreditation standards, without imposing a new financial burden. The resources of the Ministry of Health, Treatment and Medical Education are released to the extent that supervision is delegated to the aforementioned institutions. A portion of the aforementioned released financial resources can be paid to the department in the form of an agreement exchanged with the Deputy.
G- The Iranian Health Insurance Organization is required to strategically purchase health services from government and non-government sectors in accordance with the referral system and service leveling based on approved policies. The executive regulations of this clause, including the reform of the payment system and the list of services covered by basic health insurance, shall be prepared within one year by the Ministries of Welfare and Social Security, Health, Medical Treatment and Education, and the Deputy Ministry of Health and Medical Education and shall be approved by the Council of Ministers.
H- In order to improve health and hygiene indicators in food production and supply and reduce government enterprises, the Ministry of Health, Treatment and Medical Education, as the policymaker and supreme health supervisor in the country, can, in cooperation with the Central Insurance of the Islamic Republic of Iran, develop a system of supervision and compulsory insurance to ensure the quality of food production and supply in relevant places based on education and supervision and on the basis of a regulation that is approved by the Council of Ministers after being approved by the Deputy Minister, and implement it by the end of the program in cities with a population of over 100,000.
In this regard, the government supports the development of grocery chain stores.

Veterans
Article 44: In order to continue, develop and promote the culture of sacrifice and martyrdom, and to preserve the dignity of veterans, the government shall take the following measures, with priority given to providing financial resources, opportunities, facilities, facilities and privileges to veterans, fathers, mothers, spouses and children of martyrs, as well as veterans, freedmen and their dependents:
A- Children of martyrs are entitled to all privileges and benefits stipulated in various laws and regulations for veterans of fifty percent (50%) and above, with the exception of car facilities, nursing rights, and reduced working hours. The privileges arising from this clause do not include dependents of the child of a martyr.
B- Providing one hundred percent (100%) of the medical expenses of employed veterans and their dependents is the responsibility of the relevant executive body, and the medical expenses of unemployed veterans and their dependents are the responsibility of the Martyrs and Veterans Affairs Foundation.
C- All executive agencies are required to spend twenty percent (20%) of their cultural funds on promoting the culture of sacrifice and martyrdom, within the framework of the agreement exchanged with the Deputy.
D- In order to reduce the engineering costs of housing construction for the families of martyrs and veterans, the Martyrs and Veterans Affairs Foundation is authorized to provide one hundred percent (100%) of the costs of the relevant services for a one-time fee and for a maximum of one hundred square meters, and to anticipate its cost in the annual budget, in compliance with the Engineering System Law and National Building Regulations.
E - Scholarships within and outside the country are granted to veterans of fifty percent (50%) and above, freed people with at least five years of captivity, and children of martyrs, and in the case of sending abroad, subject to the academic conditions of the law on sending students abroad.
F- Executive agencies are required to meet at least twenty-five percent (25%) of their employment needs from among the children of martyrs and children of veterans fifty percent (50%) and above and children of freedmen who have at least five years of captivity experience without being required to comply with age, educational, and test requirements, and to employ them officially and definitively from the beginning. Five percent (5%) of the employment quota is allocated to other veterans, freedmen, combatants, and their spouses and children. In cases requiring specialization, academic requirements are mandatory.
G- Universities, educational and research centers and institutions, and the Ministries of Science, Research and Technology, Health, Treatment and Medical Education are required to recruit at least ten percent (10%) of their required faculty members from among the veterans' community, including combatants with more than six months of voluntary presence at the front, veterans over twenty-five percent (25%), freed persons over three years of captivity, children of veterans over fifty percent (50%), children of martyrs, and children of freed persons over three years of captivity who have a specialized doctorate degree approved by the Ministries of Science, Research and Technology, and Health, Treatment and Medical Education, as the case may be, through employment or agreement to transfer employees from other institutions or convert the status of non-faculty employees, and from the beginning, employ them as permanent official faculty members, taking into account their relevant educational, research and executive records and their impact on the academic level and rank, without requiring compliance with age requirements and exams. The responsibility for implementing this ruling lies directly with the ministers and heads of the aforementioned institutions. The aforementioned authorities are authorized to make decisions in this regard. The provisions and powers of this clause also apply to current faculty members who meet the above conditions.
H- In order to preserve the dignity and status of veterans and retired warriors, the government is obligated to take steps to ensure their livelihood and insurance coverage within the framework of the annual budget.
I- The government should take steps to organize and establish pensions for Basij members and veterans of the Holy Defense era, including Basij members under the command of the Revolutionary Guards, the Islamic Revolutionary Committee, and the Jihad of Construction.
Y- The strategic document for providing services to combatants is prepared by the General Staff of the Armed Forces in cooperation with the Ministry of Defense and Armed Forces Support, the Martyrs and Veterans Affairs Foundation, and the Deputy Minister, and is approved by the Council of Ministers.
K- Freedmen who have retired before completing thirty years of service due to the doubling of their captivity record can return to service, complete their thirty years of service, and then retire with their captivity record taken into account.
L- The Ministry of Housing and Urban Development is required to provide housing for fifty percent (50%) and above veterans, twenty-five percent (25%) to fifty percent (50%) veterans who have had at least two years of experience on the front, as well as freedmen and children of martyrs and seventy percent (70%) and above veterans who are homeless, provided that they have not previously used government land or housing.
M- Veterans who do not have accident reports or clinical documents, but whose veteran status is approved by the Medical Commission, will be covered by the Martyr Foundation in proportion to the extent of their veteran status.
Article 45- Veterans and children of martyrs are exempted from paying the difference for fifteen years of insurance years resulting from the transfer of insurance records from one insurance and pension fund to another insurance and pension fund resulting from a change in the employment system. This amount is considered as the claim of the transferred fund from the government and is paid and settled by the government.

Information Technology
Article 46- In order to expand e-government services, the information technology industry, information literacy, and increase productivity in the economic, social, and cultural fields, the following measures shall be taken:
A- The Ministry of Communications and Information Technology is required to establish and develop a national information network and secure and sustainable domestic data centers with appropriate bandwidth, in compliance with the country's Sharia and security standards, and by utilizing the capacity and capacity of the public, non-governmental, private and cooperative sectors, provide high-speed access based on service level agreements in such a way that by the end of the second year, all executive agencies and subordinate and affiliated units and by the end of the program, sixty percent (60%) of households and all businesses can connect to the national information and Internet network. The level of international Internet bandwidth and the electronic readiness index and the electronic government development index must be designed in such a way that the per capita bandwidth and other communications and information technology indicators are ranked second in the region at the end of the program. Support for the public, non-governmental, private and cooperative sectors in the country's information technology industry, especially the software and security sectors, must be organized in such a way that the share of this industry in the GDP reaches two percent (2%) in the last year of the program.
The implementing regulations of this clause, including support for the information technology industry, will be prepared by the Ministries of Communications and Information Technology, Industries and Mines, and Commerce and the Deputy Minister within the first six months of the program and approved by the Council of Ministers.
B- All executive agencies are required to connect to the national information network and develop and complete their databases by the end of the second year of the program, based on Chapter 5 of the Civil Service Management Law, and maintain and update their information in internal data centers in compliance with security regulations and necessary standards. Based on the executive regulations that will be prepared by the Ministry of Communications and Information Technology and the Ministry of Information and the Deputy Minister in the first six months of the program and approved by the Council of Ministers, they are required to exchange and share information free of charge in order to create information systems and reduce the production and maintenance of duplicate information in this network by ensuring and maintaining the security of information production, processing, and maintenance.
The judiciary is exempt from the scope of this clause.
C- All executive agencies are required to:
1- By the end of the second year of the program, all inquiries between departments and their affiliated units should be sent and received electronically using the National Information Network and security must be observed.
2- By the end of the program, they should offer their services electronically through the National Information Network, and also transfer all services that can be provided outside their office environment and that can be assigned or outsourced to post offices and government service counters that are established and managed by non-governmental sectors, whether private or cooperative. Other currently operating offices of the aforementioned agencies that provide these types of services should be changed to government counter offices.
The law specifies the scope of activity and the entities covered, as well as the criteria related to counter offices and the characteristics of their constituent persons and managers.
3- The government is authorized to prepare a comprehensive e-government map by the end of the first year of the program in such a way that the provision of government services may be carried out through the electronic system at the end of the program.
D- The Ministry of Interior (Registration Organization) is required to complete and amend the identity database in a manner that includes all vital events such as birth, marriage, divorce, death, and changes in identity details, and issuance of certificates (electronic signatures) and other applications, and to provide and issue a multi-purpose national smart card to all citizens by the end of the program. All eligible natural and legal persons are required to use this card in providing their services to the public. The executive regulations of this clause, including the timing, examples, cases of inclusion, level of access, amount and method of charging the card issuance fee, and method of using it, shall be prepared by the Ministries of Communications and Information Technology and the Interior in coordination with the Deputy and shall be approved by the Council of Ministers.
E- The Deputy will take action to establish a National Spatial Data Infrastructure (NSDI) at national to local levels and develop criteria and regulations for their production and publication by the end of the third year of the program at the latest.
Note - All agencies are required to produce their basic spatial information and place it on the national spatial data infrastructure based on the instructions compiled and communicated by the Deputy.
F- The National Real Estate and Documents Registration Organization is obliged to:
1- In cooperation and coordination with the National Surveying Organization and other relevant agencies, by the end of the program, take steps to create a legal database of real estate and complete the cadastre plan.
2- To develop an integrated system for recording official documents and properties, take action to electronicize all stages of transfers, official documents and property registration by the end of the second year of the program.
3- Complete the database of information on registered companies and institutions based on the template and standards announced by the Deputy, stating the details of shareholders, board members, and their national code numbers, as well as a unique identifier with search and public access capabilities, by the end of the first year of the program.
G- The National Land and Property Registration Organization and the Ministry of Commerce, in cooperation with the Tax Affairs Organization, are required to develop a secure electronic system for real estate transactions nationwide. All relevant executive agencies are required to cooperate in the full implementation of this system.
H- The Ministry of Communications and Information Technology should take steps to create the necessary infrastructure to develop the country's scientific network. Universities, educational, research and technological institutions are required to connect to the aforementioned network and place their scientific content and software and hardware facilities on this network, while preserving intellectual property and observing the necessary standards.
Note - Seminaries and non-governmental sectors, including cooperatives and private ones, are required to comply with the provisions of this clause if they wish to use the country's scientific network.
I- The Ministry of Communications and Information Technology is authorized, with the approval of the Communications Regulatory Commission, to collect amounts as royalties, damages, and fines for failure to fulfill obligations stated in issued licenses and deposit them into the general revenue account with the national treasury. The funds required for the development of communications and information technology infrastructure in less developed regions are provided in the form of annual budgets.
The fine subject to this clause and its amount can be collected if it is stipulated in the contract.
Y- The government is allowed to organize deposits and payments from government accounts in a way that can be tracked, with priority given to the use of electronic cards or other electronic-computer interface devices and tools.
Note 1: In implementing the provisions of this article, the use of available, capable resources has priority.
Note 2 - The country's national information network (IP) is a network based on the Internet contract, along with switches, routers, and data centers, in such a way that internal access requests and information retrieval that are stored in internal data centers are not routed through outside the country in any way, and it is possible to create intranets and private and secure internal networks within it.
Note 3: The Ministry of Communications and Information Technology may develop an information and communication technology indicator monitoring system by the end of the second year of the program to assess indicators and the situation in national, regional and international dimensions. All executive agencies and non-governmental institutions are required to provide the required and relevant information and statistics to this ministry.
The Ministry will report annually to the Islamic Consultative Assembly on the extent to which the goals of this article have been achieved.
Article 47 - Given the increasing importance of space development and the acquisition of new sciences and technologies in order to meet the country's needs for space applications and services, the government may take the following measures:
A- Creating the necessary conditions and providing the necessary support to create and develop infrastructure and industries related to the design, construction, testing, launch, and operation of satellite space systems, satellite carriers, and ground stations.
B- Maintaining and preserving the orbital positions belonging to the Islamic Republic of Iran and anticipating the necessary measures to create infrastructure and implement national satellite projects in order to preserve the aforementioned points.
Article 48- In order to develop e-government, e-commerce, and the provision of e-services, and to authenticate electronic documents and reduce paper documents, the following measures shall be taken:
A- The Ministry of Commerce will take steps to develop electronic certificate issuance centers and the use of electronic signatures in such a way that by the end of the third year of the program, electronic service and commercial systems will be able to use this tool.
B- An electronic document is considered a paper document, provided that the authenticity of its issuance and its integrity are proven.
C- In any case where, by law, it is necessary to prepare papers or documents, as well as to issue or grant licenses, notify and notify, exchange funds, and the like, its electronic execution is permitted and sufficient, in compliance with the provisions of the Electronic Commerce Law.
Article 49- In order to develop and strengthen the banking system, the following measures shall be taken:
A- Complete establishment of the Core Banking system and conversion of all accounts, including individual deposits and facilities, into centralized accounts by banks in compliance with the standards and guidelines of the Central Bank.
B- Establishing and operating an electronic certificate issuance center for the banking network in cooperation with the National Electronic Certificate Issuance Center by the Central Bank of the Islamic Republic of Iran in order to increase the security of electronic information exchange.
Note - All banks should facilitate the use of electronic signatures in their banking transactions.
Administrative system
Article 50
A- The Council of Ministers, as the authority, shall approve the annual increase in the salary coefficient of employees covered and not covered by the Civil Service Management Law (in such a way that the inclusion of the difference in the application of the subject of Chapter Ten of the said law does not prevent the increase) and approve the increase in the tables and privileges and the amount of the extraordinary benefits subject to Chapter Ten of the said law, including the extraordinary extension of self-sacrifice for the children of martyrs up to a maximum of fifty percent (50%) and the determination of holiday and semi-annual bonuses only for employees who, as determined by the relevant body, have rendered outstanding services, and the determination of the cases and limits of direct and indirect welfare assistance shall be determined in accordance with Article (125) of the Civil Service Management Law. The approvals of the Council of Ministers, the Salary and Wages Council, and the Management Development Council shall be within the ceiling of the approved credits included in the annual budgets.
B- The use of the government's general budget and other resources to increase the salaries and benefits of employees in the stage of matching the general, specialized, and exclusive jobs of the executive branch with the relevant tables will be subject to the determination of the aforementioned jobs and their approval by the Management Development Council.
C- In order to eliminate inequality and discrimination between retirees of different years, it is permissible to apply the provisions and tables of Articles (109) and (110) of the Civil Service Management Law based on the coefficient approved by the government for retirees with the approval of the Council of Ministers.
D- The government is authorized to approve a comprehensive unemployment insurance program for official and contract employees and individuals under contract with executive agencies and implement the program from the second year using the resources of the executive agency and the tier holder and resources obtained from the participation of the insured.
E- Employing individuals under fixed-term or hourly employment contracts to perform organizational duties is permitted only within the limits set forth in the Civil Service Management Law. Renewal of previous contracts is permitted.
F- Transfer of official or permanent employees, surplus of executive agencies at the level of a city without the consent of the employee, with the agreement of the relevant agencies, transfer to other cities must be done with the consent of the employee.
G- The difference in the application of the subject of the note to Article (78) of the Civil Service Management Law is not taken into account in the calculation of the extras subject to paragraph (9) of Article (68) of the aforementioned law.
H- Any payment outside the provisions of Chapter 10 of the Civil Service Management Law is prohibited, except in the exceptional case of state-owned companies, banks, and insurance companies covered by Article (4) and the last part of Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law, which is carried out in accordance with the instructions approved by a working group consisting of the Vice President, the Vice President for Management Development and Human Capital, and the highest official of the requesting institution.
Article 51 - Any employment of official and contractual human resources in executive agencies subject to the Civil Service Management Law is prohibited.
Note 1: The number of employment permits for each year within the ceiling of the approved credits in the budget law, in compliance with the provisions of paragraph (b) of Article (24) of the Civil Service Management Law, shall be approved by the Management Development Council upon the joint proposal of the Presidential Deputy for Management Development and Human Capital and the Deputy. The Presidential Deputy for Management Development and Human Capital, the Chairman of the aforementioned Council, and the Deputy shall be designated as members of the councils subject to the Civil Service Management Law.
Note 2- Clause (c) of Article (24) of the Civil Service Management Law, Government Jobs, and Less Developed Areas, as well as Clause (c) of Article (16) of the Fifth Program Law, which is subject to the provision of Clause (b) of Article (20) of this law, and the implementation of the Law on Determining the Employment Duties of Tuition-Paying Teachers and Literacy Movement Educators in the Ministry of Education, approved on 15/7/2009 and its subsequent amendments, are exempt from the provisions of this article.
Article 52 - The tax exemption ceiling subject to Articles (84) and (85) of the Direct Taxes Law shall be determined in the annual budget during each year's program. Also, in Article (85) of the aforementioned law, instead of the phrase "subject to the Law on the Coordinated Payment System approved on 12/6/1370", the phrase "ministries and institutions and other government agencies subject to Articles (1) and (2) and the last part of Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law, as well as other executive agencies subject to Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law and Article (5) of the National Public Accounts Law in the event of the implementation of Chapter Ten of the Civil Service Management Law and its subsequent amendments and additions, judges, faculty members of universities and higher education and research institutions" shall be replaced.
Article 53 - The government is required to merge one or more ministries into other ministries in such a way that by the end of the second year of the program, the number of ministries is reduced from twenty-one to seventeen.
The duties and powers of new ministries are proposed by the government and approved by the Islamic Consultative Assembly.
Article 54 - In order to integrate, organize, and eliminate parallel work in the country's statistical system:
A- The Statistical Center of Iran is the official authority for the preparation, announcement, and publication of official statistics of the country.
B- The Statistical Center of Iran, within the first year of the program at the latest, in cooperation with the executive agencies, shall prepare the country's national statistical program based on modern technologies, in compliance with the standards and regulations for the production and publication of official statistics, the establishment of a system of registered statistics, and the establishment and continuous feeding of the country's statistical database. This program shall be effective for the executive agencies upon the proposal of the Statistical Center of Iran and the approval of the Supreme Council of Statistics.
C- Executive agencies are allowed to produce specialized statistics in their respective fields, within the limits of their legal duties and within the framework of the criteria and standards approved by the Supreme Council for Statistics.
Supervision of the proper implementation of this article is the responsibility of the Deputy and, if delegated, the relevant Deputy (Head of the Statistical Center of Iran).
Article 55 - In order to create uniformity in the production of maps and spatial information in the country, the National Mapping Organization shall be responsible for the leadership, coordination and necessary measures in the field of all activities related to mapping and spatial information, aerial photography, preparation and production of coverage and urban maps at various scales and its supervision in the civilian sector. Relevant guidelines and standards shall be prepared and communicated by the Deputy Development.
Article 56 - In order to eliminate parallel organizations, centralize the construction of government and public buildings of the executive branch, improve the quality of construction, and also the necessity of observing regulations, rules, and standards, the government is obliged to centralize the planning, study, design, and implementation of the aforementioned buildings in the Ministry of Housing and Urban Development (the implementing organization for government and public buildings and facilities). The ministries of education and information, the armed forces, the Atomic Energy Organization, and cases that are proposed by the Deputy and approved by the Council of Ministers are exempt from this ruling.
The Deputy Ministry of Development and Human Capital is required to reform the structure of the agencies that are involved in the construction of public government buildings by the end of the first year of the program.
Article 57 - Recruitment of human resources, either formally or on a contractual basis, in the executive branch, subject to the limits of credits and employment permits, is subject to a public examination, with the recognition and approval of the Vice President and the Vice President for Management and Human Capital Development, respectively.
Note 1: Veterans and children of martyrs are subject to their own relevant laws and regulations.
Note 2: Converting contractual status to official status without the need for an exam is permitted, subject to compliance with the credit ceiling and employment permits, with the recognition and approval of the Vice President and the Vice President for Management and Human Capital Development.
Note 3: In agencies formed by the merger of two or more agencies, as well as in special cases approved by the Council of Ministers, it is not necessary to comply with the ceiling on the number of deputy departments in the agency, as stated in the Civil Service Management Law.
Article 58 - Management incentive fees for efficiency and production of products, whether goods or services or activities, exceeding the figures foreseen in the relevant memorandum of understanding in units that are assigned to the relevant managers based on the cost price and control of the product and result in the form of a memorandum of understanding, are paid based on an analysis of the resources and costs and efficiency of the relevant unit over the past ten years without observing the limitations stipulated in employment laws and regulations.
The implementation of this ruling in the executive bodies of Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law is only permitted in the form of a mutual agreement with the Deputy.
Article 59 - Those employees of institutions who are subject to the Social Security Law are subject to the aforementioned law and its amendments in every respect, including retirement conditions and other benefits.
Those whose retirement deductions were deducted from their overtime pay before the implementation of the Civil Service Management Law (1/1/2009) will have their severance pay and retirement benefits calculated in proportion to the amount deducted. In organizations where the labor job classification plan has been implemented in accordance with Article (124) of the Civil Service Management Law, employees subject to the Labor Law will only benefit from the benefits of the Labor Law.
Article 60 - Determining and applying job and employee privileges and special benefits related to veterans (veterans, freedmen, combatants and children of martyrs and children of veterans seventy percent (70%) and above) and implementing other provisions of the Civil Service Management Law regarding them must be done in a way that their income for their sacrifice is not reduced and legal privileges such as not having to participate in employment exams and age and educational requirements continue to be implemented and applied.
Note - War-affected areas, the subject of paragraph (2) of Article (68) of the Civil Service Management Law, are determined by the Council of Ministers.
Article 61 - Workers and employees of the units to be transferred shall be granted preferential shares only once. The government is authorized to transfer shares or other properties and assets to the executive agencies subject to Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law and Article (5) of the Public Accounts Law and to pension funds and its employees in exchange for their claims, with their agreement. Payment of claims of government employees through the transfer of shares and enterprises, properties and assets to pension funds and cooperatives and the aforementioned executive agencies is also authorized, in compliance with Note (1) of Article (6) of the Law on the Implementation of General Policies, Article (44). The aforementioned agencies and funds are required to transfer the properties and shares they acquire in the implementation of this ruling to the private sector or cooperatives in accordance with the laws and regulations in such a way that in no case is their investment activity in the form of a business.
Article 62 - In all cases where the activities of natural and legal persons are subject to obtaining a license, including a certificate, permit, license, inquiry or approval and similar matters from the executive agencies subject to Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law and Article (5) of the Public Accounting Law, the aforementioned agencies are required to officially announce, within a maximum period of three months after the notification of this law, the type of license and related activity, as well as the legal bases for the activity being subject to obtaining a license, as well as the method of issuance, extension, cancellation and other regulations governing it, to a working group consisting of the Vice President, the Vice President for Management and Human Capital Development, the Vice President for Legal Affairs, the Ministers of Economic Affairs and Finance, Labor and Social Affairs and Justice, as well as three members of the Parliament from the Planning and Budget Commissions, Article Ninety (90) of the Constitution and Economic Law as observers. In the event that the documents subject to this article are not sent within the specified deadline, it is prohibited to require natural and legal persons to obtain a license, and the highest official of the agency and the authorized officials and managers on his behalf are responsible for the proper implementation of this ruling.
The working group is required to issue a directive within six months after the expiration of the above deadline, including reviewing their legal validity with the approach of facilitating, accelerating, reducing the cost of issuing and renewing licenses and coordinating various agencies and eliminating unnecessary licenses and amending or replacing the method of regulating each type of activity and conducting spot inspections to ensure compliance with those regulations, instead of the method of deferring the activity to obtaining a license, to issue a directive containing the review, facilitation, amendment, and replacement of methods, consolidation of licenses, and cancellation of unnecessary licenses within the framework of legal regulations for each type of activity after approval by the President. This directive replaces the directives and executive procedures canceled or amended pursuant to this article and is applicable to all the aforementioned executive agencies.
Changes or amendments to the provisions of the laws related to licenses and matters covered by this article shall be subject to approval by the Islamic Consultative Assembly.
Note 1- The aforementioned working group is required to make the instructions on the subject of this article, including the types of licenses, the authority and method of issuance, extension, cancellation, and restoration, and the relevant timing, public in a database that it designs and establishes for this purpose. All information on the subject of this article shall be published in the form of a yearbook before the beginning of each year. Applying regulations related to carrying out activities or providing services on the subject of this article outside of the cases published in the yearbook and the aforementioned information database is prohibited and the perpetrator shall be sentenced to the punishment stipulated in this article in addition to compensating the injured party.
Note 2 - In addition to licenses, the provisions of this ruling will include all services and activities of the aforementioned executive agencies, including the document and property registration organizations (company registration), civil registration, social security, environmental protection, the Iranian Institute of Standards and Industrial Research, the Municipality of Tehran and metropolitan cities, the police force, customs, the judiciary services, and the ministries of education, science, research and technology, health, medical treatment and education, and industries and mines, as well as cases where, based on the laws, funds are collected from natural and legal persons in exchange for services.
The implementation of the provisions of this note in institutions affiliated with the judiciary is subject to the approval of the head of that branch.
Regarding regulations and matters that are based on the laws, measures, orders, and opinions of the Supreme Leader, as well as regarding the institutions that are directly under his supervision, the implementation of this ruling is subject to the permission of His Majesty.
Note 3: The aforementioned executive bodies are not allowed to charge more than what is prescribed in the laws and legal regulations in return for providing services or granting various types of licenses, even by agreement. Violation of the implementation of this provision and other provisions of this article is subject to the punishment referred to in Article (600) of the Islamic Penal Code.
Article 63 - In order to utilize the services of experts and consultants in executive agencies, especially in the fields of budgeting, legal, administrative, and employment planning, the qualification recognition system, including ranking, disciplinary investigation, and tariffs for the expert services of the aforementioned consultants and experts, shall be proposed by the Vice President and the Vice Presidents of "Legal Affairs" and "Management and Human Capital Development" and approved by the Council of Ministers.
Article 64 - In cases where, in the implementation of the Civil Service Management Law, government functions are transferred to the private sector or cooperatives, the transfer of functions does not remove the government's sovereign responsibility towards citizens. In such cases, the regulation of the relationship between the government and the private and cooperative sectors that have assumed the functions of the government's transferred functions is based on a regulation that is approved by the Council of Ministers.
If the desired result is not achieved, the government is obliged to delegate the aforementioned duties to other private sector or cooperative applicants, while complying with the Civil Service Management Law and relevant regulations based on the bylaws subject to this article, in such a way that service provision to the public continues without interruption. In this case, the costs incurred by the private sector and cooperatives can be covered in the annual budget with the approval of the Economic Council, taking into account the losses incurred.
The Secretariat of the Economic Council (Deputy), in compliance with the laws, may use official judicial experts or qualified experts working in the relevant executive agencies to calculate acceptable costs and damages to the parties. The Deputy is required to notify the Economic Council's enforceable approvals.
Assignments subject to the Law on the Implementation of General Policies, Article Forty-Four (44), are not subject to the provisions of this Article.
Article 65 - In order to organize and reduce human resources and downsize the government:
A- Any transfer to metropolitan cities is prohibited except for officials and in special cases. This ruling and other regulations related to the transfer and assignment of official and permanent employees between agencies are applicable to contract and contractual employees (during the contract). Special cases are determined by a working group with representatives from the Vice President, the Vice President for Management Development and Human Capital, and the relevant agency.
B- Any employment of persons who have been or will be retrenched or retired in the implementation of laws and regulations, with the exception of officials, faculty members, veterans, children of martyrs and children of veterans seventy percent (70%) and above, in executive or tiered agencies or any agency that uses the entire country's budget in any way, is prohibited, as is any payment from any place and under any title to retired persons and retrenching by the aforementioned agencies. All general laws and regulations contrary to this ruling are hereby repealed, with the exception of Article (41) of the Law on the Addition of Articles to the Law on the Regulation of Part of the Government's Financial Regulations, Article (95) of the Civil Service Management Law, and Article (91) of the Civil Service Employment Law.
C- The total number of employees of each institution in transferable positions, including official, contractual, fixed-term, hourly, and similar positions, with the exception of faculty members, shall be reduced by at least two percent (2%) annually. Annual exceptions shall be determined in the budget law of the same year.
D- Any transfer or assignment of more than six months in one or more periods from non-governmental public institutions and organizations to government ministries, institutions and companies subject to Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law is prohibited, with the exception of officials.
Article 66 - The government's debt to the agencies subject to Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law and Article (5) of the State's General Accounting Law, and to state and public funds and legal entities, with the exception of the debt related to the government's legal share of insurance premiums, shall be calculated and settled from the resources resulting from the implementation of the provisions of this law, and any new obligation shall only be enforceable within the framework and limits of the annual budget of the entire country.
The debt related to the government's legal share of the funds' insurance premiums, after calculating the portion that is offset from the remaining resources from the implementation of this law and the country's budget for previous years, while observing the managers' legal responsibility to observe the fund's interests and the normal profit of investments, can be paid and secured through provisions in the annual budget laws.
Article 67 - All executive branch agencies that are authorized by the annual budget laws to provide assistance to natural and legal persons from the funds of the aforementioned laws are required to take the national number of the natural persons receiving assistance or the main shareholders and owners of company shares and members of the board of directors and the legal inspector or inspectors of the legal persons receiving assistance and enter it in the database created by the deputy for this purpose, and then pay the amount of assistance. Assistance from the resources and lines at the disposal of the deputy is also subject to this ruling.
Article 68 - In order to prepare statistics and information required to calculate indicators of various specialized fields, such as:
1- Islamic-Iranian development model
2- Evaluation of the country's twenty-year vision document
3- Evaluation of the performance of the Fifth Schedule Law
4- National productivity
5- Human development
6- Consumption pattern
7- Developing national standards
8. Spatial data
9- Land planning
10- Administrative system, management and information technology
11- Millennium Development Goals (MDG)
12- Comprehensive scientific map of the country
After receiving the identification certificate of the indicators, the Statistical Center of Iran is required to implement the relevant executive plan, including census plans, the country's statistical database, operational information systems, and scheduling, and to prepare the required funds within a maximum of six months and submit them to the Supreme Council of Statistics for approval.
The executive agencies in charge of the above specialized fields are required to submit the relevant identity cards to the Statistics Center of Iran within a maximum of six months after the program is announced.
One member of the Planning, Budget and Accounting Commission and one member of the Economic Commission of the Islamic Consultative Assembly participate in the meetings of the Supreme Statistics Council as observers.
Chapter Five - Economic
Improving the business environment
Article 69 - In order to improve the business environment in the country, the Ministry of Commerce is required to prepare a plan for implementation by the end of the first year, in cooperation with the Iranian Chamber of Commerce, Industries and Mines, the Cooperative Chamber, the National Real Estate Registration Organization, and other relevant agencies, in coordination with the Deputy and within the framework of annual budgets, the necessary incentive criteria and policies, including awarding prizes or granting facilities or financial assistance, for the use of goods and services under the trade name and trademark in the retail and wholesale markets, while preserving the intellectual rights of the trademark owner.
Article 70: In the case of those economic activities that require obtaining permits from multiple agencies, the main agency responsible for the activity will be responsible for the integrated management, coordination, and administration of obtaining, completing, and issuing permits. By creating a single window in real time or in cyberspace, with the participation of other relevant agencies, it will act in such a way that, while observing the principle of simultaneous issuance of permits, the time limit for issuing permits does not exceed the time foreseen in the Law on the Implementation of General Policies, Article 44 (44) of the Constitution.
In establishing the single window process, the subsidiary licensing agencies are required to provide services through the establishment of an authorized representative at the single window location or in cyberspace. The relevant instructions, including procedures and criteria and how to deal with violators, as well as the list of main agencies in issuing licenses in various activities, shall be approved by the working group under Article (62) of this law.
Article 71: In order to improve the efficiency of the administrative system, increase public satisfaction, and provide citizens with easy and rapid access to public and government services, the government may adopt an arrangement whereby all executive agencies, including ministries, government institutions, non-governmental public institutions or bodies, government companies, banks, credit institutions, and insurance companies, and all agencies whose inclusion in the law requires mentioning their names, keep their organizational units or parts that are required to provide services to the public active and ready to serve in an appropriate and possible manner, whether in person or virtually, by adjusting the working hours of human resources, and by using existing facilities around the clock or in two shifts.
Note 1: The list of examples of organizations subject to this article and the manner, method, and implementation details are in accordance with the instructions prepared and announced by the Vice President and the Vice President for Management and Human Capital Development.
Note 2: Recruiting any human resources, expanding the organization, and creating new facilities and buildings in the implementation of the duties of this article is prohibited.
Article 72 - In order to reduce the risk of price fluctuations, including exchange rate fluctuations, for production-export units, the Central Insurance of Iran, through commercial insurance companies, provides the possibility of providing insurance services related to price fluctuations and exchange rate fluctuations.
Article 73: The government is required to take the necessary legal measures to amend the Labor Law, Social Security Law, and Labor Relations Law by the end of the first year of the program, observing the following:
A- Creating flexibility to resolve disputes between workers and employers and aligning the interests of both parties.
B- Strengthening unemployment insurance as part of workers' social and occupational security with the approach of increasing coverage and expanding unemployment insurance and the unemployed in situations of definite employment.
C- Strengthening the alignment of the interests of workers and employers and the duties of the government with the approach of supporting production and tripartism.
D- Strengthening new working conditions and situations, taking into account technological changes and the specific requirements of producing goods and services.
E - Strengthening labor and employer organizations, ensuring the legal right of union protest for these organizations.
Article 74- In line with the formation of competitive markets and the realization of paragraph (y) of Article (45) of the Law on the Implementation of General Policies of Article 44 (44) of the Constitution, if the company subject to the transfer is being transferred to carry out the subject of activity or to supply production factors, including raw materials, or to sell goods and services, or to the location and composition thereof, or to the rate of supply of production factors, including raw materials, or to sell goods and services, it is necessary to comply with specific regulations by government agencies, government-owned or government-affiliated companies and institutions, before the transfer, all information related to the continuation or non-continuation, as well as possible changes to these requirements, must be disclosed based on the regulations of the Securities and Exchange Organization.
Article 75- In order to exchange views between the government and the private and cooperative sectors and facilitate the economic activities of these sectors, examine and remove business obstacles, and make effective decisions for necessary measures within the framework of existing laws and regulations, and present proposals and appropriate implementation strategies to the relevant authorities, a Government-Private Sector Dialogue Council shall be formed with the following members:
1- Minister of Economic Affairs and Finance (Chairman of the Council)
2- Governor of the Central Bank of the Islamic Republic of Iran
3- Deputy President for Strategic Planning and Supervision
4- Minister of Industries and Mines
5- Minister of Cooperatives
6- Minister of Commerce
7- Minister of Agricultural Jihad
8- Minister of Labor and Social Affairs
9- Minister of Petroleum
10- Minister of Energy
11- Two deputies of the Judiciary, elected by the Head of the Judiciary.
12- Chairman of the Economic Commission of the Islamic Consultative Assembly
13- Chairman of the Planning, Budget and Accounting Commission of the Islamic Consultative Assembly
14- Chairman of the Industries and Mines Commission of the Islamic Consultative Assembly
15- Chairman of the Agriculture, Water and Natural Resources Commission of the Islamic Consultative Assembly
16- President of the Iranian Chamber of Commerce, Industries and Mines
17- Secretary General of the Cooperative Chamber of the Islamic Republic of Iran
18- Mayor of Tehran
19- Eight CEOs of leading private and cooperative companies from different sectors and fields
Note 1- The method of selecting members of Tier (19) and the method of holding meetings, which are held at least once every fifteen days, and presenting proposals and reports are in accordance with the instructions that are prepared and approved in the first meeting of the council.
Note 2: The secretariat of this council, without receiving any assistance from the government, is located at the Iranian Chamber of Commerce, Industries and Mines.
Article 76- The Iranian Chamber of Commerce, Industries and Mines is obliged, in cooperation and presence of the Central Cooperative Chamber of the Islamic Republic of Iran and the Council of Guilds of the country, to identify laws, regulations and circulars that hinder production and investment in Iran and, by continuously soliciting opinions from production and export organizations throughout the country and examining and processing their problems and demands, to continuously prepare reports and proposals and submit them to a committee consisting of two people from each branch, selected by the head of that branch. The said committee is obliged to examine the submitted proposals and to follow up on the necessary legal solutions.
Note 1 - The reports subject to this article include troublesome laws, approvals, and circulars, legal vacuums, incorrect or incomplete implementation of laws, as well as proposals to amend laws and regulations and promote economic security, property rights, and support for investment, production, exports, and employment, and how to reduce the cost price of goods and services in Iran, along with improving the quality and growth of production, and matters related to investment and production, and the optimal use of existing investments and capacities.

Article 78: The government is obligated to purchase the goods and services it needs from domestic producers with priority in order to support domestic production.
Productivity
Article 79- In order to increase the share of productivity in economic growth to one-third by the end of the program and for the purpose of planning, policy-making, guidance, monitoring and evaluating the productivity of all factors of production, including labor, capital, energy, water and soil, the National Productivity Organization of Iran shall be established as a government institution affiliated with the Deputy, using existing facilities, to formulate the country's comprehensive productivity program, including productivity standard indicators and an executive system for productivity promotion, including the distribution of roles and responsibilities in all social, economic and cultural sectors, including government and non-government sectors, as a mandatory program for all the aforementioned sectors, and submit it to the approval of the Council of Ministers. All executive agencies are required to continuously publish productivity changes and their impact on economic growth related to their sector from the second year of the program, and to identify policies and variables affecting productivity growth so that the productivity effect of the aforementioned agencies has an upward growth. The aforementioned policies can include incentive policies for non-governmental sectors and state-owned companies.
A maximum of three percent (3%) of each stage of allocation of spending credits for executive agencies shall be kept in the treasury, and its payment to the agency shall be subject to the submission of the Deputy's approval of compliance with the approvals of this article and other legal obligations related to productivity.
The executive regulations of this article, including the program proposed by the National Productivity Organization of Iran, will be approved by the Council of Ministers.
Employment
Article 80 - The government is allowed to take the following measures in order to create sustainable employment, develop entrepreneurship, reduce regional imbalances, and develop new jobs:
A- Financial support and encouragement of the development of networks, clusters and production chains, creating appropriate links between small, medium and large enterprises (providing targeted assistance) and taking the necessary measures to strengthen the technical, engineering and specialized capabilities, research and development and marketing in small and medium enterprises and developing information and e-commerce centers for them.
B- Removing problems and obstacles to the growth and development of small and medium-sized enterprises and helping them mature and transform into large and competitive enterprises.
C- Expanding home businesses and remote jobs and private and cooperative sector employment projects, especially in areas with higher than average unemployment rates in the country.
D- Financial and legal support and the regulation of incentive policies to transform unorganized household economic activities into organized units.
E - Financial support for the non-governmental sector to develop and expand business, entrepreneurship, technical and vocational, and scientific-applied education.
F- Applying a graduated discount or providing a portion of the insurance premium for employers of workshops that, with the approval or introduction of units of the Ministry of Labor and Social Affairs, are seeking to employ new workers, provided that the unit is newly established or has not experienced a reduction in its workforce in the previous year.
Note - The executive regulations of the above clauses, including the manner, amount, and duration of using the privileges subject to the above clauses, shall be approved by the Council of Ministers within a maximum of six months after the ratification of this law.
G- To ensure continuity of job opportunities, Article (10) of the Law on the Regulation of Part of the Regulations for Facilitating the Modernization of the Country's Industries and Amending Article (113) of the Law on the Third Economic, Social and Cultural Development of the Islamic Republic of Iran shall be implemented during the years of the Fifth Plan.
Currency
Article 81 - In order to regulate the country's foreign exchange obligations:
A- Executive agencies are required to comply with the following:
1- Conduct their foreign exchange operations and transactions through foreign exchange accounts in domestic or foreign banks that they have opened or will open with the approval of the Central Bank. Iranian operating banks are required to provide the services they need at the level of international standards.
2- They must notify the Central Bank of the list of all their foreign currency accounts abroad, so that after approval by this bank, their activities can continue.
B- The government is obliged to:
1- Schedule the payment of foreign debts and obligations, both medium-term and short-term, in such a way that the annual repayments of these debts and obligations, excluding obligations arising from the counter-purchase, at the end of the Fifth Plan do not exceed thirty percent (30%) of the government's foreign exchange earnings in the last year of the Fifth Plan. In using foreign facilities, priority shall be given to long-term facilities.
2- Adjust the amount of foreign obligations and liabilities guaranteed by the government and the Central Bank in the years of the Fifth Plan in such a way that the net present value of the government's debts and liabilities, excluding liabilities arising from contracts subject to paragraph (b) of Article (3) of the Law on Encouragement and Support of Foreign Investment approved on 19/12/1380 (the difference between the present value of debts, obligations of the country and foreign exchange reserves of the Central Bank of the Islamic Republic of Iran) in the last year of the plan does not exceed fifty billion (50,000,000,000) dollars.
3- Present tables of foreign exchange receipts and payments along with annual budget bills for the remaining years of the program.
C- The country's currency system is a "managed float." The exchange rate will be determined with a view to maintaining competitiveness in foreign trade and taking into account domestic and global inflation, as well as macroeconomic conditions, including determining an optimal level of foreign reserves.
Article 82
A- The use of foreign financial facilities by executive agencies during the program is permitted within the framework of annual budget laws. The remaining quotas of approved cross-purchase facilities and foreign financial facilities (finance) related to the third and fourth programs (in compliance with Islamic criteria, including approval by the Expediency Discernment Council) remain in force during the fifth program.
B- Executive agencies are allowed to use the cross-selling method to secure financial resources for their projects, while observing the principle of no guarantee of repayment of obligations created by the government, the Central Bank, and state-owned banks after approval by the Economic Council.
C- Commercial and specialized banks are permitted to provide financial resources for investment projects of non-governmental sectors and municipalities from international sources without government guarantees. The determination of the ratio of foreign currency obligations and liabilities to assets of each bank shall be subject to approval by the Money and Credit Council.
D - In using foreign financial facilities subject to paragraphs (a), (b) and (c) of this article, it is essential for the executive bodies and operating banks to observe the following:
1- All plans of executive agencies, in compliance with the general policies of Article 44 (44) of the Constitution, under the responsibility of the Minister or the highest executive authority of the relevant agency and approved by the Economic Council, must have technical, economic and financial justification and the total costs of their full implementation must not exceed the specified ceilings. The timing of receiving and repaying the facilities of each plan and the extent of its use of domestic construction, considering domestic capacities, facilities and capabilities and compliance with the law "Maximum use of the country's technical and engineering, production, industrial and executive capacity in implementing projects and creating facilities for exporting services approved on 12/12/1375" as well as compliance with environmental conditions in the implementation of each of the plans must be approved by the Economic Council.
2- Users of foreign exchange and financial facilities, including foreign finance and direct loans, must provide the Central Bank with a schedule and amount of obligations created or used, as well as the repayment schedule of their foreign currency debts and obligations, within two months of the conclusion of the contract. After the implementation of the aforementioned plans, a report on the performance of the plan, including the principal and interest of funds received or paid and the remaining obligation, is announced to the Central Bank and the Deputy at quarterly intervals.
Note - Users of direct loans are required to make the necessary coordination with the Central Bank of the Islamic Republic of Iran in establishing a commitment.
D- All foreign transactions and contracts exceeding one million (1,000,000) dollars shall be concluded and concluded only through limited or international tenders, with advertisements placed in widely circulated newspapers and domestic and foreign electronic media, in compliance with the relevant laws, including the law “Maximum Use of the Country’s Technical, Engineering, Production, Industrial and Executive Capabilities in Implementing Projects and Creating Facilities for Exporting Services, approved on 12/12/1375”. Exceptions shall be approved by a three-member committee consisting of the Minister of Economic Affairs and Finance, the Deputy of the President for Strategic Planning and Supervision, and the Minister of the relevant Ministry or the highest relevant executive authority. In all tenders, the right to control and inspect the quantity and quality and control the price of all imported goods and projects is reserved for the buyer. The Minister or the highest relevant executive authority is responsible for the proper implementation of this matter. The Central Bank of the Islamic Republic of Iran is only authorized to commit or pay for transactions and contracts that have the approval of the highest authority of the executive bodies, stating that the provisions of this paragraph are complied with.
Article 83
A- The Central Bank is permitted to issue participation bonds and various types of Islamic sukuk in international markets within the framework of the annual budget law to finance government development infrastructure projects.
B- Government companies and municipalities are allowed to issue participation bonds and various types of Islamic sukuk in order to secure foreign exchange resources for their investment projects, after approval by the Ministry of Economic Affairs and Finance and the Central Bank. The principal and interest of these bonds are guaranteed by the aforementioned companies and municipalities.
Article 84 - The National Development Fund, referred to in this article as the Fund, is established with the aim of converting part of the revenues from the sale of oil, gas, gas condensates, and petroleum products into sustainable, productive wealth and economically productive capital, as well as preserving the share of future generations from oil, gas, and petroleum products resources.
The Fund is based in Tehran and will not have any branches in Tehran or other parts of the country. The property and assets of this Fund belong to the Government of the Islamic Republic of Iran. This article is the statute of the Fund.
A- The pillars of the fund are:
1- Board of Trustees
2- Executive Board
3- Supervisory Board
B- The Board of Trustees, as the highest body of the Fund, has the following duties and powers:
1- Leadership, determining policies and guidelines
2- Approving the conditions and procedures for granting facilities for production and investment to the private, cooperative, and non-governmental public sectors.
3- Approving the fund's regulations, strategic plans, annual budget, financial statements, and performance reports.
4- Election of the chairman and other members of the executive board in accordance with clauses (d) and (e)
5- Dismissal of the chairman and members of the executive board upon the proposal of any member of the board of trustees and approval of the board of trustees.
6- Making decisions on referring claims to arbitration and settling claims in accordance with Article 139 of the Constitution.
7- Determining the types of activities that are acceptable and eligible for priority payment of facilities in productive and economically profitable production and service sectors and sub-sectors.
8- Determining the minimum expected rate of return from the fund's resources, as well as the acceptable rate of return for production and investment projects for the payment of facilities, and determining the rate of participation in investment projects in such a way that the average of these rates is not less than the average rate of return on Central Bank deposits in foreign markets.
C- Board of Trustees
The composition of the Board of Trustees is as follows:
1- President (Chairman of the Board of Trustees)
2- Deputy President for Strategic Planning and Supervision (Secretary of the Board of Trustees)
3- Minister of Economic Affairs and Finance
4- Minister of Labor and Social Affairs
5- Minister of Petroleum
6- Governor of the Central Bank of the Islamic Republic of Iran
7- The President of the Iranian Chamber of Commerce, Industries and Mines as an observer member without voting rights.
8- The President of the Iranian Chamber of Cooperatives as an observer member without voting rights.
9- Two representatives from the Economic, Planning, Budget and Accounting Commissions elected by the Islamic Consultative Assembly.
10- Attorney General of the country
Note 1- Meetings of the Board of Trustees shall be held at least twice a year.
Note 2: The meetings of the Board of Trustees are formalized with at least two-thirds of the voting members, and its decisions are made with at least five votes.
Note 3: The heads of the executive board and the supervisory board may participate in the meetings of the board of trustees without voting rights.
Note 4: Any decision of the Board of Trustees regarding the approval of the balance sheet and financial statements of the Fund, as well as the appointment of the Chairman and members of the Executive Board, shall be published in the official newspaper of the country and in one of the widely circulated newspapers selected by the Board of Trustees.
Note 5 - The agenda, date, and location of the Board of Trustees meetings are determined by the Secretary of the Board of Trustees and sent to the members of the board at least fifteen days before the meeting.
Note 6 - The budget of the Fund headquarters is subject to approval by the Board of Trustees.
D- In order to manage the affairs of the Fund within the framework of the provisions of the statute and the resolutions of the Board of Trustees, the Executive Board shall consist of five knowledgeable, experienced and reputable individuals in economic, legal, financial, banking and planning matters with at least ten years of relevant experience and a master's degree, elected by the Board of Trustees and appointed by decree of the President, and shall have the following duties and powers:
1- Proposing acceptable activities that are eligible for priority payment of facilities in productive and service sectors and sub-sectors with appropriate economic returns to the Board of Trustees.
2- Proposing investment cases in international and domestic monetary and financial markets to the Board of Trustees.
3- Determining the framework of agency contracts with agent banks and determining the responsibilities and powers of the agent bank within the framework of these contracts.
4- Establishing an internal audit system and establishing an appropriate internal control system.
5- Approving foreign currency and rial financial statements and presenting them to the Board of Trustees.
6- Submitting proposals to the Board of Trustees regarding regulations, conditions and methods of granting facilities.
7- Making decisions on any action in the name of the Fund within the scope of the duties and objectives stated in the statute, except for those decisions about which are explicitly within the scope of the competence of the Board of Trustees or the Chairman of the Executive Board, unless delegated to the Executive Board.
8- Making decisions on all matters placed on the agenda of the Executive Board by the Chairman of the Executive Board within the scope of his authority.
9- Implementation of the resolutions of the Board of Trustees
10- Proposing to the Board of Trustees to refer claims to arbitration, appoint an arbitrator, and settle claims.
11- Opening or closing foreign currency accounts with the Central Bank and introducing authorized signatories from among the members of the Executive Board, the Chairman, and other managers of the Fund.
12- Signing a contract with a reputable international consultant in investment and financial engineering to evaluate, improve, and promote the fund's performance.
13- Other matters referred by the Board of Trustees
Note 1: The dismissal and acceptance of the resignation of members of the Executive Board shall be subject to the approval of two-thirds of the voting members of the Board of Trustees.
Note 2- The members of the Executive Board must be full-time and not have any economic activity or other job except teaching. The members of the Executive Board are subject to the provisions of Article One Hundred and Forty-Two (142) of the Constitution.
Note 3: The term of office of the Executive Board is five years and the re-election of members is not prohibited.
Note 4- All securities, checks, promissory notes, bills of exchange, contracts and other binding documents will be valid if signed by two of the three members of the Executive Board, who are appointed by this Board, along with the Fund's seal.
Note 5 - The decisions of the Executive Board are effective with at least three votes.
E- The Chairman of the Board of Directors, who is the highest executive officer of the Fund, is elected from among the members of the Board of Directors by the Board of Trustees and appointed by decree of the President. The Chairman of the Board of Directors has the following duties and powers:
1- Notifying and issuing orders to implement the decisions of the Executive Board and supervising their proper implementation.
2- Setting the agenda and conducting the meetings of the Executive Board
3- Managing the fund within the framework of the approvals of the Executive Board and the Board of Trustees.
4- Preparing and organizing executive plans and programs within the scope of the Fund's activities.
5- Preparing and organizing the fund's program, budget, financial statements, and draft reports from the Executive Board to the Board of Trustees.
6- Managing the internal affairs of the Fund, employing human resources, and meeting the Fund's current and administrative expenses.
7- Preparing and organizing the fund's performance report to be presented to the board of directors at least once every three months.
8- Representing the Fund before third parties and all domestic and foreign authorities, including judicial, administrative, registration and similar ones, with the right to delegate power of attorney to others, even on a recurring basis.
9- Filing or defending lawsuits or complaints related to the affairs of the Fund, including legal and criminal matters, with all powers related to litigation matters, except for the right to conciliation and referring the matter to arbitration.
10- Other matters referred by the Executive Board
F- In order to ensure the achievement of the Fund's objectives and continuously monitor its current operations and prevent any possible deviation from the provisions of the statute, guidelines and policies, the Supervisory Board shall be formed by the Chairman of the Court of Accounts, the Chairman of the National Audit Office, and the Chairman of the National Inspection Organization.
Note 1: The supervision of this board will not negate the legal duties of supervisory bodies such as the Court of Accounts and the General Inspectorate of the country.
Note 2: The Supervisory Board will have a Chairman, Vice Chairman, and Secretary who will be elected at the first meeting of the Supervisory Board.
G- Duties of the Supervisory Board:
1- Reviewing the fund's financial statements and reports and preparing case and periodic reports for the Board of Trustees and the Islamic Consultative Assembly;
2- Reviewing the statement of micro-assets, claims, liabilities, and summary of the fund's accounts and their certification for publication in the country's official newspaper;
3- Reviewing the fund's performance in terms of compliance with legal standards, the fund's statutes, and its objectives.
In performing its duties and without interfering in current affairs, this board shall examine all documents, assets and accounts of the fund and may have access to the information, documents and records of the fund as it deems necessary. This board is required to submit its supervisory report to the Board of Trustees and the Islamic Consultative Assembly every six months.
Note - The Supervisory Board may use the services of qualified natural and legal persons to audit the performance of the Fund.
H- Fund resources:
1- At least equivalent to twenty percent (20%) of the resources derived from oil exports (crude oil, gas condensates, gas and petroleum products) in the years of the program and its amount to be determined in the annual budget laws.
2- At least twenty percent (20%) of the barter export value of the above-mentioned items
3- Increasing the share of deposits from the resources of paragraphs (1) and (2) by three percentage points each year.
4- Fifty percent (50%) of the cash balance of the foreign exchange reserve account at the end of 2010 and subsequent years
5- Resources that can be obtained from international money markets with the permission of the Board of Trustees, in compliance with relevant laws.
6- Net profit of the fund during the fiscal year
7- Income from interest on the fund's account balance at the Central Bank, equivalent to the average interest rate on Central Bank deposits in foreign markets, calculated and paid quarterly.
8- Twenty percent (20%) of the resources subject to Subsection (d) of Section (4) of the 2010 Budget Law for the whole country
Note 1: The repayment of the principal and interest on the facilities paid from the National Development Fund will be deposited into the fund's account and reused for the fund's purposes.
Note 2 - The fund's accounts will only be maintained with the Central Bank.
T - Fund Banks:
1- Granting facilities to the private sector, cooperatives, and economic enterprises belonging to public non-governmental institutions for the production and development of technically, financially, and economically justified investments.
2- Granting export facilities for technical and engineering services to Iranian private and cooperative companies that win foreign tenders through their own resources or syndicate facilities.
3- Granting purchasing facilities to buyers of Iranian goods and services in the country's target export markets.
4- Investing in foreign monetary and financial markets
5- Granting facilities to foreign investors, taking into account competitive conditions and appropriate economic returns, is in order to attract and support investment in Iran, in compliance with Article 80 of the Constitution.
6- Providing for the expenses of the fund
Note 1: The use of the Fund's resources for expenditure credits, acquisition of capital assets, and repayment of government debts in any form is prohibited.
Note 2: The granting of facilities subject to this article is only in foreign currency, and investors using these facilities are not allowed to convert foreign currency into rials in the domestic market.
Note 3: All facilities of the Fund will be granted solely through the agency of state and non-state banks.
Y- Other regulations:
1- The share of banks' agency fees is determined by the board of directors in competitive conditions, commensurate with the banks' commission rates and specialized and expert capabilities.
2- The Fund's share of the resources referred to in sub-paragraphs (1) and (2) of paragraph (h) of this article shall be deposited monthly by the Central Bank into the Fund's account and settled at the end of the fiscal year, subject to the determination of the resources.
3- Disbursing facilities from the Fund's resources without confirming the technical, economic, financial justification and the applicant's eligibility by the operating bank is prohibited and will be considered illegal appropriation of public property. The evaluation of technical, economic and financial justification reports and the determination of the adequacy of the return on investment plans, taking into account the risk factor, to an extent not less than the expected facility interest rate announced by the Board of Trustees, is the responsibility of the operating bank and serves as a guarantee for the operating bank to repay the principal and interest on the facility to the Fund.
The fund provides the resources required for investment projects in the agricultural, water, and natural resources sectors to investors in the sector through the operating bank or the Agricultural Investment Development Support Fund in foreign currency and with a lower expected profit.
4- The total amount of facilities allocated from the Fund's resources through the agency of banks to non-governmental public institutions and organizations and subsidiaries and affiliated companies should not exceed twenty percent (20%) of the Fund's resources.
Note 1: For the purposes of this article, institutions and companies are considered non-public if at least eighty percent (80%) of their shares or partnership interests are owned by natural persons, directly or through legal entities.
Institutions and companies whose absolute majority of shares belong to public and non-profit institutions, such as endowments, insurance and pension funds, and public charitable institutions, are, for the purposes of this article, considered institutions and companies affiliated with non-governmental public institutions.
Note 2: Economic enterprises, regardless of the type of ownership, in which more than twenty percent (20%) of the members of their board of directors are appointed by government officials, are considered government entities for the purposes of this article and payments from the Fund's resources are prohibited.
Note 3: The Fund is required to consider regional balance in distributing resources between economic sectors and provinces.
5- The fund's fiscal year is from the first of Farvardin each year to the end of Esfand of the same year.
6- The Fund shall be subject to this Statute and the laws of non-governmental public institutions and organizations and monetary and foreign exchange policies in administrative, employment, financial and transactional matters. The necessary regulations shall be approved by the Council of Ministers upon the proposal of the Board of Trustees of the Fund and shall be implemented in accordance with Article 138 of the Constitution.
7- The Chairman of the Executive Board of the National Development Fund is required to report the amount of resources and expenditures and the location of the fund's expenditures to the Board of Trustees, the Supervisory Board, and the Islamic Consultative Assembly every six months.
8- In the event of encountering a violation or crime in the implementation of the provisions of this article, the Supervisory Board shall notify the competent authorities, as appropriate.
9- The balance sheet and profit and loss account of the fund, along with the report and opinion of the supervisory board, must be submitted to the board of trustees at least fifteen days before the meeting.
10- Issuance of any guarantee and payment of any facilities from the fund's resources shall be carried out only through operating banks, whether governmental or non-governmental, and the Agricultural Sector Investment Development Support Fund.
11- Changes to the statutes and dissolution of the fund are only possible with the approval of the Islamic Consultative Assembly.
Article 85 - The activity of the foreign exchange reserve account derived from oil revenues, subject to Article (1) of the Law on the Fourth Economic, Social and Cultural Development Plan of the Islamic Republic of Iran, approved on 3/6/2004, shall continue with the following amendments and changes:
A- Revenues from oil exports, including (crude oil and gas condensates) in cash and barter, and government revenue from the export of petroleum products and net gas exports in 1389 and subsequent years, after deducting the foreign exchange costs foreseen in the tables of the country's general budget law and after deducting the share of the National Development Fund, are deposited into the foreign exchange reserve account.
B- Financing the government's general budget deficit resulting from the reduction in foreign exchange resources from the export of crude oil, gas, and gas condensates compared to the figures predicted in the annual budget laws from the foreign exchange reserve account, provided that it cannot be financed from other sources, is permitted with the approval of the Islamic Consultative Assembly.

Note 1- The establishment and registration of monetary and credit institutions such as banks, credit institutions, credit cooperatives, Qard-ol-Hasanah funds, exchange offices, and leasing companies, as well as the registration of changes to the aforementioned institutions, is only possible by obtaining a license from the Central Bank of the Islamic Republic of Iran.
Note 2: All natural and legal persons, both governmental and non-governmental, such as the National Deeds and Real Estate Registration Organization, the Islamic Republic of Iran Police Force, and the Ministry of Cooperation are required to comply with the provisions of this article.
Note 3: Based on legal regulations, the Central Bank of the Islamic Republic of Iran has the authority to revoke professional qualifications, revoke licenses, and sentence violators active in the monetary field to pay fines.
In the event of professional disqualification, members of the board of directors and managing directors of banks and credit institutions shall be relieved of their respective responsibilities by the Central Bank of the Islamic Republic of Iran.
The continued tenure of the relevant managers is considered illegal interference with public funds and property.
The amount stated in paragraph (2) of Article (44) of the Monetary and Banking Law of the country approved in 1351 shall be increased to two hundred million rials and shall be adjusted every three years based on the growth of the consumer price index of goods and services officially announced by the Council of Ministers upon the proposal of the Central Bank.
Note 4: The appointment of the CEO and board of directors of state-owned banks is possible after their professional qualification is issued by the Central Bank. These individuals must have at least a relevant bachelor's degree. Also, two-thirds of the board of directors must have at least ten years of work experience in the banking system.
Article 97- In accordance with paragraph (23) of the general policies of the Fifth Plan announced by the Supreme Leader, the Monetary and Credit Council is required to carry out the following during the years of the plan:
A- Reforming the executive, accounting, and financial procedures in the banking system in accordance with the goals and provisions of interest-free banking.
B- Promoting the use of new Islamic financing instruments such as Sukuk to assist in Islamic financing of the country's banks.
C- Reforming the structure of the Central Bank to strengthen supervision over monetary and financial institutions in order to achieve the country's macroeconomic goals.
D- Increasing transparency and healthy competition in the provision of banking services in order to reduce the cost of banking services through the following methods:
1- Require banks to comply with the standards set by the Central Bank in presenting financial reports and improving the capital adequacy ratio and reporting suspicious transactions to the Central Bank.
2- Establishing a strong monitoring system to identify unconventional activities of banks when manipulating interest rates on deposits and facilities in specific ways.
E - Strengthening payment systems
Article 98- The contracts listed in Chapter 3 of the Law on Interest-Free Banking Operations shall be supplemented by Islamic contracts of Istisna'a, Murabaha, and the purchase of debt. The implementing regulations of this article shall be approved by the Council of Ministers.
Capital market
Article 99- In order to enhance the position of the capital market in the country's economy and organize the organized securities market, the following measures shall be taken. Other organizational measures shall be in accordance with Article (4) of the Securities Market Law of the Islamic Republic of Iran, approved in 2005.
A- All persons who have so far issued securities are required to register them with the Securities and Exchange Organization within a maximum of six months after the notification of this law. Failure to register is considered a violation, but it will not prevent the issuer of securities from fulfilling its legal obligations. Issuers of securities registered with the aforementioned organization must publish their financial information in accordance with the arrangements stipulated in Article (45) of the Securities Market Law of the Islamic Republic of Iran.
B- Transactions in securities registered with the organization are only possible on licensed stock exchanges and over-the-counter markets, in compliance with the trading regulations of each of them, as applicable, and transactions in the aforementioned securities are invalid without compliance with the above arrangements.
C- After trading securities registered with the organization that are traded on one of the stock exchanges or over-the-counter markets, and commodities traded on commodity exchanges, settlement and clearing operations (transfers) are carried out after trading in central securities depositories and settlement companies.
D- Records of securities transactions and their traders must be recorded and maintained in a manner that allows for the reconstruction of transactions. The implementing instructions for this paragraph shall be prepared and announced by the Securities and Exchange Organization no later than two months after the promulgation of the law.
E- Members of the board of directors and the managing director of all legal entities that have been established under one of the titles of financial institutions subject to the Securities Market Law of the Islamic Republic of Iran or the Law on the Development of New Financial Instruments and Institutions in order to facilitate the implementation of the policies of Article Forty-Four (44) of the Constitution and have been registered with the Companies Registration Authority throughout the country, as well as those legal entities whose main activity is a financial institution, are required to register the financial institution under their management with the Securities and Exchange Organization. Refusal to register a financial institution is considered a violation, and the activities of their managers are subject to paragraph (1) of Article (49) of the Securities Market Law of the Islamic Republic of Iran. The State Documents and Real Estate Registration Organization is required to register financial institutions only with the permission of the Securities and Exchange Organization and to refrain from registering financial institutions without a license. Any changes in the articles of association and capital of financial institutions are also subject to the approval of the Securities and Exchange Organization in accordance with the relevant regulations. The professional qualification of managers of financial institutions is confirmed based on guidelines approved by the Supreme Council of the Securities and Exchange Commission.
F- Commodities that are accepted and traded on commodity exchanges as organized, regulated and regulated markets are offered and traded in accordance with the laws and regulations governing those exchanges. The offering, trading, settlement and final delivery of the aforementioned commodities are not subject to the laws and regulations relating to unorganized commodity markets.
G- All natural and legal persons who are or will be elected as managers of financial institutions, self-regulatory organizations and securities issuers subject to Article (13) of the Law on the Development of New Financial Instruments and Institutions in order to facilitate the implementation of the general policies of Article Forty-Four (44) of the Constitution are required to submit their identity documents and all documents and records related to their professional and educational backgrounds to the Securities and Exchange Organization for registration in the relevant managers’ database. The Securities and Exchange Organization is required to register the characteristics and educational and experience backgrounds of the aforementioned managers in the aforementioned database and publish their performance in terms of compliance with the laws and regulations governing the securities market and their related violations and criminal records during their management in financial institutions and securities issuers based on the executive arrangements approved by the Supreme Council of the Securities and Exchange. The publication of the names of managers along with their backgrounds is possible after approval by the competent authority.
Article 100- In implementation of Article (34) of the Law on the Implementation of General Policies of Article Forty-Four (44) of the Constitution, those recipients of equity shares who pay the difference in the remaining installments of the price of their received shares before the end of their installment period or through provincial investment companies can enjoy a discount on the price of shares for the remaining periods. The discount instructions and the method of transferring shares shall be approved by the Transfer Board upon the proposal of the Privatization Organization.
Note 1- Payment of any dividends on the aforementioned shares is prohibited up to the limit of the overdue installments.
Note 2: After approval by the Transfer Board, the Privatization Organization may acquire the collateral shares held by it to collect the deferred installments, in accordance with the six-month period after the legally prescribed deadline and informing the provincial investment companies.
Business
Article 101- In order to properly regulate the market, increase the level of competition, promote the efficiency of the distribution network, and make the process of distributing goods and services transparent, the government may:
A- To submit a bill to organize non-governmental units for the distribution of goods by the end of the second year of the program in a way that takes into account the standards required for distribution activities, the government's supervisory and management tools, and the needs of competitive policies, and reduces the price difference between the producer and the consumer.
B- Regulate unlicensed business units and determine and collect the tax rate and public services of water, electricity, gas, telephone and municipal services of such units until they obtain a business license based on a regulation approved by the Council of Ministers, up to twice the approved tax rate and tariff as a fine. This ruling does not prevent the implementation of other enforcement guarantees stipulated in the relevant laws.
C- Take steps to develop the national classification and services system of the Iranian Goods and Services Identification Code (Iran Code) in order to create a coherent and integrated system for exchanging information related to goods and services, and oblige all suppliers of goods and services to obtain and update this identifier (code) and oblige relevant executive bodies to use it.
D- In compliance with the Law on the Implementation of General Policies, Article 44 (44) of the Constitution, limit pricing to public and exclusive goods and services, and subsidized and essential basic goods.
Note 1: The government is required to submit to the Economic Council for approval the criteria for determining essential, exclusive goods and public services, as well as the list and criteria for determining the prices of these goods and services, within three months after the ratification of this law, upon the proposal of a working group consisting of representatives of the Ministry of Commerce, the Deputy Minister and relevant ministries.
Note 2: In implementing this clause, if the selling price of goods or services is less than the fair price of the day, the difference between the fair price of the day and the day must be determined simultaneously and paid from the government's credits and resources in the year of implementation or offset from the debt of the relevant agency to the government.
Article 102: In line with the implementation of the general policies of Article 44 (44) of the Constitution, the Ministry of Commerce is authorized to delegate the executive affairs of market regulation, including the supply, storage, distribution, inspection, and pricing of goods and services, to production, distribution, and service trade unions, unions, cooperatives, and non-governmental organizations for consumer protection.
Note 1 - The organizations, unions, and cooperatives subject to this article will be accountable and responsible for compensation for damages in response to the delegated authorities within the framework of the executive regulations that are approved by the Council of Ministers upon the proposal of the Ministry of Commerce and the Deputy.
Note 2: The Ministry of Commerce is authorized to take steps to compensate for domestic needs by granting import permits without transferring foreign exchange, as necessary.
Note 3: The law prohibiting the import of unnecessary goods during the Fifth Plan shall not apply to cigarettes. The government is required to take the necessary measures, including reducing consumption, producing authentic foreign cigarettes with the original brand name within the country, subject to the commitment of the said company not to import, in such a way that by the end of the plan, a balance of domestic production and consumption is established.
Article 103: The establishment of non-tariff and non-technical barriers to imports during the program years is prohibited, except in cases where compliance with Islamic law requires it. In the event of the existence of such barriers, the government is obliged to take steps to remove them by imposing equivalent tariff rates.
Note: Agricultural products are not subject to this prohibition.
Article 104
A- In order to promote the participation of enterprises and increase the share of organizations, networks, clusters, consortiums, export management companies, and large export companies with product diversification, the government is authorized to provide its direct and indirect assistance, facilities, incentives, and support in the field of non-oil exports through these organizations.
The organizations subject to this article will be accountable and responsible for compensation for the delegated authorities.
The executive regulations of this clause shall be prepared by the Ministry of Commerce and the Deputy Minister and approved by the Council of Ministers.
B- Any taxes and duties on the export of non-oil goods and services are prohibited during the program.
Note 1: The list of petroleum products shall be determined by the Economic Council within six months from the date of approval of this law.
Note 2: In order to protect the country's resources, the government is authorized to impose and collect special duties on the export of raw materials or materials with low added value.
Note 3- The amount of duties subject to this article is according to a table that decreases in proportion to the increase in the share of domestic value added. The list and relevant duties of these goods and the aforementioned table shall be approved by the Economic Council. These duties shall be collected at least three months after their imposition and public announcement.
C- The export of goods and services is exempt from obtaining any license, with the exception of mandatory standards and certificates customary in international trade requested by buyers.
Note - The government is allowed to implement, taking into account national interests, the necessary legal arrangements for amending the Export and Import Regulations Law, including the provision of export incentives as stipulated in this law and other relevant laws that are permanent, by the end of the first year of the program.
D- The government should adopt and implement effective protective, compensatory, and anti-dumping measures and actions in cases where a product is imported into the country under unusual conditions and with significant privileges.
E- Regulation of the domestic market does not result in a ban on exports, and the export of all goods and services is permitted, except for the following:
1- Antiques and cultural heritage objects as determined by the Cultural Heritage Organization.
2- Specific livestock, plant, agricultural and pasture items, and species that have the aspect of preserving genetic reserves or protecting biodiversity, at the discretion of the Ministry of Agricultural Jihad and the Environmental Protection Organization.
Note: Export of goods for which the government pays direct subsidies is permitted only upon the proposal of the relevant agency and approval of the Economic Council. In this case, all exporters are required to obtain a certificate of return of all direct subsidies paid on exported goods from the Ministry of Economic Affairs and Finance before leaving.
F- The government is required to take legal action to prepare and empower the country's economic institutions for membership in the World Trade Organization (WTO), while aligning the country's laws and regulations in the commercial sector with the laws and regulations of regional and international unions, including the World Trade Organization.
Article 105
A- Merger of commercial companies, as long as it does not lead to the creation of concentration and the emergence of monopoly power, is permitted in a unilateral form (the survival of one of the companies - the acquiring company) and bilateral or multilateral merger (the elimination of the legal personality of the merging companies and the creation of a new legal personality - the new company), within the framework of the articles of association of that company in other commercial companies subject to the merger.
The matters subject to this clause do not include matters that are not transferable according to Sharia law.
All rights and obligations, assets, debts and claims of the company or companies subject to the merger are transferred to the company accepting the merger or the new company.
The employees of the companies subject to the merger will be transferred to the acquiring company or the new company. If some employees do not wish to transfer to the acquiring company or the new company, termination benefits will be paid to the employees by the said company in accordance with the provisions of the Labor Law. In the case of surplus labor, the law on regulating part of the regulations for facilitating the modernization of the country's industries and amending Article (113) of the Law on the Third Economic, Social and Cultural Development Program of the Islamic Republic of Iran, approved on 26/5/2003.
The capital of a company resulting from the merger of companies subject to this clause, up to the total capital of the merged companies, is exempt from paying the tax referred to in Article (48) of the Direct Taxes Law approved on 3/12/1366 and its amendments.
B- The government is required to foresee the conditions for preventing the creation of concentration, the exercise of power and monopoly, as well as the useful and permissible scope of mergers, in accordance with Chapter (9) of the Law “Amending Articles of the Fourth Schedule Law and Implementing General Policies of Article Forty-Four (44) of the Constitution.”
Article 106: By the end of the third year of the program, the government is required to reduce the number of low-activity customs entry and exit points by at least thirty percent (30%), while reducing the time for goods clearance.
Note - The government is allowed to equip the remaining ports with modern and innovative technology in order to improve the accuracy and speed of control operations and increase trade.
Article 107- The formation of an economic group with common interests with the participation of two or more natural and legal persons in order to facilitate and expand economic and commercial activity for a limited period and based on a written contract after registration with the Companies Registration Authority in the form of a civil company and its related rules and conditions, and in compliance with Islamic standards and the principle of preventing harm to others and preventing monopoly, is permitted.
Note 1: Changes in the scope of the managers' authority in the contract are not enforceable against third parties, and the group members are jointly and severally liable for paying the group's debts from their personal assets, unless otherwise agreed upon with the third parties to the contract.
Note 2- Operations related to commercial offices, inspections and liquidations shall be carried out in accordance with Article (6) and the provisions of Chapter 11 of the Commercial Code and Articles (151) and (152) of the bill amending part of the Commercial Code approved in 1968.
Note 3: The death, imprisonment, or legal prohibition of one of the natural persons or the dissolution or bankruptcy of one of the legal persons shall result in the dissolution of the group unless otherwise stipulated in the contract for the formation of the economic group.
Article 108: The purchase of goods or services through the stock exchange without observing the provisions and formalities of the law on holding tenders by executive bodies is not prohibited.
Article 109- Importation of any type of goods into the country is subject to compliance with technical, safety and health quarantine regulations that are notified to the customs by the Ministry of Commerce after approval by the relevant competent authority. Compliance with these regulations is mandatory for the clearance of imported goods, including abandoned goods, confirmed confiscated goods, goods without owners, fugitive owners and discovered contraband.
Note - Goods that do not comply with the criteria of this article will be returned abroad at the expense of the original owner of the goods (if any) or his heirs. Otherwise, if they are partially or completely incapable of secondary use and this act of extravagance is not prohibited, they will be destroyed. The Organization for the Possession of Property and the Headquarters for the Implementation of the Decree of the Imam (may Allah have mercy on him) are not authorized to take possession of, sell, or offer them domestically, except in cases where they can be used for secondary use and secondary use is assured.
Article 110- In line with paragraph (9) of the general policies for reforming the consumption pattern in order to reform the bread consumption pattern, including reforming the production structure, improving the process, developing the production of quality, hygienic breads that are in line with the taste and culture of the people, maintaining strategic wheat reserves, regulating wheat and flour trade exchanges with the aim of regulating the domestic market and using trained human resources in the bread production process and in a competitive environment, and carrying out affairs through the non-governmental sector, including private and cooperatives, policy-making, goals, and supervision are the responsibility of the Economic Council, and the Ministry of Commerce is designated as the body in charge of regulating the bread market, which performs its duties based on the approvals of the aforementioned Council. The investigation of violations, crimes, and sanctions related to wheat, flour, and bread is only related to subsidy cases and is carried out in accordance with Chapter Eight of the Trade Union System Law approved on 12/24/1382, and conflicting general and specific laws are repealed. In other relevant cases, only the latter law prevails.
Foreign investment
Article 111- The government shall take legal action regarding the following matters within a maximum of six months after the ratification of this law, in accordance with the current needs and the necessities of achieving the program's goals and in order to increase efficiency:
A- Strengthening and equipping the "Iran Investment and Economic and Technical Assistance" organization
B- Identifying and amending laws and regulations as necessary and determining and applying incentives in accordance with current needs and international competition to attract more foreign investment.
Free zones
Article 112- In order to organize free and special economic zones and play their effective role in achieving the goals of the twenty-year vision document of the system, implementing integrated management and creating appropriate economic growth in these zones, effectively linking and interacting the national economy with the global economy, and presenting a national development model in various sectors:
A- The managers of the free zone organizations, representing the government, are considered the highest authority in the zone and all the duties, powers and responsibilities of the government executive bodies located in these zones, with the exception of defense and security institutions, are their responsibility. The free zone organizations are governed exclusively based on the Law on the Management of Free Commercial-Industrial Zones and its subsequent amendments and the Labor Law.
Note 1: The assignment of duties, authorities, and responsibilities of the agencies under the supervision of the Supreme Leader shall be done with his consent.
Note 2: The governor's authority regarding the approvals of the Islamic city and village councils in free zones is delegated to the director of the free zone organization.
B- Goods produced or processed in free and special economic zones, when entering other parts of the country, are considered domestically produced and exempt from import duties in proportion to the total value added and the value of domestic raw materials and parts used in them.
Note 1: Foreign raw materials and intermediate goods used in production are considered domestic raw materials and goods, subject to payment of import duties.
Note 2: Raw materials and foreign parts used in goods produced or processed in free and special economic zones that were previously sent to the zone from other parts of the country and used in the production or processing of a product imported into the country are considered raw materials and are exempt from paying import duties.
C- The relevant port fees collected from ships and vessels for port services in accordance with current laws, if these ports are established by the private sector, cooperatives and free commercial-industrial zones within the free zone, shall be collected by the relevant free zone organizations. Free zones are authorized to register and clear ships in accordance with current and international laws.
D- Exchanges of goods between free zones and abroad, as well as other free zones, are exempt from all duties (with the exception of duties subject to Article (10) of the Law on the Administration of Free Trade and Industrial Zones of the Islamic Republic of Iran, approved on 7/6/1372), taxes and import duties.
E - In order to expand scientific connections with internationally recognized educational and research centers and institutions, it is permitted to establish representative offices of domestic and recognized foreign universities in accordance with the announcements of the relevant ministries, and to establish private universities in free trade-industrial and special economic zones, in compliance with the relevant laws.
F- The water area of ​​the free trade-industrial zones, taking into account security and defense considerations that are approved by the General Staff, is determined at a distance of eight hundred meters from the land territory of the free zones and enjoys the privileges of the law "How to Manage Free Trade-Industrial Zones of Iran" approved on 7/6/1372 and its subsequent amendments.
G- In order to eliminate poverty from the face of free trade zones, free trade zone organizations are required to allocate at least one percent (1%) of the collection of import and export duties on goods and services in these zones to the deprived and needy indigenous people of these zones through support institutions.
Commercial insurance
Article 113: Partnerships between foreign insurance companies and domestic commercial insurance companies, with priority given to the non-governmental sector, are permitted for the purpose of establishing a joint commercial insurance company in Iran, attracting foreign capital by domestic insurance companies, and establishing and operating branches and agencies of foreign commercial insurance companies, subject to compliance with the general policies of Article (44) of the Constitution and relevant laws.
Article 114: The government is allowed to reform the tariff supervision system and gradually reduce the compulsory reinsurance premium in the commercial insurance industry, and to formulate and establish an alternative integrated supervisory system including regulations on financial supervision, prohibition of monopolies and facilitation of competition, solvency, and rating of insurance institutions by the end of the third year of the program at the latest.
Article 115
A- The imposition and collection of any taxes on commercial insurance (insurance premiums and reinsurance premiums) is limited to cases specified in laws approved by the Islamic Consultative Assembly.
B- The Ministry of Economic Affairs and Finance is required to apply third-party insurance to the driver as well as the passenger.
Article 116- The provision of insurance by executive agencies exclusively with the Iran Insurance Joint Stock Company, the subject of Article (30) of the Law on the Establishment of the Central Insurance of Iran and Insurance approved in 1971, shall be repealed during the years of the program.
Reducing dependence on oil
Article 117- In order to completely cut off the dependence of expenditure credits on oil and gas revenues by increasing the share of public revenues in securing government expenditure credits, steps should be taken to:
A- At the end of the program, the tax-to-GDP ratio should reach at least ten percent (10%), provided that direct tax rates do not increase and tax bases are expanded.
Note 1: Establishing any discount, preference, or exemption from taxes and input duties, in addition to what has been approved in the relevant laws, is prohibited for natural and legal persons, including the organizations subject to Article (222) of this law, during the years of implementation of the program.
Note 2: The government is required to fully implement the Value Added Tax Law and increase the rate of this tax by one percentage point from the first year of the annual plan, so that at the end of the plan its rate reaches eight percent (8%). The municipalities and villages’ share of the aforementioned rate is determined as three percent (3%) (as fees) and the government’s share is five percent (5%).
B- The ratio of public revenues, excluding oil and gas revenues, to expenditure appropriations shall increase by an average of ten percent (10%) annually.
C- Government spending credits should increase annually by a maximum of two percent (2%) less than the inflation rate.
Note - It is prohibited to finance the government budget deficit by borrowing from the Central Bank and the banking system.
Article 118- In order to financially support non-governmental sectors and develop the activities of these sectors, executive agencies or state-owned companies are permitted, within the framework of annual budget laws, to provide credit for the acquisition of their capital assets or domestic investment resources in the form of managed funds or facility interest subsidies, based on contracts concluded, to operating banks, so that they can be made available to qualified applicants from non-governmental sectors within the framework of relevant legal regulations.
The executive regulations of this article shall be approved by the Council of Ministers upon the joint proposal of the Ministry of Economic Affairs and Finance, the Deputy Minister, and the Central Bank of the Islamic Republic of Iran.
Tax system reform
Article 119: The government is required to take the following measures in order to target tax exemptions and clarify financial support:
A- Replacing the zero-rated tax credit policy with legal tax exemptions
B- Registering the aforementioned tax exemptions as a collective expense in the annual budget laws.
Note 1: Natural and legal persons subject to the aforementioned legal exemptions are not subject to tax.
Note 2: Submitting a tax return on time is a condition for enjoying any tax exemption.
Article 120- The Tax Affairs Organization is required to complete the taxpayers’ database, including income and expense information for natural persons, by the end of the program, and for legal persons, by the end of the second year of the program at the latest. All executive agencies and public and relevant institutions that have the information required for the aforementioned database are required to provide this information to the Tax Affairs Organization in accordance with the instructions for exchanging information while maintaining confidentiality, which are prepared by the Ministry of Economic Affairs and Finance in the first year of the program and approved by the Council of Ministers.
Note 1 - The Iranian Tax Affairs Organization is required to use modern technologies such as e-mail services, electronic signatures, or other methods from the second year of the program's implementation in order to facilitate the receipt of tax returns and other documents that taxpayers are required to submit, as well as the notification of tax documents to all or some of the aforementioned taxpayers.
Note 2 - The Fiscal Affairs Organization is required to establish an intermediate electronic certificate issuance center in cooperation with government centers for issuing root electronic certificates and based on the regulations of that center by the end of the second year of the program at the latest.
Article 121- In order to ensure transparency in economic transactions and to determine the income of taxpayers and value-added tax, the Ministry of Commerce is required, in coordination with the National Tax Affairs Organization and the National Guilds Council, to require business owners to use point-of-sale (POS) systems on a priority basis by the end of the second year of the program.
The equivalent of the costs incurred for the purchase, installation and operation of the cash register system (vending machine) by the owners of the aforementioned businesses can be deducted from the taxable income of the aforementioned taxpayers (in the first year of using the aforementioned systems). Failure of business owners to use the cash register system (vending machine) in any year will result in deprivation of tax exemptions stipulated in the law for the relevant year.
The Tax Affairs Organization is required to gradually and based on priority determine those business owners who are required to use the point-of-sale system (mechanized sales), and to announce it by the month of Shahrivar each year through written and official notification to the relevant trade union and publication in one of the widely circulated newspapers and the official newspaper of the country, and to implement it from the beginning of Farvardin of the following year.
Article 122- Managers of non-governmental legal entities, collectively or individually, shall be jointly and severally liable with the legal entity for the payment of income tax on legal entities, as well as taxes that legal entities are required to deduct, collect or remit under the provisions of the Direct Taxes and Value Added Tax Law, and which relate to the period of their management. This liability shall not prevent guarantors from approaching the legal entity.
Article 123- Value-added tax and pollution taxes on production units subject to clause (a) and note (1) of Article (38) of the Value-Added Tax Law shall be distributed among the municipalities and villages of the same city in proportion to the population. If pollution from large production units (fifty or more people) spreads to more than one city in a province, pollution taxes shall be distributed among the affected cities in proportion to their impact, based on the policies announced by the Deputy Minister of Planning, the governors of the relevant cities, the Director General of Environment, and the Director General of Tax Affairs of the province.
If the counties affected by pollution are located in two or more provinces, members of the pollution toll distribution committee for large units (fifty or more people) consisting of a representative of the deputy, planning deputies of the relevant provinces, a representative of the Environmental Protection Organization, and a representative of the General Tax Administration will distribute pollution tolls based on the policies announced by the deputy.
Cooperative development
Article 124- In order to develop the cooperative sector and increase its share to twenty-five percent (25%) of the national economy by the end of the program, with the approach of creating employment, expanding social justice, and empowering the middle and low-income segments of society, the government shall take the following measures, in compliance with the duties and authorities stipulated in the law amending the articles of the Fourth Program Law and implementing the general policies of Article Forty-Four (44) of the Constitution:
A- Increasing the share of cooperatives to fifteen percent (15%) in the country's money market by the fourth year of the program by providing the necessary facilities for issuing licenses for the activities of financial and monetary institutions and cooperative banks.
B- Annually increasing the capital of the Cooperative Development Bank and the Cooperative Investment Guarantee Fund in order to enhance their share in the growth of the cooperative sector from part of the financial resources of Article (29) of the Law on Amending Articles of the Fourth Plan Law and Implementing the General Policies of Article Forty-Four (44) of the Constitution in the form of annual budgets in order to provide banking facilities and cover the risks of national comprehensive cooperatives, with priority given to agricultural, rural and nomadic cooperatives.
C- The Ministry of Cooperatives shall have the governance duties and the Central Cooperative Chamber of the Islamic Republic of Iran and other cooperative organizations shall have the management duties of the cooperative sector. The executive regulations of this clause shall be prepared with the joint proposal of the Ministry of Cooperatives and the Central Cooperative Chamber of the Islamic Republic of Iran and shall be approved by the Council of Ministers within a maximum of three months after the ratification of this law. Any interference of the government in the executive, management, assemblies and elections of cooperative chambers is prohibited. The provisions of this clause do not negate the legal supervisory duties of the Ministry of Cooperatives.
Oil and gas
Article 125
A- The Ministry of Oil is authorized to issue exploration, development, and production licenses required to exploit maximum capacities for the development of oil and gas fields and increase protected production while maintaining the production capacity of 2010 up to the ceiling of additional daily production of one million barrels of crude oil and two hundred and fifty million cubic meters of natural gas, with priority given to joint fields, with an emphasis on the development of the South Pars gas field, after approving the technical and economic justification of the plans in the Economic Council and including them in the annual budget laws, as well as exchanging agreements with the Deputy, using the following methods:
1- Using various exploration, development, and production methods in a given period of time in oil and gas fields.
Note - The basic conditions of these methods, while preserving the right of ownership and exercising ownership rights for the government in the first year of the program, will be prepared upon the proposal of the Ministry of Oil and approved by the Economic Council.
2- Facilitating the issuance of rial and foreign currency financial bonds inside and outside the country, in compliance with relevant laws and regulations, without government guarantees.
3- Using the mutual sale method in compliance with the principles and conditions of paragraph (b) of Article (14) of the Fourth Development Plan Law.
Note - The Ministry of Petroleum can take the necessary steps and efforts to conclude development and production contracts from all known joint fields during the program.
B- The government is obliged to prepare the "National Energy Strategy Document" as the upper-level document of the energy sector for a period of twenty-five years, based on the country's twenty-year vision document and the general policies of the system in the energy sector announced by the Supreme Leader, and with full expert support, and submit it to the Islamic Consultative Assembly for approval within a maximum of six months after the approval of the program law.
Note - The Ministries of Oil and Energy are required, in cooperation with other relevant executive agencies, to prepare the "Executive Program of the National Energy Master Plan" within twelve months after the ratification of the National Energy Strategy Document Law and submit it to the Cabinet for approval.
Article 126- The Ministry of Petroleum is authorized to identify and explore as many oil and gas resources as possible throughout the country, as well as to transfer and apply new technologies in exploration operations, in all regions of the country, with the exception of the provinces of Khuzestan, Bushehr, Kohgiluyeh and Boyer-Ahmad, with emphasis on marine and land areas shared with neighbors, where exploration operations related to the risk of the contracting party are carried out and lead to the discovery of a commercially productive field, within the framework of the figures mentioned in Article (125) of this law and within the framework of the annual budgets of the whole country and the approval of the Economic and Trade Council of the agreement with the Deputy, to proceed with the conclusion of joint purchase and sale contracts for the exploration and development of new fields through holding tenders and to select the contracting party in accordance with legal criteria. Exploration costs, both direct and indirect, shall be included in the aforementioned concluded contract and shall be reimbursed along with development costs from the sale of the products produced by the same field.
The issued licenses have a limited time and are determined in each case by the Ministry of Petroleum and can be extended once. If at the end of the exploration phase, a commercial field is not discovered anywhere in the area, the contract will be terminated and the contracting party will not have the right to claim any money.
Article 127- The Ministry of Oil is authorized, within the framework of the annual budget, to complete the two gas condensate refineries, "Persian Gulf Star" and "Fars", with a nominal capacity of 360,000 and 120,000 barrels respectively, in the form of investments or facilities in the form of managed funds, from the internal resources of subsidiaries and external resources, preferably with the participation of the private and cooperative sectors, after approval by the Economic Council.

Article 128- The Ministry of Oil is authorized to provide facilities from the internal resources of the National Iranian Petroleum Refining and Distribution Company in the form of managed funds for investment by the private and cooperative sectors in projects to complete existing refineries, including optimization and gasoline production, with the approval of the Economic Council.

B- For exploration, development, extraction and production activities of oil and gas by subsidiaries of the Ministry of Petroleum and authorized companies, by adopting the criteria of safe production, issue exploitation licenses without ownership rights to the produced oil and gas and, based on the approved plan, supervise the exploration, development and production operations of the aforementioned companies in terms of production volume and reservoir protection and health, safety and environmental standards.
C- Establish and launch an integrated system for controlling and measuring the production, processing, transmission, refining, distribution, and export of crude oil, natural gas, and petroleum products by the end of the second year of the program at the latest.
D- In order to increase and promote scientific, technological and innovative capacity in the oil industry, allocate one percent (1%) of the annual development plan credits of the subsidiaries during the program to create the capacity (potential) to absorb, develop priority oil, gas and petrochemical technologies and apply them in related industries, and to upgrade existing technologies and localize them and reduce energy intensity, while exchanging agreements with the Deputy Minister, and submit the performance report of this clause annually to the Education and Research Commission of the Islamic Consultative Assembly.
Article 130- In order to increase the recovery rate of the country's reservoirs during the program by one percent (1%), the Ministry of Petroleum is required to prepare a comprehensive plan for the protection and increase of hydrocarbon reservoirs during the first year of the program, prioritizing reservoirs by separating onshore and offshore areas, and to take the necessary measures for its proper implementation.
Article 131: The Ministry of Petroleum is required to develop and implement a comprehensive plan to increase bunkering and ancillary services in the Persian Gulf and the Sea of ​​Oman by at least twenty percent (20%) annually through the non-governmental sector. Article 132- The government shall allocate two percent (2%) of the income from the export of crude oil and natural gas, one-third to oil- and gas-rich provinces, and two-thirds to less developed cities and regions, respectively, for the implementation of development programs in the form of annual budgets.
Electricity
Article 133- In order to diversify the country's energy supply, optimize production and increase the efficiency of power plants, reduce waste, and develop simultaneous production of electricity and heat, Tavanir Company and affiliated and subsidiary companies of the Ministry of Energy are required to:
A- Using the resources obtained from the sale of existing or under construction power plants and other properties and assets of the aforementioned companies, and in compliance with the Law on the Implementation of General Policies, Article Forty-Four (44), they shall pay subsidies for the purchase of electricity from small-scale distributed power producers and subscribers' electricity generation capacities through the conclusion of long-term contracts, as well as convert up to twelve thousand (12,000) megawatts of gas-fired power plants to combined cycle.
Note - If non-governmental sectors wish to convert their existing gas power plants to combined cycle, Tavanir Company and affiliated and subsidiary companies of the Ministry of Energy can pay them facilities in the form of managed funds from the resources referred to in paragraph (a) of this article.
B- Tavanir Company and affiliated and subsidiary companies of the Ministry of Energy are allowed to conclude long-term contracts for the guaranteed purchase of electricity generated from new energy sources and clean energy, with priority for purchase from the private and cooperative sectors. The purchase price of electricity from these power plants, in addition to the energy conversion costs in the competitive market of the national electricity market network, will be approved by the Economic Council, taking into account the average annual value of imported or exported unused fuel, efficiency, non-emission of pollutants, and other matters.
Note - The Ministry of Energy is authorized, in compliance with the Law on the Implementation of General Policies, Article Forty-Four (44), to provide the resources required for this component from the resources obtained from the sale of power plants and other assets, including movable and immovable property, shares and shares of its own company and other subsidiaries and affiliated companies and enterprises, and to provide the necessary arrangements for these power plants to be used in the national electricity network.
C- Support the development of small-scale power plants by the private and cooperative sectors.
D- The Ministry of Energy is authorized to increase the electricity generation capacity up to twenty-five thousand (25,000) megawatts during the program through investment by the public, cooperative and private sectors, both domestic and foreign, or internal resources of subsidiaries, or through conventional investment methods, including build, operate and own (BOO) and build, operate and transfer (BOT).
Note - The share of the private and cooperative sectors of the twenty-five thousand (25,000) megawatts mentioned in this paragraph is at least ten thousand (10,000) megawatts.
E- The Ministry of Energy shall, upon request, issue licenses for the export and transit of electricity from non-subsidized fuel power plants belonging to the private and cooperative sectors.
F- The Ministry of Energy is required, if necessary, while maintaining the responsibility for supplying electricity, to purchase electricity from power plants outside the management of that ministry in order to encourage other domestic institutions to produce as much electricity as possible from these power plants, based on a guideline approved by the Economic Council.
G- If the private sector reduces electricity losses in the transmission and distribution network with its own capital, the Ministry of Energy is required to take action to purchase the recycled energy at a price and under conditions within a period approved by the Economic Council or issue an export license for the same amount.
Note - The executive regulations related to this clause must be approved by the Minister of Energy within three months after the approval of this law.
H- The energy price for units whose annual fuel consumption exceeds two thousand cubic meters of fuel oil equivalent or whose power used exceeds two megawatts, if the criteria and regulations subject to the guidelines of this article, which are approved by the Economic Council, are not observed, will be increased upon providing an appropriate opportunity.
Article 134- In order to save, encourage and support consumers in order to rationalize and improve the energy and electricity consumption pattern, preserve the country's energy reserves and protect the environment, the Ministries of Power, Oil and Industries and Mines are allowed, based on a guideline that will be approved by the Economic Council by the end of the first year of the program at the latest, to apply financial incentives to observe the consumption pattern and optimize energy consumption, produce low-energy and high-standard products. The financial resources required for the implementation of this article are provided from the funds obtained from the implementation of the Law on Targeting Subsidies, internal resources of subsidiaries of the Ministries of Oil, Power, Industries and Mines or the sale of power plants and other assets, including movable and immovable properties, shares and partnership interests of the Ministry of Power and other subsidiaries and affiliated companies and enterprises in the form of annual budgets.
Note 1: All imported energy-intensive products and equipment are subject to compliance with the approved mandatory standard. The Ministry of Commerce and Customs of the Islamic Republic of Iran are obliged to comply with the provisions of this article.
Note 2: Executive agencies are allowed to enter into contracts with private and cooperative sectors to save energy consumption while maintaining and not reducing the previous level of utilization. The contract amount can only be secured and paid from the savings resulting from the reduction in consumption as described in the contract in the form of a reciprocal agreement with the Deputy.
The guidelines for this note will be approved by the Cabinet upon the proposal of the Ministries of Energy and Petroleum and the Deputy Minister.
Note 3: The Ministry of Energy is authorized to equip the country's subscribers with smart meters to manage the country's electricity network load during the program.

Article 139: In order to create infrastructure for the production of wind and solar power plant equipment, develop the use of clean energy, and increase the share of production of these types of energy in the country's energy production portfolio, the government is authorized to provide the basis for the production of up to five thousand megawatts of wind and solar energy during the program, commensurate with the realization of production, by supporting the private and cooperative sectors through managed funds and facility interest subsidies.
Water resources
Article 140- For the purpose of comprehensive (interconnected) management and sustainable development of water resources in the country:
A- In order to create a balance between recharge and withdrawal from groundwater aquifers in all plains of the country, the Ministry of Energy is committed to implementing:
1- Structural and non-structural projects in all plains of the country, with priority given to irrigated plains.
2- Protective measures to prevent and eliminate unauthorized withdrawals from groundwater resources within the framework of the law on determining the status of unlicensed water wells.
3- Installing volumetric meters on all licensed drilled water wells at the expense of their owners.
4- Applying supportive and encouraging policies
5- The implementation of the country's water management system based on the three levels of national, watershed and provincial levels should be carried out in such a way that by the end of the program, considering the precipitation, the negative balance of groundwater tables in these plains compared to the last year of the Fourth Plan will be at least twenty-five percent (25%) (twelve and a half percent (12.5%) from the surface water control area and twelve and a half percent (12.5%) through watershed management and aquifer management) with the participation of the Ministry of Agricultural Jihad and by establishing a system for the proper exploitation of the plains subject to this paragraph, the anticipated goals will be achieved.
B- The Ministry of Agricultural Jihad shall implement balancing projects such as watershed management, aquifer management, canal rehabilitation, improvement and reform of irrigation methods, and establishment of a proper exploitation system for the plains subject to clause "A" in such a way that the anticipated goals are achieved.
C- Importing and exporting water to regional countries and implementing joint water projects with neighboring countries is permitted, subject to national interests and technical, economic, social, and environmental justifications, with the approval of the Economic Council.
D- Manufacturing, industrial, livestock, service and other units that produce wastewater with pollution levels exceeding the permitted national standards are required to implement wastewater collection, treatment and sanitary disposal facilities. Violating units will be fined.
E- The government is required to allocate the necessary funds in the annual budget to accelerate the implementation of plans for the extraction, regulation, transfer, and use of the country's rights from border rivers and shared water resources.
Article 141
A- In order to increase agricultural water productivity, the Ministry of Energy is required to amend existing water allocations and licenses and deliver water volumes to water users in such a way that at least one percent (1%) of the volume of water used for existing uses is reduced annually, especially in plains with a negative groundwater balance, so that the saved water can be used to develop new agricultural lands or for other uses using modern irrigation methods.
B- The Ministry of Energy is required to gradually issue water utilization certificates to all water rights holders and water allocation license holders by the end of the program.
The exchange of these documents is permitted with the notification of the Ministry of Energy (regional water companies) and in compliance with the Law on the Conservation of Agricultural Land Use and Gardens and its subsequent amendments, as well as in compliance with the preservation of the cultivation level in local markets.
C- The government is required to coordinate the preparation and simultaneous implementation of water supply plans and complementary plans, such as the construction of irrigation and drainage networks, the equipping and renovation of downstream lands, and soil protection and watershed management plans in the upstream basins of reservoir dams.
D- The government should expand the main and secondary irrigation and drainage networks of the catchment areas of the constructed dams in such a way that the annual increase is at least twenty percentage points compared to the performance of the previous year's plans.
Article 142- In order to strengthen local and regional markets and pay attention to the value of water:
A- The Ministry of Energy is authorized to purchase extracted water and treated wastewater from investors, both domestic and foreign, and to guarantee surplus water resulting from savings by rightholders in consumption sectors, as well as the costs of water transportation by the non-governmental sector, at an agreed price or by paying a subsidy based on the guidelines approved by the Economic Council.
B- Investment in, ownership, management and exploitation of dams and water supply networks, while preserving all rights of right holders, is permitted by non-governmental public institutions and enterprises, and the cooperative and private sectors, in compliance with the general policies of Article 44 (44) of the Constitution and the relevant law.
C- In order to collect surface water and scattered water waste, as well as to use and collect precipitation during non-agricultural seasons to improve agriculture, the Ministry of Energy is required to rehabilitate known dams in the first two years of the program and, if necessary, construct new dams.
Agriculture
Article 143- In order to maintain production capacity and achieve self-sufficiency in the production of basic agricultural and livestock products, including wheat, barley, corn, rice, oilseeds, sugar beets and sugarcane, white meat, red meat, milk and eggs, reforming consumption patterns based on nutritional standards, expanding industrial and knowledge-based agriculture, providing food security infrastructure, and enhancing the added value of the agricultural sector based on sustainable development considerations, the following measures will be taken annually by seven percent (7%) compared to 2009 during the program:
A- Improving the irrigation efficiency of the sector to at least forty percent (40%) in the last year of the program through the implementation of water and soil infrastructure operations, including equipment and renovation projects, the development of networks, drainage and modern irrigation methods, and the implementation of crop and breeding operations.
B- Delivering the water needed by farmers in volume based on the cultivation pattern of each region and using the participation of the non-governmental sector.
C- Improving the water consumption efficiency index in the agricultural sector and increasing product production per unit of volume consumed.
D- Expanding integrated pest and disease control, optimal use of pesticides, chemical fertilizers, biological materials and animal medicines, as well as biological control and development of organic farming, integrated production management and application of national quality control standards for agricultural products and products in order to cover at least twenty-five percent (25%) of the production level by the end of the program.
E- Outsourcing non-governmental activities and agricultural sector enterprises to the private and cooperative sectors, with emphasis on employing qualified experts from the Agricultural Engineering and Natural Resources Organization and the Veterinary Organization as supervisors or technical advisors to agricultural farms, livestock, animal husbandry and aquaculture units, cooperatives and agricultural sector organizations.

G- Promoting the use of organic and biological fertilizers in the country's farms and gardens at least within the subsidy ceiling of the last year of the Fourth Plan and increasing the consumption of these types of fertilizers to thirty-five percent (35%) of the total fertilizers consumed at the end of the plan.
H- Issuance of ownership documents for all agricultural lands by the National Land and Deeds Registration Organization by the end of the program.
I - Expanding insurance coverage for agricultural products and production factors to at least fifty percent (50%) of production by the end of the program.

Article 144- In order to support the agricultural sector, sustainability of production and development of exports and competitiveness and reduction of the cost price, the Ministries of Industries and Mines, Agricultural Jihad, Commerce, Housing and Urban Development, through coordinated policy-making and planning based on a memorandum of understanding approved by the Deputy, shall take targeted support for the establishment and expansion of transformation and complementary industries and the maintenance of basic agricultural products by the non-governmental sector in production hubs.
Article 145- In order to make the production of agricultural products economical and competitive and increase the export of agricultural products, organize resource management, protect basic resources, and add value and complete the value chain of agricultural products:

Note 1: Imports of agricultural production inputs such as seeds, seedlings, fertilizers, and pesticides are exempt from the effective tariff with the coordination and permission of the Ministry of Agricultural Jihad.
Note 2: The Ministry of Agricultural Jihad will be responsible for selecting tariff instruments, determining quantitative quotas, entry times, and tariff amounts for agricultural goods and food products.
C- The operations of production, processing, storage, quarantine, and marketing of newly emerging agricultural products, inputs, and commodities, the provision of new agricultural services, and the agents and certifiers of processes will be based on regulations and standards prepared by the Ministry of Agricultural Jihad in the first year of the program and approved by the Council of Ministers.
Article 146 - In order to increase production and improve the productivity and efficiency of agricultural lands per hectare, the government shall provide the necessary legal and financial support for the formation of legal entities, with priority given to agricultural production cooperatives, in order to implement unified management or adopt incentive policies for the integration of agricultural lands.
Article 147- In order to empower the country's natural resources and watershed management structure, the following measures shall be taken:
A- The economic value (market and non-market functions) of natural resources, costs and social benefits of development plans and projects should be the criteria for action in feasibility studies of plans and projects.
B- In order to resolve disputes over state and national lands, the National Forests, Rangelands and Watershed Management Organization is exempt from paying litigation costs, as the case may be, after approval by the highest authority of the relevant executive body.
C- The Ministry of Agricultural Jihad (the National Forests, Rangelands and Watershed Management Organization) is required, within the first year of the program, to manage, construct, maintain, develop and exploit forest parks and rangelands suitable for tree planting, abandoned nurseries and lands located in green uses and green belts of cities within the boundaries and boundaries of cities, while maintaining the ownership of the government and the use according to the plan agreed upon by the municipality and the aforementioned organization, without receiving any property rights, to the relevant municipality for the purpose of developing green space and optimal use.

A- Replacing fossil fuels and renewable energies instead of wood fuel
B- Developing wood agriculture and intensifying the fight against smuggling of wood and forest and rangeland products, eliminating wood import tariffs and allowing the import of untreated wood.
C- Organizing forests and supporting industrial livestock production
D- Organizing construction in forest areas based on relevant laws and regulations.
E - Development of man-planted forests
F- Implementing watershed management operations up to eight million hectares by the end of the program.

Article 149- The government is authorized to take the following measures with the aim of ensuring food security:

C- Supporting increased production of animal protein from all types of livestock, poultry and aquatic animals.

Note 2: The government approved the program to ensure food safety from farm to fork during the first year of the program and took the necessary legal measures to implement it.

Article 161

B- The government is required to diversify the rates of air cargo and passenger transportation services by the end of the first year of the program, and from the beginning of the third year of the program, while fully liberalizing air transportation rates, airport services, and flight network, canceling mandatory discounts and any exemptions in the field of utilizing air navigation services, airports, landings and takeoffs, flight services, assignment of sites, and other related matters, to make their rates realistic.

Article 163
A- In order to provide the resources needed for the development, maintenance, and operation of road transportation, the Road Administration and Road Transport Organization is permitted to collect tolls from the movement of goods and passengers on the country's roads, with the exception of rural and tribal roads, based on ton-kilometers and person-kilometers, upon the proposal of the General Assembly of the Road Administration and Road Transport Organization and with the approval of the Economic Council.
B- The amount of tolls collected per ton-kilometer of goods transported within the country from international transport companies that undertake the transport of goods in transit and abroad shall be determined upon the proposal of the Ministry of Roads and Transport and approved by the Economic Council.
C- One hundred percent (100%) of the damages caused to technical buildings and facilities will be recovered from the person causing the damage by the aforementioned organization.
D- In order to assist in the provision of intercity road transportation fleets and the construction of passenger and cargo terminals and roadside welfare service complexes by the private and cooperative sectors, with priority given to veterans' cooperatives, funds will be paid from the internal resources of companies affiliated with the Ministry of Roads and Transport in the form of managed funds.
E- Iranian road transport companies are allowed to import their required trucks that meet current standards and are less than three years old, with the approval and recognition of the Road Administration and Road Transport Organization, under the name of their company, provided that they have after-sales services as per the Law on the Protection of Automobile Consumer Rights approved on 23/3/1386. The government is authorized to apply customs discounts and facilities in this regard. The use of these discounts is subject to the export of one worn-out truck with an age of more than twenty-five years for each imported truck. The regulations for the implementation of this clause shall be prepared by the Ministries of Roads and Transport, Economic Affairs and Finance, and the Deputy Minister and shall be approved by the Council of Ministers within a maximum of six months after the approval of this law.
F- In order to optimally utilize the country's capacities and geographical location:
1- The Ministry of Roads and Transportation, in coordination and partnership with the Statistical Center of Iran, is required to establish a comprehensive national transportation bank and establish a comprehensive information system for transportation accidents and incidents by the end of the second year of the program, including information received from the police, the country's health and treatment sector, road organizations, civil aviation and ports and shipping, the railway company, and other relevant organizations, with the aim of collecting, clarifying, and providing accurate and usable data and information in analyzing and formulating national safety goals and programs. The statistical report on the country's transportation is compiled and published annually by the Statistical Center of Iran.
2- The comprehensive transportation plan of the country, with the aim of responding to actual and potential demands and achieving an appropriate position in the areas of safety, energy, economy, transportation and environment, will be approved by the Council of Ministers by the end of the third year of the program. From the time of approval of the comprehensive plan, the start of all new projects for the development and construction of transportation infrastructures can only be implemented based on this plan and within the framework of the annual budget.
3- The government is required to provide the necessary legal mechanisms to reduce the number of fatalities from traffic accidents on the country's roads by ten percent (10%) annually during the program.
G- In order to facilitate trade, make transportation activities competitive, considering the comparative advantages in the supply chain and regional and international transportation services, the government is authorized to:
1- Support the creation of logistics parks, the construction of terminals, combined passenger and freight transportation towns, and the expansion of dry ports by the private and cooperative sectors.
2- Prepare a plan to locate container terminals and combined transportation in the country's main and transit network, including the North-South, East-West, and Asian networks, by the end of the second year of the program and implement it through the private and cooperative sectors.
3- Provide the necessary mechanisms to achieve an increase in the volume of foreign transit of goods by at least ten percent (10%) annually by the end of the program.
Note 1- All receipts subject to clauses (a), (b) and (c) of this article, after collection, shall be deposited into the account of the Road Administration and Transportation Organization with the General Treasury to be spent based on the annual budget of that organization.
Note 2- In the cases referred to in paragraph (d) of this article, as well as in the case of establishing fuel supply stations, the Ministry of Agricultural Jihad shall take action to transfer the right to exploit national resource lands outside the boundaries of cities at preferential or free prices based on policies approved by the Economic Council, upon the introduction of the Ministry of Roads and Transportation.
Article 164- The government is authorized to take the usual legal action and provide the basis for using domestic and foreign resources for the growth and development of railway activities in order to create a development approach in the railway transport network and increase the share of freight and passengers by the private and cooperative sectors, and with the aim of separating the duties of governance and entrepreneurship in transportation, regarding the reform of the structure and change of the statute of the Islamic Republic of Iran Railways and the transfer of its non-governmental sectors to the private and cooperative sectors, and the creation of new capacities by the private and cooperative sectors.
In order to strengthen rail freight and passenger transportation, the government can transfer part of the infrastructure equipment, improvement, and capacity increase projects, including double-tracking, electrification, track construction, and station extension, to the private sector and cooperatives through methods such as partnership, build, operate, and transfer (BOT) and design, procurement, and supply (EPCF), by transferring privileges such as using rail transportation infrastructure and transferring access rights to it until the agreed capital and profit are fully depreciated.
Article 165- In order to enhance the competitiveness of the country's maritime transportation services, the Supreme Council of the Ports and Maritime Organization is authorized to formulate a flexible tariff system for maritime and port services in accordance with the supply and demand conditions for proposal and approval by the Economic Council.
Article 166- In order to strengthen the country's position in the international air transport network and increase income from transit and transportation of goods and passengers, and productive employment, and to transform Imam Khomeini International Airport into the first hub for regional cargo transport and the second hub for regional passenger transport, with emphasis on the organizational, financial, and managerial independence of this airport and the creation of a sustainable flow of financial resources for the beneficiaries, the government shall take the following measures:
A- Establishing an airport city within the Imam Khomeini International Airport and establishing a free trade zone and a special economic zone in part of the airport lands to provide banking and insurance services and other airport city services such as tourism, medicine, welfare and similar services.
Note - The area of ​​the free trade and special economic zones will be from the southern and western ends of the air section of Imam Khomeini Airport, with an area of ​​one thousand five hundred hectares and two thousand five hundred hectares, respectively. The airport city administration, the subject of this article, which will also include the above-mentioned free trade and special economic zones, will be in the form of a state company affiliated with the specialized parent company of the country's airports, whose statutes will be approved by the Council of Ministers upon the proposal of the Ministry of Roads and Transport and the Deputy.
B- Creating and completing the necessary infrastructure and infrastructures, with priority given to the construction of the second phase of Imam Khomeini International Airport.
Housing
Article 167- In Article (18) of the Law on Organizing and Supporting the Production and Supply of Housing, the phrase “remaining from the Fourth Economic, Social and Cultural Development Plan of the Islamic Republic of Iran” is changed to the phrase “Fifth Plan”.
Article 168- In order to make buildings more resilient and improve consumption patterns, especially energy consumption, in the building and housing sectors, the following measures shall be taken:
A- Municipalities are required to include the requirement to comply with national building regulations in building permits. The issuance of completion certificates for units constructed on the basis of these permits is subject to full compliance with these regulations.
Note - Violators of national building regulations, including the designer, supervisor, contractor, and owner, are required to fix the defects and compensate for the damage, based on a regulation approved by the Council of Ministers.
B- Issuance of any building permit is subject to the submission of the basic approval of the quality insurance of the constructed building. Insurance companies are required to compensate for any damage to the constructed building within three months based on the issued insurance policy.
C- All government executives are required to insure their professional liability and, if necessary, the liability of other relevant factors in the design, calculation, and supervision of buildings under their coverage, whether urban or rural, with one of the qualified companies.
D - In order to conduct the necessary studies to reduce earthquake risk:
1- The network of accelerometer and seismograph stations and earthquake precursors should be developed.
Note - Accelerometer stations in cities and large villages will be developed at a rate of one station for every twenty-five thousand people.
2- Effective materials and methods for building retrofitting should be standardized and their manufacturers should be supported.
Article 169- The Supreme Council for Urban Planning and Architecture is obligated to formulate and promote Islamic-Iranian architectural and urban planning models:
A- Form a working group composed of representatives of relevant agencies and experts and specialists in the fields of architecture, urban planning, and theology to conduct applied research, policy-making, develop rules and regulations, and promote the desired models.
B- Review, prepare and compile plans to adapt urban and rural buildings and spaces for the physically and motorically disabled.
Municipalities and rural districts are required to take steps to improve urban and rural public streets and spaces based on the regulations and plans covered by this clause.
Note 1: The approvals of the aforementioned council are binding on all executive bodies, city and village Islamic councils, and all owners and builders.
Note 2: The Ministry of Science, Research and Technology and other educational institutions should review the syllabus and content of the relevant courses based on the recommendations of the Supreme Council of Urban Planning and Architecture.
Article 170- In order to achieve sustainable development in urban and rural areas, the Ministry of Housing and Urban Development is required to organize economic, social, and physical interactions between cities with a population of more than one hundred thousand people or villages located within their boundaries through the preparation and implementation of comprehensive and detailed urban complex plans with a priority approach to the internal development of cities, utilizing the development capacities of potential villages, and protecting agricultural lands and gardens located inside and on the outskirts of cities and villages.
Article 171
A- The Supreme Council of Urban Planning and Architecture of Iran is responsible for identifying special areas in need of improvement and renovation in dilapidated structures and categorizing projects located in these areas, with priority given to:
1- Projects whose timely implementation is essential due to public interest.
2- Take action on projects that can be implemented over time through the development of rules and regulations, public participation, and support from the government, municipalities, and villages.
Note - Projects in group (1) are included in the government's public and development programs, subject to the "Legal Bill on the Procedure for Purchasing and Acquiring Land and Real Estate, Approved by the Revolutionary Council in 1979", and all or part of the resources required for them can be provided through non-governmental sectors. The decision issued under Article (4) of the aforementioned law is subject to appeal in the competent judicial authorities.
B- The relevant executive bodies are required to take steps to revive worn-out and unsuitable rural structures in order to increase productivity and land extraction.
C- The Ministry of Housing and Urban Development and municipalities are required to support the efforts of the non-governmental sector to revitalize and reconstruct dilapidated urban areas within the framework of the approved budget by implementing incentive policies and within the framework of the law supporting the revitalization of dilapidated urban areas.
D- The Ministry of Housing and Urban Development and municipalities are required to rehabilitate and reconstruct at least ten percent (10%) of the dilapidated urban fabric each year during the program. The facilities and budget required annually will be foreseen in the general budget upon the proposal of the Ministry of Housing and Urban Development and made available to the Ministry of Housing and Urban Development and municipalities or the implementers of renovation projects upon introduction by the municipality.
Note - The government is required to allocate at least fifty percent (50%) of the resources, credits, and facilities granted to the housing sector, including the Mehr housing projects, housing for youth and the needy, and the like, to the implementation of the aforementioned projects within the boundaries of dilapidated urban areas.
E- The Ministry of Housing and Urban Development is required to prioritize the organization and revitalization of cities damaged by the imposed war and the use of abandoned lands and remaining dilapidated buildings within the targeted cities.
Article 172- In order to promote sustainable and inclusive environmental conditions for residents of marginalized areas from the benefits of urbanization and to anticipate and prevent the creation of unauthorized settlements, the government is required to take the following measures:
A- Organizing the marginalized areas designated by the Supreme Council of Urban Planning and Architecture of Iran through the development and implementation of legal, financial, and cultural mechanisms and empowering residents of urban areas with their participation, within the framework of the "National Document for Empowerment and Organizing Unauthorized Settlements" and creating satellite towns to accommodate the immigrant population.
B- Establishing deterrent regulations and imposing a ban on providing all infrastructure services to unauthorized constructions outside the boundaries of cities and villages, and their demolition in cooperation with the judiciary.
C- Preparing and implementing a master plan for villages located within the boundaries of metropolitan cities with the approach of controlling the areas of the aforementioned villages within their natural growth limits.
D - Designing and creating green belts around population centers through government resources by municipalities.
Article 173: The government is authorized to take legal action during the program to prepare a comprehensive urban management plan in order to achieve an appropriate structure and coordinated and integrated urban management within the boundaries and boundaries of cities, with the approach of achieving sustainable development of cities, centralizing management by delegating the duties and responsibilities of government agencies to the private and cooperative sectors and municipalities, reviewing and updating the laws and regulations of municipalities, and promoting the status of municipalities and their unions.
Article 174- Islamic councils, municipalities, and other relevant authorities are required to take action by the end of the first year of the program by formulating a sustainable revenue system for municipalities by implementing the following policies:
A- Reducing the rate of building permit fees for commercial, administrative, and industrial uses in proportion to residential uses in the same area, taking into account climatic conditions and local location, and converting these fees into fees and service charges for operating the construction units of these uses, as well as fees on the added value of lands and properties resulting from the implementation of urban development plans.
B- Determining the citizens' share in covering the costs of public and urban services, maintenance, renovation and urban development, as well as determining the government's share in creating development, urban development and transportation infrastructure.
C- Converting taxes that are the subject of municipal revenue from taxes on real estate to taxes resulting from consumption and services.
D- Determining enforcement guarantees for the collection of fees, service charges, and other legal revenues of municipalities.
E - Increasing the density of wooded lands with a lower occupancy level compared to non-wooded lands with a higher occupancy level.
F- Strengthening management and oversight mechanisms for municipal spending.
G- Outsourcing of assignable tasks and directing the budget of municipalities towards spending in the area of ​​their main and legal duties and prohibiting the payment of any expenses from municipal funds to executive agencies.
Article 175- In order to create sustainable income for municipalities:
A- The Tax Affairs Organization is obligated to provide information on taxable income of businesses within the limits stipulated in laws related to municipalities.
B- Note (2) of the single article of the law establishing the Tehran and Suburbs Urban Train Company, approved on 16/2/1354, applies to other municipalities and underground water and sewage, traffic and service tunnels.
Article 176: The Ministry of Housing and Urban Development (National Land and Housing Organization) is required to sell urban lands in its possession at auction and deposit the proceeds into the national treasury account as specific income in order to secure the necessary financial resources to pay the compensation for the ownership quota, both for eligible and non-eligible properties, and to purchase the lands required for the implementation of housing projects, especially housing for young couples and low-income groups, so that they can be spent in the form of annual budgets to achieve the aforementioned objectives.
Note - The implementation of the projects subject to this article is carried out through the private and cooperative sectors.

Article 177- In order to revive, develop, and optimally utilize the capacity of endowments and foundations and to combat the abuse of endowments, the following measures shall be taken:
A- The National Deeds and Real Estate Registration Organization, in cooperation with the Endowment and Charity Affairs Organization, is required to register uncontested endowments whose authenticity is proven but have not yet been registered.
B- In order to create a comprehensive database of endowments, all natural and legal persons in charge of endowments are required to place information on all endowments, vows, remaining donations, deposits, alms, and general endowments, both non-custodial and under guardianship, on a system that will be created by the Endowments and Charity Affairs Organization by the end of the first year of the program.
Chapter Six - Regional Development
Article 178
A- In order to make decisions, approve, guide, coordinate, and supervise the planning and budget affairs of the provinces, within the framework of the country's major programs, policies, and guidelines, a Planning and Development Council for each province is formed, headed by the governor and composed of the following members:
1- Head of the provincial unit of ministries with provincial units (one person)
2- Deputy Provincial Planning Officer
3- Director General of Physical Education of the Province
4- Head of the Provincial Cultural Heritage, Handicrafts and Tourism Organization
5- Director General of Environmental Protection of the Province
6- Governors (as applicable)
7- The commander of the provincial mobilization
Note - In the case of ministries that have multiple provincial units, one of the heads of the provincial units, selected by the minister, participates in the council meetings.
B- To increase the powers of the cities within the framework of the duties of the Provincial Development Planning Council, a planning committee will be formed in each city, headed by the governor.
Note 1- The members of the county planning committee include the county administrative heads (corresponding to the provincial general offices).
Note 2: The deputy chairman of the council in the absence of the governor and the secretary of the aforementioned council are the responsibility of the governor's deputy for planning.
C- Two representatives of the province, upon the proposal of the Provincial Assembly of Representatives and the approval of the Parliament, will serve as observers on the Provincial Planning and Development Council, and representatives of each county will serve as observers on the County Planning Committee.
D- The meetings of the council are official with the presence of two-thirds of the members and its resolutions are valid with a majority of more than half of the members present. Voting in the meetings of the Provincial Planning and Development Council is by paper ballot and secret.
E - The executive regulations of this article, including the description of duties, authorities stated in this article, and the specialized working groups of the Provincial Planning and Development Council and the County Planning Committee, shall be submitted to the Council of Ministers for approval upon the proposal of the Deputy.
And- The Ministry of Interior is required to submit a copy of the approved provincial and city budgets and programs to the provincial representatives, and to submit a report on the provincial performance of the program every year and a budget progress report every three months to the Islamic Consultative Assembly.
Article 179: In order to decentralize and increase the powers for the development and construction of the provinces, the provincial revenue-expenditure system shall be organized as follows:
A- The annual provincial budget includes provincial resources, including provincial income, specific income, transfer of financial and capital assets, and share of national resources, as well as provincial expenditures, including expenditure credits and acquisition of capital and financial assets, each of which is included in the annual budget laws in aggregate form. The distribution of expenditure credits to the “program-system” and the distribution of the total capital asset acquisition credits of the cities are the responsibility of the Provincial Planning and Development Council.
Note - The credit for projects that are provincial in nature or are located in multiple cities is determined directly and separately by the council.
B- The annual provincial budget document, which specifies the obligations of the Provincial Planning and Development Council and the Deputy, shall be prepared in the form of a contract and exchanged between the Council Chairman and the Deputy. The said contract form and the relevant instructions shall be prepared and notified by the Deputy.
C- In each province, a provincial treasury is established under the supervision of the General Treasury and the General Department of Economic and Financial Affairs of the province. The General Treasury is required to open an account for each of the provincial treasury as the “General Treasury Account” (Provincial Treasury) in the provincial capital, and at the beginning of each year, calculate the amount of each province’s payroll in proportion to the approved spending credits and the acquisition of capital assets of that province and deposit it into the “General Treasury Account” (Provincial Treasury) no later than the 20th of Farvardin.
D- In order to allocate provincial funds to the province, taking into account the status of revenue realization, policies and general guidelines governing the government's general budget, a provincial funds allocation committee is formed under the responsibility of the governor and with the membership of the provincial deputy planning officer and the head of the provincial economic and financial affairs organization.
E - In proportion to the transfer and implementation of activities and plans for the acquisition of national capital assets to the provinces, the Deputy, in addition to the aforementioned capital asset acquisition credits, transfers the relevant portion of the cost credits of the national executive agencies to the provinces during the year, as determined by the Deputy.
Article 180- In line with the implementation of Article 48 of the Constitution, general policies, and in order to achieve the visionary goals and for the purpose of balanced use of the country's facilities, fair distribution, elimination of discrimination, and promotion of the level of less developed regions, and realization of progress and justice, the government is obliged to act in the distribution of public resources and facility benefit subsidies in such a way that the gap in the index of the enjoyment of counties with less than the average level of the country at the end of the Fourth Plan in the economic, social, cultural, infrastructure, and security sectors in each year of the plan approaches the aforementioned level by at least ten percent (10%).
The equivalent of two percent (2%) of the government's general budget appropriations will be made available to the Deputy in the form of specific lines for specific expenditures in the annual budget to be distributed and spent in order to achieve the above objectives.
Article 181- In order to improve the country's planning system by considering the two principles of land planning and environmental sustainability in all development activities and national and provincial investments, creating sectoral, regional and sectoral-regional coordination and observing justice in the distribution of resources and opportunities, balanced development of regions, enhancing the managerial capabilities of the provinces and transferring executive powers to the provinces and concentrating governance affairs in the center, the necessary mechanisms and indicators shall be approved by the Council of Ministers.
Note: Any discount or exemption from municipal taxes and fees by the government and approved laws is subject to its provision from the country's annual general budget. Otherwise, exemption or exemption from municipal taxes and fees is prohibited.
Article 182- In order to coordinate and supervise the preparation and implementation of territorial development plans and projects, a "Territorial Planning Council" is formed under the responsibility of the Vice President for Strategic Planning and Supervision and with the following main duties:
A- Formulating, approving, and supervising the establishment of the "Integrated System of Territorial Development Planning and Management" and regulating the relationships between its main elements.
B- Reviewing and approving territorial development plans and plans at the national, regional and provincial levels and supervising their implementation.
Two members of the Planning and Budget, Accounting and Construction Commissions of the Islamic Consultative Assembly, elected by the Assembly, participate in the meetings of the Council as observer members.
The executive regulations of this article, including a detailed description of the duties, responsibilities, authorities, and organs of the Council, shall be approved by the Council of Ministers, upon the proposal of the Deputy (Secretariat), within a maximum of six months after the ratification of this law.
Note - The composition of the council members, in addition to the Vice President for Strategic Planning and Supervision, includes: the Ministers of Interior, Intelligence, Agricultural Jihad, Economy and Finance, Housing and Urban Development, Defense and Support of the Armed Forces, and the Head of the Environmental Protection Organization, and three experts working in executive agencies selected by the President.
Article 183: Executive bodies are required to organize their operational plans in a way that provides the basis for the realization of spatial planning orientations.
Note 1: The responsibility for following up and supervising the implementation of the provisions of this article lies with the Deputy.
Note 2 - The Economic Council is authorized, upon the proposal of the Spatial Planning Council within the framework of the relevant laws of the country, to regulate incentive and disincentive policies, including interest subsidies and fees on bank facilities, tariffs, duties, and other regulations for various activities and regions of the country in such a way that the programs, actions, and investments of the public and non-governmental sectors are directed towards realizing the spatial planning orientations.
The Economic Council's resolutions are communicated through the Secretariat (Deputy).
Article 184- In order to achieve the goals set forth in Article 50 (50) of the Constitution, a “Strategic Environmental Assessment System” shall be developed and implemented at the national, regional, and thematic levels based on the following axes:
A- General strategies for regional development and considering the cumulative effects of these strategies
B- Development Environmental Impact Index and Biocapacity Index
C- Applying regional and national sustainability indicators
D- Monitoring regional and thematic development programs and plans
E - Empowerment program and determination of the implementation mechanism for strategic environmental assessment
Note 1: The Supreme Council for Environmental Protection is the national institution responsible for the strategic environmental assessment of national-thematic development plans and programs.
Note 2: Regional Development Coordination Councils are responsible for the strategic environmental assessment of regional development plans based on the criteria approved by the Supreme Council for Environmental Protection.
Note 3: The Supreme Administrative Council is required to delegate the executive duties of the Supreme Environmental Protection Council to the provincial units of the Environmental Protection Organization.
The executive regulations of this article, including the process and implementation mechanisms of the strategic assessment, shall be approved by the Council of Ministers.
Article 185- In order to optimally utilize natural and basic resources, a system of environmental sustainability indicators will be prepared and implemented by the second year of the program within the framework of the following:
A- Developing a national model of sustainability indicators
B- Creating a database and information on sustainability indicators
C- Determining sustainability indicators and quantifying them for the program years.
D- Determining sustainability criteria and standards governing the balancing of environmental burdens and controlling the effects of development on the natural and human environment.
The executive regulations of this article, including the responsible and cooperating executive agencies, general approaches, and the timing of the development and implementation of the system, and the authorities for its approval, shall be approved by the Council of Ministers.
Article 186: The government is authorized to formulate a comprehensive plan for national divisions, including indicators for reviewing existing divisional units to create new national division levels with a focus on decentralization and delegation of authority to local managers, and strengthening the role of governors as the highest representatives of the government in the provinces.
Environment
Article 187
A- The government is authorized to take legal action regarding the following matters, in order to protect, restore, and sustainably utilize the environment, natural resources, and biodiversity, by the end of the second year of the program at the latest:
1- Developing and implementing an integrated ecosystem management plan and an operational plan for the protection and sustainable use of the biodiversity of the country's sensitive and fragile ecosystems.
2- Amendment of the statute of the "National Environmental Fund"
Note 1: The judiciary, military and law enforcement agencies, and municipalities will fully cooperate with the government to implement the provisions of this article.
Note 2: The Environmental Protection Organization shall identify and declare coastal and marine areas of high environmental sensitivity.
Note 3: The executive regulations of this article shall be prepared by the Environmental Protection Organization in cooperation with the Ministries of Power, Housing and Urban Development, Agricultural Jihad, and the Deputy Minister, and shall be approved by the Council of Ministers.
B- The government is required to establish the Sea and Coastal Development and Development Organization to study and implement improvement and swimming plans under the supervision of the Ministry of Interior as the main custodian of organizing beaches and swimming plans in the first year of the program. The statutes of the aforementioned organization will be approved by the Council of Ministers.
Article 188- In order to facilitate investment, the Environmental Protection Organization, in cooperation with the Ministries of Industries and Mines and Agricultural Jihad, and in coordination with the Deputy, shall formulate specific, transparent, and consistent environmental criteria and standards within the framework of the laws for a five-year period and submit the program for inclusion in the investment guidebook referred to in Note (3) of Article (7) of the Law on the Implementation of General Policies of Article 44 (44) of the Constitution within the first six months. All production, industrial, construction, service, and infrastructure units are required to comply with the above criteria and standards.
From the second year of the program, any requirement to obtain a permit from the responsible agencies, except for large industries subject to Article (105) of the Third Development Plan Law, will be abolished and the implementation of the aforementioned policies will be carried out through control of the implementation of the regulations.
Article 189- The Environmental Protection Organization is obliged to:
A- In order to raise public awareness and achieve sustainable development in order to protect the environment and with an emphasis on influential and priority groups from the beginning of the Fifth Economic, Social and Cultural Development Plan of the Islamic Republic of Iran, the relevant executive regulations shall be submitted to the Cabinet for approval upon the proposal of the Supreme Council for Environmental Protection. All relevant agencies, state media and the Islamic Republic of Iran Broadcasting are required to implement educational programs without receiving funds as the subject of this legal article.
B- Establish the country's environmental information system at regional, national and provincial levels by the end of the first year of the Fifth Plan to provide the basis for environmental monitoring, information dissemination and assessment. Relevant agencies are required to cooperate in the development and implementation of this system.
Article 190- All executive agencies and non-governmental public institutions and organizations are required to reduce government spending, implement policies for the optimal use of basic and environmental resources to implement the green management program, including managing the consumption of energy, water, raw materials and equipment (including paper), reducing solid waste and recycling it (in buildings and vehicles) in accordance with a regulation prepared by the Environmental Protection Organization and the Deputy in cooperation with relevant agencies and approved by the Council of Ministers.
Article 191
A- A habitat management plan for sensitive ecosystems, especially Lake Urmia, shall be prepared and implemented. The Environmental Protection Organization, in cooperation with the Deputy Minister and the Ministries of Power and Agricultural Jihad, shall prepare the implementing regulations for this article and submit them to the Council of Ministers for approval.
B- In order to prevent excessive hunting and the destruction of biodiversity, the government is required to take measures, in cooperation with the relevant authorities in the Armed Forces of the Islamic Republic of Iran, to limit the number of hunting weapons and review the criteria for issuing the aforementioned licenses, and to determine the number of hunting weapons in proportion to the population that can be taken from wildlife, as determined by the Environmental Protection Organization, and to collect unauthorized weapons by the end of the program.
Article 192- In order to reduce environmental polluting and destructive factors, all large production, industrial, construction, service and infrastructure units are required to:
A- They should subject their large production, service and development plans and projects to environmental impact assessment before implementation and during the feasibility and location studies stage, based on the criteria approved by the Supreme Council for Environmental Protection.
It is mandatory for project and plan implementers to comply with the results of the evaluations.
B- Take action to sample and measure their own pollution and environmental destruction and submit the results to the Environmental Protection Organization within the framework of a self-declaration. Units that have the capability and necessity to install and operate a real-time and continuous monitoring system(s) must install and operate the aforementioned system(s) by the end of the third year of the program. Violators will be subject to Article (30) of the Law on the Prevention of Air Pollution, approved on 3/2/1374.
C- Improve their technical specifications in a way that complies with environmental regulations and standards and reduces pollution and the destruction of basic resources, especially natural resources and water.
Note 1: The government is required to calculate and include the economic value of environmental resources and the relevant tables and accounts in the national accounts.
Note 2- The Deputy is required to work with the Environmental Protection Agency and other relevant agencies to estimate the economic values ​​of natural and environmental resources and the costs resulting from pollution and environmental degradation in the development process and calculate it in the national accounts, and to prepare guidelines for calculating the values ​​and costs of priority items such as: forests, water, soil, energy, biodiversity and environmental pollution in sensitive areas and to approve them with the relevant authorities. The values ​​and costs for which the guidelines have been approved will be considered in the feasibility study of capital asset acquisition plans.
Article 193
A- Municipalities of cities with a population of over 200,000, coastal cities, and cities on the edge of inland wetlands are required to recycle their waste using new methods and technologies, with priority given to organic methods such as vermicompost, by the end of the fourth year of the program, in line with urban waste management. From the final year of the program, any landfilling of waste in such cities is absolutely prohibited.
B- The Environmental Protection Agency is required to take the necessary measures to reduce air pollution to global standards, with priority given to identifying and containing dust emission hotspots, and controlling and reducing greenhouse gas emissions.
C- From the second year of the program, any new industrial and mineral exploitation of inland wetlands is absolutely prohibited.
D - Considering the special conditions of the country's wetlands from the economic, agricultural, ecological, biodiversity and tourism perspectives, the existence of desirable pastures and agricultural lands around them, the existence of serious challenges in allocating water resources in these areas, and in general, the complexity and fragility of the natural ecosystem of these areas, the government is obliged to reorganize existing organizations in the water, agriculture, natural resources, environment and tourism sectors in the first year of the program to implement integrated management with the participation of users in the plains surrounding these wetlands.
Note - The executive regulations of this article shall be approved by the Council of Ministers upon the joint proposal of the Deputy and the Environmental Protection Organization and the Ministries of Agricultural Jihad, Interior, Oil, Power, and Industries and Mines.
Rural development
Article 194: The government is required to provide the necessary support for the following measures in order to improve the situation of villages in the fields of policy-making, planning, leadership, supervision, and coordination between executive agencies, to improve the income level and quality of life of villagers and farmers, and to reduce the existing inequalities between rural, nomadic, and urban communities:
A- Promoting rural development indicators and providing new services and preparing a plan to prioritize rural services according to regional and local conditions.
B- Supporting the expansion of industrial agriculture and rural industries with priority for the development of industrial clusters and chains - small and medium-sized agriculture, the majority of whose inputs and production factors are found in rural geography, as well as handicrafts and tourism services, and the creation and development of local markets with priority for rural centers with development potential.
C- Determining the management model in settlements without an Islamic council
D- Formulating incentive policies for reverse migration "from city to village" and stabilizing the relative rural population by the end of the first year of the program.
E- Improving, renovating, reconstructing, and securing the physical structure of the rural environment and housing based on the Islamic-Iranian architectural model with the participation of the people, the government, and public institutions.
F- Continuous technical and vocational training of villagers with the aim of empowering them to provide and use modern services, participate in industrial activities, and improve the quality of products.
G- Organizing and establishing entrepreneurial and employment-generating activities in small and medium-sized production and services in rural areas through the provision of financial and credit incentives.
H - Organizing villages into rural complexes in order to provide better and more effective services.
I - Construction, repair, maintenance and safety of the rural road network
Y- Improving the development indicators of nomads through settlement and organization of households.
K- Financial support through granting facilities, managed funds, profit and fee subsidies to develop employment for rural and nomadic households, with priority given to local and indigenous methods, as well as strengthening integrated land management through partnership with legal entities in order to prevent land fragmentation and consolidate the management of small agricultural lands.
L- Legal action to establish an agricultural township company by amending the statutes of one of the existing specialized parent companies within the framework of the general policies of Article 44 (44) of the Constitution in order to guide, manage, and construct and develop the infrastructure of agricultural, livestock, and fishery complexes.
M- Joint investment with non-governmental sectors up to forty-nine percent (49%) within the framework of the general policies of Article 44 (44) of the Constitution and through the specialized parent company "Support for the Development of Investment in the Agricultural Sector" and the specialized parent company "Iran Water Resources Management", in the field of agricultural development with modern, economic and productive technology and infrastructure and emerging projects in the agricultural and water resources sectors and the development of rural areas and the rejuvenation of the demographic fabric of rural and nomadic areas.
Note - The government is required to transfer government shares in the new enterprise to non-government sectors no later than ten years after operation.
N- Developing and targeting research, education, production and advertising, as well as developing carpet e-commerce and creating carpet houses in target and desired markets for identity, improving production quality and streamlining, customizability, supporting the creation, development and equipping of centralized and decentralized workshops and rural and urban handmade carpet unions and cooperatives throughout the country, as well as the industries and ancillary services of handmade carpets in order to promote and improve productivity, stabilize and increase the export share and domestic and foreign marketing.
Note - The Ministry of Commerce is responsible for following up on the provisions of this clause within the government.
S- Generalizing and expanding rural insurance and its one hundred percent (100%) coverage through strengthening the Rural and Nomad Social Insurance Fund.
A - Anticipating sports venues for the rural community
F- Preparing rural master plans and determining the boundaries of villages throughout the country with the suggestions of technical experts, under the supervision of the Housing Foundation and the approval of the district administration of each district, and with the knowledge of the village heads and heads of the Islamic Councils of the villages, and approving them in a committee consisting of the head of the Islamic Revolution Housing Foundation of the province as the head, the governor of the county, the district governor, a representative of the provincial Housing and Urban Development Organization, the head of the Islamic Revolution Housing Foundation of the county, a representative of the provincial Agricultural Jihad Organization, a representative of the deputy governor for development affairs, and the head of the Islamic Council of the village as the supervisor.
Note - A representative of the municipality of the city in which the village is located can participate in the meeting of the aforementioned committee as an expert and observer.
The executive regulations of this article, including the appointment of the village's senior manager, the manner and process of applying the aforementioned supports, the manner of participation and definitions, and the manner of managing the rural complex in the first year of the program, will be approved by the Council of Ministers upon the proposal of the Deputy Minister.
Chapter Seven - Defense, Politics, and Security
Defense Affairs
Article 195
A- The Ministry of Defense and Armed Forces Support is required to take the following measures in order to enhance defense capabilities, deterrence power, and defend sovereignty, territorial integrity, national interests, and security, and effectively confront external threats:
1- Quantitative and qualitative improvement of the country's air defense system and establishing coordination under the supervision of the Khatam al-Anbiya (PBUH) Air Defense Headquarters
2- Strengthening the presence and upgrading of infrastructure in order to expand the area and establish more effectively in the country's water areas and protect the country's maritime routes with an emphasis on open waters and completing the Southern Fleet Organization.
3- Optimal utilization of the opportunity of public service to enhance knowledge, skills, and social status of the target community regarding the foundations and values ​​of Islam and the Revolution, with special emphasis on the views and character of Imam Khomeini (RA) and the Supreme Leader, promoting spirituality, social discipline, strengthening constructive interactions and professional skills, and increasing the capacity for specialized employment of conscripts, enriching their jobs, and improving their livelihoods and social status, as well as optimally utilizing those involved in the construction of the country.
4- Creating infrastructure and adopting the necessary mechanisms for the widest possible participation of government agencies and the non-governmental sector in defense programs in times of crisis and war.
Note - The Ministry of Defense and Armed Forces Support, in cooperation with the General Staff of the Armed Forces, is required to formulate the desired level and requirements of each of the above components and submit them to the relevant authorities for approval within the first six months of the program.
B- The Ministry of Defense and Armed Forces Support shall prioritize the following items within the defense affairs budget:
1- Developing and strengthening the air fleet of the armed forces, including purchasing, rebuilding, renovating, and maintaining it to the approved readiness level and operationalizing all airplanes, helicopters, and related weapons, and making flights safe.
2- Completing and updating the independent and secure communications network of the armed forces
3- Establishing an advanced command, control, information, and surveillance system in strategic, space, air, sea, and land dimensions in order to enhance alertness and readiness to identify threats and deal with them quickly and effectively.
4- Qualitative and quantitative development to increase the power and operational stability of missile units.
5- Quantitative and qualitative improvement of the system for handling, providing services, and supporting armed forces personnel in defense, security, combat, and border and difficult-to-reach areas missions.
Article 196 - The government is obligated to provide the necessary measures and facilities throughout the program in order to quantitatively and qualitatively strengthen the mobilization of the oppressed and increase the presence of popular forces in the security and defense of the country, the ideals and foundations of the Islamic Revolution's thought, and the development of the culture of enjoining good and forbidding evil:
A- Necessary support to increase the number of at least one and a half million active and special Basij members by the end of the program, with an emphasis on training committed and revolutionary human resources.
B- Supporting infrastructure for the development of county areas and Basij resistance ranks, commensurate with the development and changes in the country's divisions.
C- Supporting the ranks of the Basij resistance and the mobilization of professors, students and scholars in the executive bodies, especially in research and scientific activities.
D- Helping to deepen the values ​​of the Islamic Revolution and develop Basij culture and thought through the formation of educational centers, enriching leisure time, developing a culture of values, and using Basij elites for consulting and producing thought, developing a culture of chastity and hijab, and familiarizing with the foundations of the thought of Imam Khomeini (may Allah have mercy on him) by implementing projects for students, pupils, and the ranks and ranks of the Basij resistance.
E - Support and assistance in combating soft war in various fields, with a priority on increasing presence in cyberspace and computer space with a native approach.
F- The possibility of using the services of active and specially employed Basij personnel to participate in training, camps, and other programs for a maximum of fifteen days per year instead of administrative service without receiving mission pay.
G- Forecasting one percent (1%) of the total provincial asset acquisition funds in the annual budget to support the construction and development of Basij resistance bases and areas.
H - Providing the necessary support to attract and employ thirty million people from the tens of millions of people's mobilization with a cultural, social, and scientific approach.
I - Supporting the ranks of the resistance of the women's community mobilization, especially in activities related to developing the culture of chastity and hijab and strengthening the family foundation.
Note 1: The Basij Organization for the Oppressed is permitted to provide the necessary support from its approved funds to provide insurance coverage and compensate for damages resulting from the deployment of Basij members without insurance coverage in various missions, especially defense, security, and light convoys.
Note 2: The executive agencies are allowed to delegate to the Basij Sazandegi Organization, in proportion to its level of readiness, the implementation of part of its educational, health, cultural, leisure enrichment activities and development projects, such as the construction and repair of mosques, schools, and rural health centers, desertification, afforestation, and water supply to villages, as well as in deprived, rural, and border areas, to that organization, using Basij and popular volunteer forces, youth, and specialists in an organized manner.
Article 197: The government is authorized to take the following measures in order to develop the scientific and technological capabilities of the armed forces:
A- Anticipating the funds needed to support the acquisition of new knowledge and technologies, the development of knowledge frontiers, and the production of innovative defense products by allocating at least ten percent (10%) of defense equipment and development funds, in a way that moves toward self-reliance in the following areas over the years of the program:
1- Production of vital and key materials needed by the defense industries
2- Developing new technologies, especially in the fields of navigation and precision components, microelectronics, lasers, optics, nano, biology, information and communications, cognitive and soft materials, sensors, micromechanics, and safe and sustainable satellite manufacturing and launching.
B- Increasing the export of defense products, technical and engineering services by at least fifty percent (50%).
C- Financial support and sponsorship of research activities and projects related to defense affairs in universities and research centers.
D- Financial support for the country's industry to utilize the empty capacities of the defense industries, as well as financial support for utilizing the capacity of the country's industries to meet defense needs, provided that there is no harm to the country's domestic needs and exports.
E- The government is required to make the necessary provision and forecast in the annual budget bill for an annual increase of two percent (2%) in the share of defense appropriations during the action plan.
Fifty percent (50%) of the aforementioned increase will be allocated to maintain the production capacity of defense industry lines, as well as the production of vital materials and key components, and the development of emerging technologies.
Article 198- In order to reduce the vulnerability of infrastructure, promote national stability, protect the people and national resources of the country, and ensure the continuity of services to them in order to complete the civil defense cycle, the following measures shall be taken:
A- Developing the technical standards required for passive defense during the first year of the program.
B- Establishing a monitoring, warning, and neutralization system for new threats in vital, sensitive, and important centers.
C- Securing and protecting the country's vital, sensitive, and important centers to continue their safe and sustainable activities.
Note - Passive defense is only in the field of defense and security threats. The coordination guidelines for the fields of passive defense and the National Crisis Management Organization are submitted to the Council of Ministers for approval with the joint proposal of the Secretariat of the Permanent Committee of Passive Defense and the National Crisis Management Organization and the approval of the General Staff of the Armed Forces.
Article 199- Projects related to passive defense will be implemented upon the proposal of the Secretary of the Standing Committee and the approval of the Chairman of the Standing Committee. Agreements for such projects will be exchanged between the Chairman of the Standing Committee on Passive Defense and the Deputy.
Article 200- In times of peace, with the approval of the General Staff of the Armed Forces, part of the manpower needs of the government, municipalities and villages shall be met by using the services of those subject to military service, provided that they do not harm the country's combat readiness and after completing a military training course. The needs of the government, municipalities and villages shall be announced to the General Staff through the Ministry of Defense and Armed Forces Support.
The costs related to the military training course are borne by the Ministry of Defense and Armed Forces Support, and the salaries, benefits, and rations during the service period are provided from the relevant department's funds. The total amount received by such individuals should not exceed the limit stipulated in the Armed Forces laws.
At least fifty percent (50%) of the above human resources who are university graduates must serve in undeveloped or less developed areas of the country.
Article 201: The government is obligated to take the following measures, subject to approval by the Supreme Commander of the Armed Forces, in order to strengthen the country's defense capabilities, enhance the deterrence capabilities of the armed forces, protect the country's territorial integrity and security, prepare for threats, protect national interests, the Islamic Revolution of Iran, and the country's vital resources, and make defense systems smarter:
A - Strengthening the components of the defense force with an emphasis on modernizing and making equipment intelligent, upgrading human resources, and command systems (I 4C)
B- Promoting modern and intelligent technologies and information systems in the use of defense systems, especially electronic, aerospace, naval, and air defense systems.
C- Optimizing and improving agile and responsive structures in national defense
D- Promoting the presence and contribution of popular forces in establishing security and defending the country and the mutual and optimal use of human resources and capabilities.
E- Modernization, reconstruction, and improvement of defense industries with an eye toward modern industrial systems and processes.
F- Promoting initiative and the ability to effectively confront threats and protect national interests, vital resources, and the Islamic Revolution of Iran.
G- Improving and increasing the level of knowledge and skills of the armed forces, both quantitatively and qualitatively.
H - Improving the level of education, research, and technology in the defense sector and expanding cooperation with domestic and foreign academic scientific centers.
I - Presence and deployment commensurate with threats in the country's water basins (Persian Gulf, Sea of ​​Oman, and Caspian Sea)
Y- Improving the social status and livelihood of armed forces personnel.
K- Observing the principles of passive defense in the design and implementation of sensitive and important projects or those under study, as well as infrastructure facilities and sensitive buildings and the country's main and vital arteries, and public education of the public by the executive bodies subject to Article (179) of this law, in order to prevent and reduce the risks arising from natural disasters.
The implementing regulations of this article will be prepared by the Deputy and Ministry of Defense and Armed Forces Support and the General Staff of the Armed Forces within two months of the ratification of this law and will be approved by the Council of Ministers. If approved by the Supreme Command of the Armed Forces, they will be put into effect.
Article 202: The Ministry of Defense and Armed Forces Support is required, based on the opinion of the General Staff of the Armed Forces, to prepare a comprehensive plan for the deployment of the armed forces at the national level, commensurate with the size and type of threats and environmental conditions, in order to observe the dispersion in the deployment of sensitive and vital facilities and defense industries, as well as the transfer of military center barracks and large defense industrial factories from large cities, especially Tehran, and to submit it to the General Command of the Armed Forces for approval.
The government is required to take the necessary measures to secure and transfer credit, banking, land facilities, change of use, and create privacy for the required facilities. Credits received from bank sources will be repaid from the sale of the transferred premises.
Political, security
Article 203: In order to ensure sustainable security of border areas and effective border control, the Ministry of Interior is required, in cooperation with the Ministries of Economic Affairs and Finance, Justice, Intelligence, and the Police Force, and other relevant ministries and organizations, to prepare a comprehensive plan for sustainable security of border areas by the end of the first year of the program and submit it to the Supreme National Security Council for approval, so that the approved plans for border control and closure are fully implemented by the end of the program.
Note - The comprehensive plan includes control, management, security perimeter, construction, obstruction, intelligence, scientific, equipment, cultural and social, economic measures, and strengthening border guards, using mobilization and popular forces. Any action in the borders and border areas will be carried out based on the plan and indicators included in this plan.
Article 204: In order to promote and strengthen the economy and livelihood of border residents, while observing the considerations and ensuring the sustainable security of border areas, the government shall provide the necessary financial and legal support for the establishment of industries and the formation of border residents' cooperatives, and the right to exploit lands suitable for agriculture and maximize the use of natural resources and tourism in border areas.
Note - In order to guide and provide appropriate infrastructure for the industrial development of border areas, increase economic and social exchanges with neighboring countries, and expand the market for products from border areas, priority will be given to establishing industries in the aforementioned areas, with border industrial towns located on the borders.
Article 205: The Ministry of Intelligence is required to take the following measures with the aim of defending national sovereignty and interests, strengthening interaction with the intelligence community, and preventing and confronting internal and external intelligence and security threats and attacks, especially global arrogance:
A- Quantitative and qualitative improvement of information infrastructures with the aim of consolidating and strengthening sustainable and comprehensive security.
B- Preventing and combating corruption and disruption of economic security, organized crimes against security, terrorist acts, and soft security threats as a judicial officer.
C- Creating and expanding the necessary infrastructure and mechanisms for greater public participation in order to strengthen and consolidate the country's sustainable security.
The regulations for this article will be prepared by the Ministry of Intelligence, in cooperation with the Deputy, and approved by the Council of Ministers by the end of the first year of the program at the latest.
Article 206: The Ministry of Intelligence is obliged to take legal action within the scope of the country's laws and within the framework of its legal authorities, in coordination with the relevant agencies, regarding the following:
A- Preparation of a comprehensive national database plan
Note - All agencies and institutions that use the country's public budget are required to make their digital banks available, with the exception of classified matters, privacy, and matters prohibited by information disclosure laws.
B- Preparing a plan for a national system for determining the security qualifications of government, public and private jobs and a plan for organizing a system for protecting classified documents and information in order to prevent the infiltration and access of foreign agents and enemies of the system to classified information and documents.
C- Forming a special commission composed of the main relevant agencies to prepare and implement a comprehensive plan to combat bioterrorism and determine the duties of each agency in order to coordinate the fight against bioterrorism as an institutionalized principle in the country until the end of the program.
Note - The aforementioned commission will submit to the Islamic Consultative Assembly matters requiring legislation in the field of bioterrorism.
Article 207: The government is authorized to:
A- At the suggestion of the Anti-Narcotics Headquarters, based on the general anti-narcotics policies, with the participation of relevant institutions, prepare a comprehensive bill for the comprehensive fight against narcotics, psychotropic substances, and precursors and submit it to the Islamic Consultative Assembly for approval.
B- With the participation of relevant agencies and chambers of commerce, industries, mines, and cooperatives, prepare a comprehensive bill to combat smuggling of goods and currency and submit it to the Islamic Consultative Assembly for review and approval.
Article 208- In order to modernize and develop professionalism, and to make the Islamic Republic of Iran's police force more efficient and stronger, the following measures will be implemented during the program, with the agreement of the government and the General Staff of the Armed Forces:
A- Providing the necessary mechanisms and facilities for the police force to benefit from new technologies and advanced equipment in order to "technology-based police"
B- Strengthening professionalism by developing specialized training
Article 209- In order to strengthen and promote social and moral security and prevent and combat any cultural and social abnormalities and disruptions to public security;
A- The government is obliged to provide and implement the necessary executive mechanisms with emphasis on the following:
1- Fighting organized movements, groups, and gangs that promote moral deviations, superstition, vulgarity, and nihilism.
2- Promoting a sense of security and making the public space of society healthier.
3- Utilizing and coordinating cultural, educational, training, propaganda, and media measures to combat cultural and social anomalies.
B- All executive agencies are required to take the necessary measures to implement the comprehensive plan for the culture of chastity and regular hijab and to adapt the service environment to the requirements of the Islamic society.
The Ministry of Culture and Islamic Guidance is responsible for overseeing the proper implementation of the plan.
Foreign policy
Article 210- The Ministry of Foreign Affairs is required to present appropriate policies to the relevant authorities to implement the following provisions, taking into account the opinions of experts from all responsible organizations and institutions, in order to elevate the dignity, position, authority, and role of the Islamic Republic of Iran, take advantage of economic opportunities in the region and the international system, and expand the discourse of seeking justice in international relations:
A- Regulating the level of political, economic and cultural relations and relationships with countries that have views and positions that are not aligned with the Islamic Republic of Iran.
B- Striving for an active, effective, and inspiring presence in bilateral relations and regional and international organizations through:
1- Developing a comprehensive system to strengthen Iran's presence in regional and international organizations with a specific agenda for action on each of them, no later than the first year after the program is implemented.
2- Assessing the efficiency and effectiveness of officers working inside and outside the country and employing them with priority given to expertise and efficiency in organizations and embassies of strategic importance.
3- Formulating Iran's proposed process for reforming the structure of the United Nations in line with the national interests of the Islamic Republic of Iran, no later than the first year of the program, and striving to realize it.
4- Pursuing support for the rights of Muslims and the oppressed people of the world, especially the oppressed Palestinian people, in regional and international organizations.
5- Adopting active diplomacy to stop the process of issuing human rights resolutions against the Islamic Republic of Iran.
C- Cooperation with regional and Islamic countries, as well as the use of other economic, legal, political, cultural, media and international capacities to reduce the military presence of foreigners in the region.
D- Strengthening good relations with neighbors, building trust, reducing tensions, and integrating with regional and Islamic countries, especially the Islamic Iranian civilization.
E- Strengthening economic diplomacy through serious support for economic activities, especially the non-governmental sector, and creating the necessary grounds for the relationship and presence of the aforementioned sector in other countries and the Southwest Asian region, especially neighboring and Islamic countries.
F- Developing and deepening justice-oriented diplomacy in the world
G- Improving Iran's management in the economic sphere in the region, especially in the field of energy distribution and transit, and trying to be on the energy transfer routes in the region.
H- Utilizing the capacities of Iranians living abroad, especially in investment, to secure national interests.
Note - The Ministry of Foreign Affairs is required to submit an annual report to the Islamic Consultative Assembly on the extent of implementation of the provisions of this article.
Chapter Eight - Judicial Law
Article 211
A- The judiciary is required to prepare a program of necessary judicial bills by the end of the first year at the latest, in order to reduce criminal titles and lawsuits, establish a judicial police, standardize criminal enforcement guarantees, and replace them with effective and up-to-date non-criminal enforcement guarantees, such as enforcement, disciplinary, civil, administrative, and restorative, to be submitted to the Islamic Consultative Assembly through the government.
B- In implementing paragraph (12) of the general policies of the system in judicial affairs, the needs of the judiciary in the financial, organizational and employment fields, in accordance with principles (156), (157) and (158) of the Constitution, shall be met during the program in such a way that ten percent (10%) of the approved vacant positions existing at the end of 2009 are filled annually.
Note: The annual recruitment limit for judges is up to 800.
C- In implementing paragraphs (1), (6), (8), (11) and (12) of the system's general policies in judicial affairs, the judiciary is required to take the following measures to expedite the processing of cases:
1- Strengthening and organizing supervisory institutions, including the Supreme Court, over the performance of courts and judicial and administrative staff to increase accuracy in carrying out assigned tasks.
2- Developing a plan to specialize judicial officers in order to empower and train them professionally, by the end of the first year of the program.
Note - All agencies that are somehow subordinate to the judiciary are required to implement relevant specialized training under the supervision of the judiciary, upon request from the judiciary.
D- In order to expand legal and judicial culture, improve people's legal and judicial behavior, institutionalize a culture of law-abiding behavior, and also to prevent crimes and reduce legal lawsuits, the following measures are being taken:
1- Public education according to the program approved by the Judiciary through the Iranian Broadcasting Corporation.
2- Public education on citizenship rights regarding tax, administrative, labor and social security, environmental, banking, insurance and similar matters by relevant agencies through the Iranian Broadcasting Corporation.
3- Anticipating the necessary course materials for the aforementioned training in middle and high school courses.
Note - The Islamic Republic of Iran Broadcasting Organization is required to allocate appropriate time for broadcasting programs prepared in this regard by the judiciary, relevant agencies, or that organization in the implementation of parts (1) and (2).
4- The Judiciary is required to adopt an arrangement by the second year of the program that, while preserving the privacy of individuals, the decisions issued by the courts will be exposed to analysis and criticism by experts and specialists online.
E- In order to implement paragraph (3) of the five-year general judicial policies announced by the Supreme Leader, executive agencies, including the police force, the Basij Organization for the Oppressed, public non-governmental institutions and organizations such as the Red Crescent Society and municipalities, are required, within the framework of their duties, to cooperate with the judiciary in implementing crime prevention programs.
F- In implementing paragraph (9) of the five-year general judicial policies announced by the Supreme Leader, the judiciary is required to:
1- Take steps to establish specialized branches of criminal and civil courts and prosecutors' offices.
2- In cooperation with the government, prepare a bill for an independent domestic and international arbitration institution by the end of the third year of the program, so that it can be submitted to the Islamic Consultative Assembly through the government.
G- In order to facilitate the handling of cases and improve the quality of handling cases, the judiciary is required to do the following:
1- In cooperation with the Forensic Medicine Organization, the Attorney General's Office, and the Islamic Republic of Iran's law enforcement forces, take the necessary measures to strengthen and integrate crime scene investigation teams and prepare genetic identity cards for individuals using specialized forces and up-to-date methods and equipment.
2- Using available resources and facilities, take steps to establish provincial representative offices of the Administrative Court of Justice in the provincial judicial offices.
H- In order to increase the speed and efficiency in providing legal and judicial services, the Judiciary is required to take the following measures to develop information and communication technology:
1- Operational systems and development of the level of use of information and communication technology, especially the judicial case management system, launching the National Data Center of the Judiciary, implementing and completing the information security management system, providing electronic legal services to the public, using information technology in establishing communication between judicial authorities and other subordinate or related institutions such as the National Deeds and Property Registration Organization, the Prisons Organization, and the Forensic Medicine Organization, should be launched, expanded, and upgraded.
2- The National Real Estate and Documents Registration Organization is required to replace ownership documents in order to improve the security factor in ownership documents, reduce document-related cases in courts, digitize document information items, and implement modern registration using information technology.
Note - The Ministries of Jihad, Agriculture, Housing and Urban Development, and the Endowment and Charity Affairs Organization are required to cooperate with the National Land Registry Organization to prepare a map of the aforementioned properties based on the cadastral standard during the Fifth Plan, in addition to identifying and providing documentation of all real estate under their custody.
3- The Judiciary is authorized, based on a regulation prepared by the Minister of Justice in cooperation with the Attorney General's Office and the National Archives and Library Organization of the Islamic Republic of Iran and approved by the Head of the Judiciary, to convert documents and papers of judicial cases whose records need to be kept into electronic documents using modern information technologies and then proceed with their destruction, provided that at least thirty years have passed since their definitive archiving period. The converted information and documents will be authentic and can be relied upon in all judicial and administrative references.
The originals of important and national files that have a historical documentary aspect will be preserved and maintained by the National Archives and Library Organization of the Islamic Republic of Iran.
4- The Prisons, Security and Educational Measures Organization is required to establish electronic systems for the execution of prison sentences and security orders.
I - In order to expand the community's access to judicial justice and preserve the dignity and respect of clients and employees, the judiciary is required to do the following:
1- Renovating and developing the physical spaces of the judiciary and its affiliated organizations, including headquarters buildings, prosecutors' offices, justice departments, prisons, detention centers, correctional centers in needed areas, registration units, forensic units, offices of the General Inspectorate of the country and the Judicial Organization of the Armed Forces, while observing the necessary standards within the approved budget.
2- Facilitating the realization of the rights of needy individuals approved by support agencies in judicial cases and providing an appropriate mechanism for these individuals to use lawyers and official justice experts in necessary cases through the relevant centers, in addition to using the capacities of the occupied attorney, within the approved credit ceiling and using non-governmental organizations.
3- Improving the quality and increasing the accuracy, speed, and also the satisfaction of the people with the process of execution cases by increasing the number of document execution departments in provincial centers.
Y- The Judiciary is required to prepare its own comprehensive administrative and employment bill to be submitted to the Islamic Consultative Assembly through the government.
K- The judiciary is required to design an electronic system to reduce trial time in all judicial authorities in a way that includes at least:
1- The reason for the extension of the hearing time must be known and accessible to the parties to the dispute.
2- The time for handling cases should be automatically determined, taking into account urgent times outside of the shift.
3- The duration of case processing in criminal courts shall not exceed a maximum of three months and legal cases shall not exceed a maximum of five months.
Note - If the subject of the dispute is such that more time is needed to complete the file and issue a verdict, this will be announced to a higher authority, stating the reason, in a way that is also accessible to the parties to the dispute.
4- The heads of the districts are required to continuously monitor and determine the time for handling cases that are due for a decision but have not been decided without reason, while notifying the judge and notifying the competent authorities.
5- Supervisory authorities should automatically be informed of cases that have not been decided in accordance with the law.
L- The Administrative Court of Justice is required to increase the accuracy and speed of handling cases and reduce the time of proceedings by strengthening specialized commissions, increasing the number of consultants and experts, and holding specialized training courses for judges, while increasing interaction with the executive bodies subject to Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law and Article (5) of the Public Accounts Law of the country, and gaining knowledge of specialized issues complained about.
M- The National Real Estate and Documents Registration Organization is required to take steps to electronicize all stages of transaction registration by the end of the second year of the program, in line with the development of an integrated system for registering real estate and documents and launching a national registration data center, in a way that enables immediate and electronic responses to registration inquiries and immediate registration of transactions using secure electronic signatures.
N- The Prisons and Security and Educational Measures Organization is required to implement the following measures, in cooperation with executive agencies, public institutions and NGOs, in order to re-socialize convicts during the program:
1- With the approach of promoting deterrence and the corrective aspect of imprisonment, reforming the prison environment through measures such as classifying prisoners and detainees based on their history and type of crimes committed, separating defendants from convicted persons in temporary detention centers in cities with a population of over twenty thousand.
2- Training qualified prisoners in cooperation with the Ministry of Education.
3- Resolving the livelihood problems of the families of innocent prisoners in cooperation with the Imam Khomeini Relief Committee, the Welfare Organization, and other relevant institutions.
4- Introducing prisoners in need of employment to the relevant authorities after their release.
S - The Forensic Medicine Organization is required to conduct the necessary investigations to investigate the factors leading to injuries and unnatural deaths in referred cases and, based on the results obtained, take action on planning and training to prevent and reduce crimes, violations, and social harms resulting from them.
A- The judiciary is required to make the necessary arrangements to improve the level of utilization of the capacities of the Ministry of Justice.
F - The government shall, during the program, foresee the necessary funds to implement the tasks stipulated in this article in the form of an annual budget.
Article 212: The judiciary is required to prepare a comprehensive bill on legal representation and legal advice during the first year of the program, in accordance with the general policies of the system in judicial affairs, and submit it to the Islamic Consultative Assembly through the government.

B- Apply appropriate implementation methods such as “finance, build, operate and transfer”, “finance, build and operate”, “turnkey design and construction”, “public-private partnership” or “build, operate and own” with adequate guarantees.

Article 215- Proposing plans for acquiring new capital assets in annual budget bills is possible by observing the following:

F- Approval of new capital asset acquisition plans in each quarter by the Deputy is subject to full funding for the said plans so that the funding for the first year of implementation of the new plan does not fall below the ratio of the total funding required to the duration of the plan (in years). The funding for capital asset acquisition plans is estimated at the beginning of the plan by taking into account the forecast inflation rate during the plan period, and the projected funding for amending or changing the plan for each quarter and department until the end of the last year of the plan is only permitted to the extent of the deviation of the actual inflation rate from the forecast rate.

The aforementioned revenue stream shall be settled by the end of the same year at the latest from savings in public funds or from the amendment of the annual budget. Article 217: All ministries and government institutions are required to prepare their executive and operational plans in the form of executive program packages within six months from the date of ratification of this law. Article 218- All state-owned companies subject to Article (4) of the Civil Service Management Law, the list of which is determined by the government, as well as companies listed on the stock exchange, are required to conduct operational audits from the second year of the program and at least once until the end of the program through the Audit Organization or audit firms that are members of the Iranian Society of Certified Public Accountants, as the case may be, in order to increase the economic efficiency, efficiency and effectiveness of the companies' activities and increase the reliability of financial reports. The boards of directors of these companies are responsible for implementing this clause.
Note 1: Companies that are classified based on a government decree are exempt from the scope of this article.
Note 2: The Audit Organization is required to prepare and notify the operational audit framework and standards within six months after the approval of this law.
Article 219- In order to establish an operational budgeting system, the government is obliged to gradually provide the necessary grounds for preparing the budget in an operational manner in all executive agencies by the end of the second year of the program, so that the budget bill for the third year of the program is prepared, compiled and submitted to the Islamic Consultative Assembly in the aforementioned manner. The executive instructions shall be prepared and notified by the Deputy. Also, in the implementation of paragraph (32) of the general policies of the Fifth Program and the establishment of an operational budgeting system, the credits allocated based on the cost price referred to in Article (16) of the Civil Service Management Law shall be spent after payment to the relevant units without the obligation to comply with the general laws and regulations governing government agencies and only based on the financial, transactional, administrative and employment regulations, which include the prediction of the method of monitoring the costs and the achievement of the anticipated goals, and shall be approved by the Council of Ministers upon the proposal of the Deputy and the Ministry of Economic Affairs and Finance.
Article 220- The government is authorized to take the following measures, as appropriate, within the framework of the conditions and types of transfer contracts approved by the Economic Council regarding capital asset acquisition plans:
A- Transfer of new, semi-finished, and completed capital asset acquisition projects ready for operation in the form of contracts and conditions approved by the Economic Council, by determining the method of financing the construction period, paying operating costs or purchasing services during the contract period, in compliance with the standards for implementing the quality of services, and finally transferring the project to the non-governmental sector after the contract period.
B- Transferring unfinished and completed capital asset acquisition projects whose services can be provided by the non-governmental sector in cash and installments to the non-governmental sector.
C- Transfer of the right to exploit transferable capital asset acquisition plans, as well as movable and immovable property and financial rights in excess of the needs of the government and executive agencies.
D- Transfer of ownership or exploitation of transferable capital asset acquisition plans, as well as movable and immovable property and financial rights in excess of the needs of the government and executive agencies.
Note 1: Government revenue resulting from the implementation of the provisions of this article, after being deposited to the General Treasury and from a specific line item foreseen for this purpose in the annual budget law, as well as the credit of lines related to capital asset acquisition projects in the form of managed facilities and funds, including subsidies, interest and fees, or facilities and assistance and other financing methods approved by the Economic Council, can be allocated to capital asset acquisition projects or the conversion and improvement of movable and immovable property in the form of a reciprocal agreement with the Deputy.
Note 2: Grants subject to this article are not considered personal income and are not subject to income tax.
Article 221: In order to establish coordination to make the oversight system efficient and promote productivity, and to strengthen the country's management, a Council of Oversight Bodies shall be formed, consisting of two supervisory officials from each branch, elected by the head of the branch, while maintaining the independence of each of them within the limits stipulated in the Constitution.
The implementation guidelines for this article and the manner of holding council meetings, decision-making, and follow-up on decisions will be determined within six months of the first year of the program, upon the proposal of council members and with the agreement of the heads of forces.
Article 222- All executive agencies subject to Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law, as well as agencies subject to Articles (2), (3), (4) and (5) of the National Public Accounts Law, in relevant cases, are subject to the provisions of this law.
Article 223- In all cases where the implementation of the provisions and articles of this law and the annual budget laws or the implementation of other laws during the program requires the approval of regulations, approvals, instructions, executive packages and other regulations such as national and provincial development documents and strategic documents related to the executive branch, including authorities, officials and executive agencies, the proposal of the relevant agency, after approval by the Deputy, shall be submitted to the Cabinet of Ministers or the relevant authority in the executive branch for approval in terms of compliance with the goals of the program and the budget law.
Executive agencies are required to provide all relevant information and documents to the Deputy when necessary. Classified documents and information are subject to their own regulations. The Deputy's instructions and circulars within the framework of the provisions of this law, the Program and Budget Law, the annual budget laws, and the relevant regulations are binding on all executive agencies.

A- In Article (2) of the Law on the Addition of Articles to the Law on the Regulation of Part of Government Financial Regulations, the phrase “and provincial water and sewage and electricity distribution companies” is added after the phrase “state-owned companies”.

C- All state-owned companies and banks subject to Article (5) of the Civil Service Management Law for which special benefits are foreseen in the national budget are required to:
1- Every month, they must pay the equivalent of one-twelfth of their estimated tax for the desired year on account to the Tax Affairs Organization for the performance tax for that year, so that it will be credited to the country's general revenue account with the General Treasury.
2- Deposit at least forty percent (40%) of the projected profit each year into the general revenue account through the mechanism of subsection (1) of this paragraph.
State-owned companies whose shares are partly owned by the non-governmental sector are not subject to the payment of funds under this clause in proportion to the non-governmental sector's shares, and the non-governmental sector's share of forty percent (40%) of the aforementioned declared profit (special profit) must be paid by the relevant state-owned companies to the shareholders of the aforementioned sector.
The collection of the aforementioned amount is subject to the relevant provisions stipulated in the Direct Taxes Law approved in March 1987 and its subsequent amendments.
D- The surplus of the specific revenues of the executive agencies over the ceiling of the figure foreseen in the annual budget laws of the entire country can be spent by the same agency in the proportion stipulated in the annual budget, with the approval of the Deputy.
E - In order to facilitate the performance of affairs and accelerate the provision of services to the people and decentralize administrative and financial decision-making and avoid parallel work and accountability of managers, the powers, duties and responsibilities that are foreseen in the laws and regulations for executive bodies or their heads and highest officials can be delegated, as the case may be, to the managers of the relevant provincial and headquarters units of the aforementioned bodies based on the job descriptions of organizational positions or the appointment or delegation orders, but in no case does the responsibility of the aforementioned provincial or headquarters managers override the authority of the head or highest official of the body.

Also, in all legal cases, any non-cash assistance and transfer of capital and financial assets, including real estate, benefits and similar, including movable and immovable property, as well as the repayment of debts, claims, rights and the like, are permitted in the form of forms, conditions and instructions notified by the Deputy. A copy of these forms will be attached to the agency's expense credit agreement. The provisions of this paragraph shall be applicable to non-governmental public institutions and organizations within the limits of the credits stipulated in the law and in return or compensation for them. The conditions of this paragraph must not be contrary to the law.

In the case of other loss-making companies, action will be taken, upon the proposal of the Deputy Minister and using the legal powers of the government, in the form of transferring shares or liquidating the company and transferring the remaining assets.
Y- Executive agencies are required to observe the following when making any commitment and paying money from public funds:
1- Priority in assigning affairs, duties, management, and enterprises to the non-governmental sector over the direct expenditure of public funds.
2- Priority of the method of payment of facilities such as technical assistance, credit, and managed funds over payment methods and grants.
Note - The deputy heads and heads of executive agencies, and the officials authorized by them, and the relevant financial managers are required to observe the above priorities when signing agreements and payment orders and the like.
K- It is prohibited to create and impose any financial burden in excess of the figures stated in the annual budget laws by all executive bodies, including government agencies, in various cases such as guaranteed purchases and costs associated with purchases, compensation for losses, price differences, market regulation, input subsidies and others, fulfillment of specific obligations, basic goods, export bonuses, and the like, where public funds are used. The responsibility for implementing this ruling lies with the highest official of the executive body or authorized officials and relevant financial managers. Violation of this ruling and other cases is considered an obligation in excess of the funds and is subject to the relevant penalty.

Article 228- During the program years, individuals who have served full-time in the Islamic Revolutionary Committee and the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps from the beginning of the Islamic Revolution until the beginning of 1991 will be eligible for pension insurance in proportion to their length of service, as well as combatants who have voluntarily been present on the war fronts in proportion to their length of presence on the front.
Enjoying the benefits of the aforementioned insurance requires the payment of the employee's share of insurance premiums by the aforementioned individuals and the employer's share by the government.

Article 229- The Ministry of Oil is required to take action to ensure the safe production of oil and gas fields during the years of the plan, in sequence, with at least ten percent (10%), twenty percent (20%), fifty percent (50%), seventy-five percent (75%), and one hundred percent (100%) of the fields. For this purpose, ten percent (10%) of the value of crude oil, condensates, and gas liquids produced after depositing them into the treasury shall be allocated annually to the subsidiaries of the Ministry of Oil for the following operations:
A- Protective extraction from existing oil reservoirs by injecting gas, water, and other methods in such a way that during the Fifth Plan, the average crude oil production from the aforementioned reservoirs is at least equivalent to the average extraction in 2009.
B- Increasing natural gas production, especially by implementing the planned phases of the South Pars gas field and other joint gas fields and collecting the burning gases of the fields currently in operation, in a way that, in addition to providing sufficient gas for injection into oil fields, the country's domestic consumption and committed exports are guaranteed.

B- All executive agencies and legal entities providing public services are required to implement the information management system from the second year of the program and to implement the "AFTA" guidelines and standards from the first year of the program.

Article 232: The government is obligated to organize the transportation of goods and passengers in villages, cover the maintenance and safety programs of the rural road network, and provide for the welfare of villages with more than twenty households and nomadic settlement centers through appropriate means.
Article 233: Executive agencies may, after obtaining the approval and permission of the General Staff of the Armed Forces, form security units. Article 234: In line with the general policies of the Fifth Plan, the government is required to act in implementing the provisions of this law in such a way that the following goals are achieved by the end of the Fifth Plan:
A - Average annual growth rate of eight percent (8%) in gross domestic product.
B- Reducing the savings and investment gap by creating incentive mechanisms and incentives to convert national savings into national investment and maintaining the savings to GDP ratio at an average of forty percent (40%).
C- Cutting the dependence of current government expenditures on oil and gas revenues until the end of the program.
D- Taking necessary and necessary measures to reduce the unemployment rate to seven percent (7%) at the end of the program.
E- Reducing the gap between the top and bottom income deciles of society through adopting appropriate executive policies, as well as establishing a comprehensive social security system, expanding the tax base, and redistributing income so that the Gini coefficient reaches (0.35) at the end of the program.
F- Developing and strengthening the national standards system.
Article 235: This law is valid until the end of the year 1394 AH.
